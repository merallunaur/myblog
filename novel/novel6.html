Love Letter 情書

Dear Arthur: 親愛的亞瑟：
Your letter flew faster than a goldfinch's wings. The postman's bicycle bell was skipping lightly outside my window when I was frying a pancake. 你的信比金翅雀的翅膀飛得還要快，郵差的車鈴聲從窗子外面輕快地跳進來時我正在煎一塊鬆餅。
If I were a painter, the inkblot that spilled during my pause might have made the Seine, which I had spent hours outlining, burst its banks. If I were a writer, behind the impassioned dialogue of my hero and heroine I might have written a string of meaningless words, then crumpled it all up and thrown it into the bin. If I were a violinist, the serenade Schubert had bestowed upon me would have fallen silent under a snapped string. Fortunately, I am none of these three, so my loss was only a pancake. Yes, a pancake that should have had a flawless, smooth body, bathed in sweet blueberry jam like a maiden, its wheat-coloured skin dusted with icing sugar. In the mere minutes it took me to wipe my hands carelessly on my apron and rush out to fetch the letter, her skin was scorched by the unprovoked flame. Alas, it is most impolite to compare a maiden's soft skin to charcoal, but by the time I returned to the house with your letter, the pancake was already burnt. I could only apologise on an empty stomach to that departed maiden. I still believe you should take responsibility for this rare mistake, so perhaps you will never again taste a pancake made by my own hands, Artie. 如果我是個畫家，停頓間筆下洇出的墨漬可能會讓花了數小時勾勒的塞納河沖出河床；如果我是個作家，男女主角深情對白後面說不定會寫上一串意義不明的單詞，再全揉碎扔進垃圾桶；如果我是個小提琴手，舒伯特恩賜給我的小夜曲會在崩斷的琴弦下噤聲。不過幸好我不是這三者中的一員，要不然我損失的可就不止一塊鬆餅了。嗯，一塊本該擁有光滑無瑕身體，像個少女般沐浴在香甜藍莓醬中，讓糖霜染上小麥色肌膚的鬆餅。就在我在圍裙上胡亂擦擦手衝出門取信的短短幾分鐘之內她的肌膚就被無妄的火焰灼傷。唉，拿黑碳來形容一位少女的柔膚真是太不禮貌了，但當我拿著你的信回到屋子裡鬆餅就已經煎糊了，我只能餓著肚子向那位死去的少女道歉。我至今還覺得你應該為這次為數不多的失誤負責，所以也許你以後再也吃不到我親手做的鬆餅了，亞蒂。
When I rushed out the door, I saw that the roses by the corner of the house had bloomed again. I had not tended them for a long time since we last parted. The two rose bushes had grown wild, laughing, dancing, splashing their colours about like flamenco dancers flinging their scarlet skirts—passionate and deep. Beneath those crystalline petals shimmering in the sunlight, the translucent emerald-carved stems stretched freely. Looking through them was like seeing your eyes. So I tore off only a small petal and pressed it between the pages, rather than plucking a whole flower. When you open this letter, you will surely detect a faint, elusive fragrance, as soothing as a glass of sweet berry wine. If the wisps of cloud over the English Channel were to form a bridge, allowing me to cross the blue abyss and alight by your side, I would break off a rose branch and lay it by your pillow every morning before dawn. 我衝出門時看到屋角的玫瑰又開了，其實自從上次我們分別後我已經很久沒有打理。兩叢玫瑰瘋了似的長，嬉笑著，舞蹈著，揮灑著色彩如弗拉明戈舞娘揮舞猩紅的裙裾，熱烈又深情。在那日光下明媚的水晶花瓣底下，綠寶石雕琢的通透花莖任意舒展，透過它們就彷彿看到了你的眼睛，所以我只撕下一小片花瓣夾在紙裡，而沒有摘一朵。你在打開信時一定能聞見若有似無的淡香，那香味會讓你像喝了杯甜果子酒般舒暢。如果加來海峽上空的細碎卷雲鋪開一架橋，讓我能跨過碧藍深淵在你身旁落腳，每天晨曦未露，我就會折一枝玫瑰在你枕旁。
The mailbox is old. The black paint has peeled away, revealing a rust-red skeleton stretching wide. I forgot to repaint it. The mailboxes around have been dismantled one by one. How pretty those little villa-like mailboxes once were! Now only the ugly iron poles remain. It is like the ancient, weather-beaten branches of an old tree being smashed to pieces; the remaining twigs point upward, accusing heaven. Those iron poles cry out the same. A mailbox separated from its house is like a sentry separated from his post. Our mailbox is almost an anomaly. If some little boy on a scooter happened to pass by and saw us—two old, toothless antiques, still clinging to the habits of the last century or even the one before in an age when a single email can connect anyone—he would surely widen his curious blue eyes. 信箱舊了，黑漆剝落下鏽紅骨架張狂地展開，我也忘了刷漆。周圍人家的信箱一個接一個拆毀，在原有的那些小別墅一樣的信箱多漂亮！現在只留下醜陋的鐵桿。好比一棵古樹飽經風霜的枝幹被粉碎，枝葉指向上空是對上蒼的指責，那些鐵桿也在發出同樣的吶喊。信箱離開房屋就像陣地失去了哨兵。我們的信箱幾乎算是異類，如果周圍有個什麼小男孩踩著滑板車經過，看到我們，兩個掉牙的老古董，在一封電郵就能溝通的年代還保留著上個世紀甚至上上個世紀的習慣，一定會睜大他好奇的藍眼睛。
Artie, despite all my rambling, I received the letter only moments after the postman's wheels had retreated. I took the letter, went back inside, cleaned up the burnt pancake, untied my apron, washed my hands carefully, then poured myself a martini, dropped in an olive, and sipped it while reading your letter. 亞蒂，別看我說了這麼多，我拿到信的時間比郵差的車輪撤退的時間晚不了多少。我拿了信，回到屋子裡，清理了被烤焦鬆餅，解下圍裙，仔細洗了洗手，然後倒了一杯馬提尼，放上一顆橄欖，邊品邊看你的來信。
Well, your handwriting, as always, is written in blue-black ink—stiff and dull, so flawless that it makes me wonder whether, if you made a single mistake, you would tear the page to shreds in frustration and start over on a fresh sheet. The salutation is 'Mr. Bonnefoy' again? Couldn't you use a more intimate form of address, my dear Mr. Kirkland? I won't bother to reproach you for not saying you miss me—you never change. Then you spend two long paragraphs explaining why you wrote to me. Very interesting: you say you didn't actually want to write, it was just that you had finished your work and had nothing else to do, and your blue ink was down to the last dregs, so you wanted to use it up. That's all. Oh my, if Artie ever openly admitted that he missed me, that he wanted to see me, that he wished to become a nightingale and perch on my windowsill, God's jaw would drop. He would fall from his gem-encrusted, gold-leafed throne, rub his eyes, part the layers of clouds before him, and ask some passing little angel to help him see clearly—because he would suspect he had grown senile. 嗯，你的筆跡一如既往地用藍黑墨水寫就，死板又無趣，完美得讓我懷疑你是不是每寫錯一個字都要把原來那張紙氣急敗壞地撕得稀碎，然後換一張紙重新寫。開頭又是波諾弗瓦先生？您就不能換一個更加親密的稱呼嗎，親愛的柯克蘭先生？我就不再譴責你不說想我了，反正你不會有任何改變。接下來你花了兩大段解釋你給我寫信的原因，有意思，你說你並不想給我寫信，只是工作做完突然無事可做，再加上自己那瓶藍墨水只剩一點底子，你想把它趕緊用完，僅此而已。噯呀，如果哪天亞蒂能大大方方地承認他就是想念我，想見到我，想變成夜鶯站在我的窗台的話，上帝的下巴都會驚掉的。他會從他鑲滿寶石的鍍金箔座位上摔下來，擦擦眼睛，再把眼前重重疊疊的雲朵撥開，讓周圍的什麼小天使幫他看個清楚。因為他懷疑自己已經老糊塗了。
You told me about your recent circumstances. From your tone, I can tell you are doing well—after all, when you are sad, you don't even have the energy to be sarcastic. Take last year, for example, when the Three Cats team lost the match. Not only did you drink two bottles of beer and hold onto me while singing 'God Save the Queen' all night, you also wandered dejectedly around the streets for a whole day, and later, because France won the championship, you slapped me twice. Oh, I never want to go through that again. It was even harder to bear than a dozen servals scratching my face with their filthy claws. You say your work is passable, that no one gives you trouble and no one is particularly close to you—that is your ideal state. Indeed, it is also my ideal state, because both kinds of people would be drowned in cheap perfume by me. No, just joking, Artie. You ran into Gilbert on the street? I haven't seen him for years. I heard he is on a honeymoon trip. Did he have a girl on his arm and show you his engagement ring? I don't need to be told it was Elizabeta—yes, our Lisa. Alas, such a slender, dazzlingly beautiful girl ended up in the arms of that stinky, hard-headed rascal, while I am left with only a foul-tempered little cat for company. How unjust fate is! 你向我談起你的近況，從你說話的語氣裡能看出你過得不錯，畢竟你難過時可是連譏諷別人的氣力都沒有。比如去年三貓子隊輸了比賽，你不僅在喝了兩瓶啤酒之後抱著我唱了一晚上天佑女皇，還在大街上垂頭喪氣地晃了一天，後來又因為法國奪冠打了我兩巴掌。哦，我可不想再經歷一次，那比十幾隻藪貓拿骯髒的尖爪子抓我的臉還要讓人難以忍受。你說你工作還過得去，沒有人為難你，也沒有人與你特別親近，這是你最理想的狀態。的確，也是我最希望的狀態，畢竟這兩種人都會被我拿廉價香水溺死。不，開個玩笑而已，亞蒂。你在大街上遇到了基爾伯特？我都已經好幾年沒有見到他了，聽說他正在旅行結婚。他是不是拉著一個姑娘，向你展示了他的訂婚戒指？不用說我也知道是伊莉莎白，對，我們的麗莎。唉，這麼身材窈窕，耀眼華美的姑娘到頭來還是投入了這個混小子又臭又硬的懷抱，然而我卻只能和一隻脾氣極差的小貓咪相伴，命運真是太不公正了。
Alright, my good cat, don't bristle just yet, and don't claw a hole in this letter paper—otherwise you won't be able to read what I have to say next, even though it is quite ordinary. By rights, I should tell you how I am doing, shouldn't I? Though you have never asked in your letters. Hmm, let me think. 好了，乖貓，先別炸毛，也別用你爪子在信紙上撓一個大窟窿 ，這樣你就看不到我接下來的話了，雖然它們平平無奇。照理我應該跟你談談我怎麼樣？雖然你從未在你的信裡問起過。嗯，讓我想想。
It feels as though there is a lump of honey lodged in my throat. When it is finally about to burst out, my lips touch only colourless foam. Very fine, very trivial—like summer fireflies igniting the stars. When the light pours down before me, I cannot grasp any of it; it slips through my fingers. Alright, Artie, you may frown now and put on that patented expression of yours—half disgust, half something else. You will certainly sneer at the sentimentality that follows. 好像有很多話像塊蜜糖堵在我的喉頭，當它們終於要衝出來時我的嘴唇觸及的卻只是無色的泡沫。很細碎，很煩瑣，像夏日的流螢點燃滿天星斗，光芒傾瀉在我面前時我卻什麼也抓不到，我的指縫任憑它們白白溜去。好吧，亞蒂，你現在可以皺起眉頭，擺出你專有的兼惡表情了，你絕對會對我接下來的深情嗤之以鼻。
The other day, while tidying the house, I came across a photo album. The first picture was of the two of us—we were only teenagers then, taken on a whim during summer break. We were playing baseball. Though we had known each other since we were very young, back then whenever I climbed over the wall and tumbled onto your soft green lawn, your older brother would always pick me up and toss me out. Scott is so insensitive. Still, I am grateful for his acceptance of us now. Give him my regards. When my fingertip brushed over that photograph, do you know what I was thinking? I was thinking: Arthur Kirkland is truly the most oblivious person in this world. The way I looked at you then was like the blue-violet aurora flowing over the North Pole. Under that canopy, the dark green forest glowed with light, yet its leaves merely trembled slightly. I saw you. 我前幾天整理屋子時翻出一本影集，第一張是我們的合影，那時我們才十幾歲，是一個暑假隨手拍的，我們在打棒球。雖然我們幾歲就認識了，不過以前我翻過圍牆，跌進你家柔軟的青草地時總會被你哥哥拎起來扔出去。斯科特真是不解風情，不過我依舊感謝他現在對我們的認可，代我向他問聲好。當我的指尖撫過合照時你知道我在想什麼嗎？我在想亞瑟·柯克蘭真是這個世界上最後知後覺的人，我那時看你的眼神就像北極上空流淌著的藍紫極光，穹頂之下深綠樹林光輝降臨，但樹葉只是輕輕顫了幾下。我看見了你。
We were two parallel planets. All the astronomers in the world calculated the changing laws of our orbits, but even if they bit their pencils to pieces and tore their hair out, they could not explain the convergence that occurred when we were teenagers. After all, reason offers no answer—feeling, on the other hand, is another matter. I held out my hand to you at the ball, but you did not place yours in mine. You slapped it away, apparently thinking I was playing a prank. So infuriating. You have no idea how many pretty girls I turned down for you that night, watching them blush, come running with their little deer-like steps, and then walk away with their heads bowed. When tears rolled from their limpid eyes, I felt as if I were committing a crime. So I pulled you out of the ballroom—that may have been the most reckless thing I have ever done. I pressed you against the rough school wall, cradling your head with my hand, and kissed you uncontrollably. You barely struggled. When we parted, you slapped me and called me every name under the sun—bastard, French frog, stubble-faced stinky man—but in the end, you sighed and embraced me. 我們是兩顆平行的星球，所有的天文學家都在計算我們的軌道的變化規律，可就算他們把鉛筆咬斷，頭髮撓掉都無法解釋我們在十幾歲時出現的並行。畢竟沒有理性給出答案，感性倒另當別論。我在舞會上向你伸出手，你卻沒有把你的手放在我的手上，你狠狠地打了一下，似乎以為我在惡作劇。太讓人生氣了，你不知道那天晚上我為了你拒絕了多少漂亮姑娘的邀約，看著她們紅著臉，邁著小鹿似的步伐跑來，又低頭離開。當她們水靈的眼睛裡滾下淚滴時我簡直感覺我在犯罪。於是我把你拉出了大廳，那可能是我做過的最魯莽的一件事了。我把你按在學校粗糙的牆面上，用手護著你的頭，控制不住地親吻你，你幾乎沒有掙扎。當我們分開時你扇了我一巴掌，把混蛋、法國青蛙、有鬍茬的臭男人輪著罵，最後卻嘆了口氣，抱住了我。
I never imagined we would be together for so long. Ten years? I don't know where to begin counting. Perhaps when I carried you, feverish, to the hospital at age ten? Perhaps when you and I took a kitten trapped in a sewer to the vet at age eight? Perhaps you were already imprinted on my heart when we first met at age three. Ah, back then, when you introduced yourself, your voice was crisp, your eyes large and bright, as if a bewitching potion had been poured into a crystal ball. 我從沒想過我們會在一起這麼久，十年？我不知道以何為起點，也許是十歲時我把高燒的你背去醫院？也許是八歲時你和我一起把被下水道卡住的小貓送去醫院？也許是三歲我們第一次相見時你就已經印在我心裡了。唉，那時候你介紹自己時講話脆生生的，眼睛很大很亮，像在水晶球中注入攝人心魄的魔藥。
Now you are nowhere near as adorable as you were then. How time flies. In those days you would tug at my coat and call me 'Fran-gor-gor.' Now your terms of address have degenerated into things like 'wine bastard' and the like. It has already been two years since you moved back home. I still remember going to the dock to see you off. You were wearing a long trench coat, carrying two large suitcases, and at the cabin door you waved to me with nonchalant elegance, then repeatedly reminded me not to forget your address. As you spoke, your cheeks flushed. You looked down and kicked at the small stones that lay everywhere on the dock, sending some into the sea where they raised silvery-white splashes, while others rolled to the roadside and were kicked farther by others. When you had no more stones to kick, you finally looked up at me. Your emerald eyes were burning hot, and like a skilled artisan, you branded your shadow deep into my eyes. I opened my arms and asked with a smile: 'No goodbye kiss?' You hesitated, glanced around, then cupped my face and pressed a gentle kiss to my lips. 現在你遠不如那時候可愛，過得真快，當年你還會牽著我的衣角叫我弗朗哥哥，現在你的稱呼已經變成什麼紅酒混蛋之類的了。自從你搬回國也已經兩年了，我還記得我去碼頭送你。你穿著一件長風衣，提著兩個大箱子，在艙門口瀟灑地向我揮了揮手，然後再三叮囑我不要忘了你的地址。你說話的時候臉色緋紅，低頭有一下沒一下地踢著碼頭上隨處可見的小石子，一部分掉進了海裡，激起碎銀子般的白浪，還有一部分滾到街邊，又被別人踢得更遠。當你終於沒有石子可踢的時候你終於抬起頭來看我了，祖母綠被火焰燒得滾燙，你像個精巧的工匠般把你的影子深烙在我的眼底。我張開雙臂，笑著問你不來個臨別的吻嗎，你猶豫了一下，看看周圍，捧住我的臉輕輕在我唇上印下一個吻。
It was not a final farewell, but it was too quick. By the time I tried to return the kiss, the sea wind had already taken your coat hem and whisked you away like a feather. 雖然不是生離死別，但也太快了，等我要回吻時海風已經牽著你的衣襟把你羽毛似的捎走了。
Well, I suppose this letter is coming to an end. As per custom, I will ask: how is the weather in London? Anything troubling you? When can we meet again? And please give my regards to all your family. 嗯，信大概要結尾了。按照慣例，我會問你倫敦天氣怎麼樣？有沒有什麼煩心事？什麼時候能再見面？然後讓你代我向你所有的家人問聲好。
I will say that I miss you terribly. At the beginning of this letter, I said I am not a painter, not a writer, not a violinist. If I could put a modifier before my unremarkable name, I would want it to be: an ordinary person who loves you. 我會說我非常想念你。開頭說我不是畫家，不是作家，也不是小提琴手。如果在我的平平無奇的名字前面冠一個定語的話，我希望是愛你的一個普通人。
Yours lovingly,
Francis 愛你的，
弗朗西斯
Mr. Bonnefoy: 波諾弗瓦先生：
I received your letter long ago, but unlike you, I was not in a hurry to reply. Nor do I, as you claimed, swear and tear up the page every time I make a mistake. Though I did write several drafts, because I did not want you to have any shameful history to hold over me. 我早就收到了你的信，不過不像你，我並不著急給你回信。我也並非你所說的那樣寫錯一個字就一邊罵髒話一邊換一張紙，不過我的確寫了好幾封，因為我不想有不堪回首的歷史被你掌握。
Your envelope is the same as ever—like a rural country woman who fancies herself well-educated, with an incongruous, pretentious delicacy. Such women love to daydream, love poetic sentiment, love to wear the finest silk gowns and carelessly take new male companions to balls that anyone can pay to enter. The gold foil on your envelope looks as if it were pried from the gold teeth of such a woman's father, painstakingly glued into rows of golden leaf patterns. This time, however, to preserve the pure fragrance of the roses, you did not spray perfume as usual. Every time you spray perfume, I suspect you have smashed an entire bottle of musk onto the envelope. By the way, I pressed that petal between the pages of a thick dictionary and placed it on the top shelf of my bookcase. 你的信封和從前一樣，像個自以為受過良好教育的鄉間農婦似的，不倫不類的精緻。這類婦人愛異想天開，愛詩情畫意，愛穿著最考究的綢緞長裙，滿不在乎地挽著新男伴去參加花錢就可進入的舞會。你信封上的燙金就像從這類婦人老爹的金牙上摳下來，廢了好大心思才沾成一排金葉花紋。不過這次為了保持玫瑰花純潔的香氣，你沒有像往常一樣灑香水。每次你噴香水我都懷疑你是不是把整瓶麝香都打碎在了信封上。順便提一句，那枚花瓣被我夾在一本厚字典裡，放到了書架最上面。
You asked about the weather? Well, nothing special on the day the letter arrived. Fog is London's calling card, but that day the sun was shining, adorning the pointed roofs of Tower Bridge like giant pearls.
