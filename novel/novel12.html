The Beast of the Moon 月亮的野獸

"O foolish one who cannot understand, this is but a legend." 搞不懂的愚人啊，這只是一個傳說。
◇
What he sought was merely a pair of hands. With extreme frustration and pain. In childhood, at the very beginning of memory, before all the quests, desires, and longings for the mysterious had taken shape. Those hands pulled him from the mire, breaking free from the waterweeds tangled around his ankles and the sharp green shields of floating creatures. He lay on its owner's lap. The moon hung high; a damp fragrance filled the air. Dew slowly melted the rotting leaves caught between his fingers. His matted hair stank, fish bones wedged at the roots. His saviour, whose robe smelled of frankincense and pine needles, covered his eyes—stained with mud—and then lowered his head, whispering a low prayer in his ear. 他要尋找的僅僅是一雙手。懷著極度的沮喪和痛苦。孩提時代，記憶最初的起點，所有對於神秘的探尋、渴求、慾望都尚未成形。那雙手將他從泥沼中拉起，掙脫纏繞腳踝的水草和浮游生物鋒利的綠色圓盾。他躺在它主人的大腿上，月亮升得很高，空氣中一股濕潤的香氣，露水漸漸融化結在手指間的腐爛樹葉。打結的頭髮臭烘烘的，髮根卡著魚骨。他的拯救者，他的袍子上散發著一股乳香和松針的氣息，將他被污泥侵染的雙眼蓋住，復而低下頭，在他耳邊低低地禱告。
"Force of life, I ally with the full moon. Sacred and free." 「生命之力，我與滿月結盟，神聖而自由。」
After a long gasp, he opened his eyes. A warm palm wiped away the mud. The strength stolen by the cold slowly flowed back into his gaunt frame. A cold wind broke through the thick clouds; the light of the full moon drifted down from the tops of the tall cypress trees, part of it blocked by the other's long body. The child saw him for the first time, as if it were the earliest memory of life, the warmth of a mother's womb. From that moment, he was born again from the stars that formed his constellation. He felt the intense throbbing of that body—a pull like gravity, a longing. In that instant, he knew he belonged to him, as if the other were the creator who had arranged the elements of his body. 他在大口的喘息之後睜開眼，溫熱的掌心將污泥抹去，被寒冷奪走的力量漸漸流回他瘦骨嶙峋的身軀。冷風破開厚重的雲層，滿月的光芒飄下高大柏樹的頂端，一部分被對方修長的身體擋住。孩子第一次看見了他，彷彿是生命最初的記憶，母親子宮般的溫暖。自那一刻，從構成他星座的群星間再一次出生。他感受來自他身體的強烈悸動，一股宛若地心引力的牽引，一種渴望。他在這一刻就認定自己屬於他，彷彿對方是排列他身體元素的創造者。
His skin was as white as a sable's back, his long hair falling to his shoulders. He gazed at the child sunk in the mud, almost motionless. In the depths of his eye sockets, two pale purple beasts bared their sharp fangs. His gaze tore a wound in the child's body. His arms, bare from the wide sleeves of his grey-blue linen robe, glowed with the strength of living muscle. It was as if the diamond polish of a Greek sculptor had been set into his hands. 他的皮膚白得像雪貂的背，長髮垂肩。他凝視著陷入污泥的孩子，幾乎一動不動。在他深邃的眼眶裡，兩隻淺紫色的野獸伸出它們鋒利的獠牙。他的眼神將他的身體撕出一個傷口，環抱著他手臂從雪青麻布長袍的寬大袖口中露出，散發著生命力量的肌肉。彷彿希臘雕塑家拋光所用的鑽石膏凝在他的手上。
Legends of the moon's foster child. Brown-skinned gypsies. Omens of disaster. Betrayal of the gods. 關於月亮的養子的傳說，棕色皮膚的吉普賽人，災禍的預兆，對神的背叛。
"Are you not afraid of me?" 「你不害怕我嗎？」
The child wanted to take his hand. As if he had foreseen it, he slipped his fingers into the gaps of the child's mud-clogged fingers. 孩子希望拉住他的手，他彷彿早已預料到地將手指插入孩子積著淤泥的十指縫隙。
"Not afraid." 「不害怕。」
The child looked into his eyes. 孩子看著他的眼睛。
"I don't have a home either." 「我也沒有家了。」
He laughed softly. It was clear and powerful, like the lines of his body. As if he were an albino plant, that laughter the veins pulsing through his body. 他輕輕笑了幾聲，像他身體的線條一般，清晰而有力量。彷彿他是一株白化的植物，那陣笑聲是穿過他身體跳動著的葉脈。
"Do you want to follow me?" 「你想跟著我嗎？」
The child nodded silently. 孩子無聲地點點頭。
He tore off the soiled part of his robe and set it aside. Then tore off a clean piece. 他撕下長袍上沾著髒污的部分丟在一邊。又撕下乾淨的部分。
"To keep you from going back to tell them, I must blindfold you." 「為了防止你回去通風報信，我必須蒙上你的眼睛。」
He walked over with a smile and wrapped the cloth around the child's pale green eyes. He took his hand again. The child shrank back, unsettled by the scalding warmth of that hand. For a time afterward, he was blindfolded, letting the other lead him through thickets, streams, and the roots of tall trees piercing the brown earth. He held tightly to the other's hand; his grip never changed. Gradually his palm grew sweaty, as if his entire life were passing through his skin into the hand that held his. 他微笑著走過來，將布條繞過孩子淺綠色的眼睛。再次拉起他的手。孩子縮了縮，對那隻手滾燙的溫度感到無所適從。接下來的一段時間，他被蒙著眼，任由他帶領著，穿過花叢、溪流和高大喬木刺透棕壤的根。他一直緊緊拉著對方的手，對方的力道沒有改變。他的手心逐漸變得汗津津的，彷彿自己的全部生命正透過他的皮膚，傳遞到拉著他的那隻手上。
"This is where I live." 「這是我生活的地方。」
The child smelled rosemary. He let go of the child's hand and untied the blindfold. He stood before a stone spire built centuries ago. A messenger of the moon. Fireflies rested in his hair. His body formed a warp and weft; his long hair was a silver sea, with shimmering islands floating among it. 孩子聞到迷迭香的氣息。他放開孩子的手，解下矇住他眼睛的布條。他站在一座幾世紀前用石頭搭成的尖塔前。一位月亮的使者，有螢火蟲停在他的頭髮上，他的身體構成一幅經緯，他的長髮是銀色的海面，髮間飄著閃閃發光的島嶼。
"My name is Francis Bonnefoy." 「我叫弗朗西斯·波諾弗瓦。」
◇
The instant he pressed the white-handled blade against the stem of a daffodil, Arthur felt as if he had grown a little taller. Suppressing this strange sensation, he swung the blade and cut the daffodil, placing it in his wooden basket. In the distance came the hurried sound of hooves and loud prayers. It seemed that a plague had broken out in some village; a flatbed cart was loaded with gleaming saws and knives. The bodies of those infected would rot quickly, but they usually died in agony after a few months. If the blood was not wiped clean after bleeding, the iron tools would rust. With so many patients lately, these tools were always in short supply. Some had rusted; others had grown dull from cutting through hard bones. When those saws and knives clinked together, the sound was like wind chimes. 將白柄刀的刀刃貼上黃水仙莖的瞬間，亞瑟覺得自己好像又長高了一點兒。他按捺住這股奇妙的感覺，將刀刃一揮而下，斬斷的黃水仙放進木簍。遠處傳來急匆匆的馬蹄聲和大聲禱告。似乎某個村莊鬧了瘟疫，板車上拉著銀光閃閃的鋸子和小刀。感染了瘟疫的身體會快速潰爛，但通常會在幾個月後才在痛苦中死去。放血之後不及時將血漿擦乾淨，鐵器就會生鏽。最近的病人太多，這些東西常常不夠用。一部分生鏽了，另一部分因不斷切下硬骨頭而變得遲鈍。當那些鋸子和刀叮叮噹噹地碰在一起，發出的樂音宛若風鈴。
To prevent dizziness from squatting too long, he slowly stood and bent to look at his own ankles. The linen trousers he had cut two years ago still covered the irregular scars on his ankles. Those jagged scars came from an unsuccessful bloodletting treatment—he had jumped up and run away the moment the saw made its first cut. He never went back. Arthur lowered his head, distractedly fingering the daffodil petals, and walked toward the spire where he and Francis lived in seclusion. In the autumn of the year when Francis took his hand and brought him back, they planted an ash tree at the door. That was eight years ago. Francis had pressed a branch against the top of his head and carved his height into the trunk. He had taken Francis's hand and made him carve his own height as well. One winter he caught a chill; Francis cut off a small piece of bark and boiled it, feeding him the water. That year his cut mark had reached the height of Francis's head. The two shallow marks climbed together with the trunk, winding like vines along the growing tree. At that time, he had hoped that one day he would grow taller than Francis. Now he could only look up—between them stretched an eternal distance; the marks had grown taller than Francis already. 為了防止蹲太久所造成的眩暈，他緩緩站起身，彎腰朝自己的腳踝看去。那條兩年前裁製的亞麻褲子依舊完整地蓋住他腳踝上不規則的疤痕。那道鋸齒狀的疤痕來源於一次不成功的放血治療，他在鋸子鋸下第一刀的剎那就跳起來逃跑。再也沒有回去。亞瑟低下頭，心煩意亂地用手指理著水仙花瓣，向他與弗朗西斯隱居的尖塔走去。弗朗西斯牽著他的手將他帶回的那一年秋天，他們在門口種下一棵白蠟樹。那是八年以前，弗朗西斯用樹枝抵著他的頭頂，將他的身高刻在樹幹上。他拉過他的手，讓弗朗西斯將自己的身高也刻上去。某一年冬天他染了風寒，弗朗西斯割下一小塊樹皮，將樹皮熬出的水餵給他喝。那一年他的刀痕正長到弗朗西斯的頭頂。兩道淺淺的刀痕隨著樹幹一起，像藤蔓一般沿著愈發粗壯的樹幹攀援。當時他正期盼著有朝一日他的身高能超越他。現在他只能抬起頭，它們之間隔著永恆的距離，已經長得比弗朗西斯還要高了。
He placed the daffodils in the glass bottle on the dining table and climbed up the spiral staircase. Sunlight filtered through stained-glass windows with symmetrical circular geometric patterns, filling the air with floating dust of varying shades of blue. He climbed higher, pushed aside two tapestries. The left depicted Ptolemy, the right Copernicus. Arthur heard the clinking of glass bottles. The smoke from burning herbs made him cough softly. He reached out, trying to wave away the smoke. In the white haze that stung his eyes, he rubbed his eyes and searched for Francis, as if looking for a flying eagle in the clouds. 他把黃水仙插進餐桌上的玻璃瓶中，順著螺旋台階一路而上。陽光透過鑲嵌對稱圓形幾何的彩窗，空氣中漂浮著深淺不一的藍色灰塵。他接著向上，掀開兩扇掛毯。左邊那幅畫著托勒密，右邊畫著哥白尼。亞瑟聽見玻璃瓶互相碰撞的響動，草藥燃燒散發出的煙霧使他輕輕咳嗽了兩聲。他伸出手，試圖將眼前的煙霧揮去。在薰疼雙眼的白色屏障中，他揉著眼睛尋找弗朗西斯，彷彿在雲中尋找一隻飛翔的鷹。
"Arthur." 「亞瑟。」
Hearing his own name, Arthur knocked over a few flasks in his fluster, startled by the sudden call. He knew he had come. Arthur pouted unhappily. He always deliberately softened his footsteps, yet he still caught him every time. Francis stood with his back to him, gently wiping the worn wooden table with his sleeve. A cup of almond oil burned over an alcohol lamp, mint soaking in it. The room was filled with the strong scent of mint. A few days earlier, at dusk, Francis had found a corpse by the mountain spring. A lost villager, just dead, still warm. Francis closed his open eyes with his hand, touched his arm, and rolled up his sleeve. Red spots covered the corpse's arm; the exposed calf bore the marks of some insect bite. He had died of a tick bite. Francis carried the body on his back for hours on foot, leaving it at the edge of the forest not far from the village. The early-rising villagers would find him, bring him back to his family, and place coins over his eyelids. His soul would find rest. 聽到自己的名字後亞瑟慌裡慌張地碰倒了幾個燒瓶，被突如其來的呼喚嚇了一跳。他知道他來了。亞瑟不高興地撇了撇嘴，他每次都刻意將腳步聲放得很輕，卻仍不可避免地被他抓住。弗朗西斯背對著他，用袖子輕輕擦拭著飽經磨蝕的木桌。一茶杯的杏仁油在酒精燈上燃燒，中間浸泡著薄荷，房間裡充盈著強烈的薄荷氣味。幾天前的傍晚，弗朗西斯在山泉邊發現了一具屍體。那是一位迷路的村民，他剛剛死去，體溫仍附著其上。弗朗西斯用手闔上他睜開的雙眼，觸碰他的手臂，捲起他上衣的袖子。屍體的手臂上覆蓋著紅斑，裸露的小腿肚子上留存著某種昆蟲叮咬的傷口。他死於蜱蟲叮咬。弗朗西斯將那人的屍體背在背上，徒步幾個小時，放在離村莊不遠的森林入口。早起勞作的村民會發現他，將他帶回家人身邊，在他的眼皮上蓋上硬幣，他的靈魂將得到安息。
Francis extinguished the flame and set the mint oil on the table to cool. If people would not stubbornly refuse to use insect repellent ointment, not so many would die of malaria and fever in the hot weather. He raised his hands above his head, stretching his stiff body. He looked around with an easy expression and noticed a spider had woven a web on the telescope's glass. Apart from his unusual appearance, these were the only reasons he had been forced to live apart from society. Mint was the plant of Mercury. In his unremarkable childhood, he had one brief memory of happiness. His mother used to place mint under his tongue and at the back of his throat to help him learn to speak early. When he began living alone, he had already read the books hidden in the cracks of the wall—on plants, planets, and the body—dozens of times. That was his earliest knowledge of the world, the arm that guided him. After his mother ran away, he had lived in the cellar for months. When midnight came, he would watch the changes of all things through the air vent, connecting the movements of the planets to human fate, as if stringing a pearl necklace. Until someone found him. They dipped his white body in ink, shivering as they watched him struggle. They longed to see his fear, to hear him beg for mercy. They believed he was the child of his mother's adultery with Satan. They shaved his head, cut the skin on his feet and head, trying to find the hooves and horns they thought he had hidden. He had some idea of the pressure his mother had endured raising him, but he said nothing. He was five that year. Fortunately, they thought he was a mute imbecile and did not burn him alive. 弗朗西斯蓋滅燈焰，將薄荷油放在桌上冷卻。如果能不固執地拒絕使用驅蟲藥膏，天氣炎熱時就不會有這麼多人因瘧疾和熱病去世。他將雙手舉過頭頂，活動活動僵硬的身體。神情輕鬆地環顧四周，他注意到有蜘蛛在望遠鏡的玻璃上結了網。除了他獨特的外表，這些東西就是他被迫離群索居的唯一原因。薄荷是水星的植物。在他乏善可陳的童年中有著一段短暫的快樂記憶。母親曾把薄荷放在他的喉嚨和舌頭下，以讓他早早學會說話。當他開始獨自一人生活時，已經將藏在牆磚縫隙中的那些關於植物、行星和身體的書讀了幾十遍。那是他關於世界的最早的知識，將他引導的手臂。母親離家出走後，他在地窖裡生活了幾個月，午夜降臨時透過氣窗觀察萬物的變化，將行星的變化與人的命運相連，彷彿穿起一串珍珠項鍊。直到有人發現了他。他們將他白色的身體浸在墨汁裡，打著冷顫看他掙扎。他們渴望看見他的恐懼，渴望聽到他求饒。他們認定他是他母親與撒旦私通誕下的孩子。他們曾將他的頭髮剃光，劃開他腳底和頭上的皮膚，試圖找到他藏起的蹄子和角。他大約知道母親養育他時承受的壓力，但他一言不發。那年他五歲。萬幸，他們以為他是個不會說話的低能兒，沒有把他燒死。
"Child of blasphemy, daring to challenge the authority of Jehovah with Satan's witchcraft?" 「瀆神的孩子，膽敢用撒旦的巫術挑戰耶和華的權威？」
How can one imprison the imagination? ……怎麼能囚禁想像呢？
He was brought back to reality by a careful touch on his back, saved by the present. Arthur was staring at him wide-eyed, a smudge of mud on his small nose, like a beaver that had surfaced at the sound of hooves. Curious, firefly-like green eyes. A new soul. He smelled of daffodils. The smell of spring—thick, damp. 他因背上小心翼翼的觸碰回過神，被現實所拯救。亞瑟正瞪大眼睛望著他，小巧的鼻子上沾著污泥，彷彿聽見馬蹄聲從水底冒出的河狸。好奇的，螢火蟲般的綠眼睛。他是一個新靈魂。他的身上散發著黃水仙的香氣。春天的氣味，厚重，潮濕。
"Sorry, you must be hungry?" 「抱歉，你餓了吧？」
Francis stepped forward; the sleeve of his robe swept past the jar of verbena. Arthur let out a small cry, lunging forward instinctively, losing his balance. With a quick hand, Francis steadied the nearly-fallen bottle, his other hand catching Arthur's waist. 弗朗西斯向前跨了一步，長袍的袖子甩過裝著馬鞭草的廣口瓶。亞瑟小聲驚呼了一聲，下意識地向前撲去，失去了平衡。弗朗西斯眼疾手快地將快要倒下的瓶子扶好，另一隻手扶住亞瑟的腰。
"Careful." 「小心。」
He reached out, smiling, and wiped the dried mud from the tip of Arthur's nose, then walked downstairs. Arthur followed behind him. Beneath the skin touched by those fingers, an itch spread. His hands carried the heat of flame, and the scent of mint lingered between his fingers. His face had been very close to his just now, his eyes pressing against his eyes. From the centre of his pupils grew clusters of pale purple crystals, the colour growing lighter toward the icy surface. When he blinked, his white lashes covered that dazzling radiance, like a swan hiding a precious gem beneath its wings. The light of the waning moon shone in. Arthur walked quietly, watching the forearm visible from Francis's sleeve, the muscles twitching with the movement of his swinging hand—as if he were not a man, but someone's heart. 他伸出手，笑著颳去他鼻尖上已經風乾的泥巴，向樓下走去。亞瑟跟在他身後，被手指觸碰過的皮膚下泛起一陣癢。他的手帶著火焰的溫度，指間散發著薄荷的香氣。他的臉方才離他很近，眼睛壓著他的眼睛。從他的瞳孔中央結出淺紫色的水晶晶簇，越靠近冰塊質地的表面顏色越淺。他眨眼時白色睫毛覆蓋住那陣刺眼的光輝，彷彿天鵝將珍貴的寶石藏在翅膀下。下弦月的光照了進來，亞瑟靜靜地走著，看著他從袖口露出的小臂，他的肌肉隨著他走路時手的擺動而跳動，彷彿他不是一個人，而是某個人的心臟。
He trotted a few steps, wanting to take his hand as he had as a child. A long memory suddenly struck him; he realised with a start that too many years had passed since that age. 他小跑了幾步，想如兒時一樣拉住他的手。漫長的回憶突然擊中了他，他驚覺到自己跟那個年紀，已錯過了太久。
◇
On a moonless night, Arthur knelt before the bronze astrolabe on the terrace, rolling small stones back and forth with a clatter. Francis sat beside him, recording the positions and movements of the planets on paper by the light of the celestial bodies. Hours earlier, the sun had left worm-like tan lines on his skin. His eyes would not stop tearing in the sunlight—sharp orange fingernails, the sun digging out his eyes. 沒有月亮的晚上，亞瑟跪坐在天台的銅製星盤前，將小石子叮叮噹噹地滾來滾去。弗朗西斯在他身邊，藉著天體的光輝，在紙上記錄著行星的位置與移動軌跡。幾個小時前，太陽在他的皮膚上留下長蟲般的曬痕。他的眼睛一見日光就不斷流淚，鋒利的橙色指甲，那是太陽在挖他的眼睛。
"How coincidental." 「真是巧。」
He shook his head at him, helpless. 他無奈地對他搖搖頭。
"At the moment of my birth, just like today, the sun blazed high, and even at night the moon could not be seen." 「在我出生的那一刻，就如今天一般，驕陽高懸，夜晚也看不見月亮。」
Driven by youthful curiosity, he pressed Francis further: had his fate been written among those churning planets from the moment of his birth? Francis moved the small stones quietly across the astrolabe with his long, knuckly fingers. Arthur suddenly felt that the fate of the whole world lay open like a book before Francis, that the world was born from his chest. When he pinched a stone and moved it across the astrolabe, it was as if he were holding the flowing threads of the Fates' lives—and thus, his own destiny changed. 來自年輕的好奇心使然，他追問弗朗西斯是否從出生開始，命運就寫在了那些翻湧著的行星當中。弗朗西斯用骨節分明的手指夾著小石子，在銅盤上靜靜移動。亞瑟突然覺得整個世界的命運像一本書般攤開在弗朗西斯的面前，世界從他的胸中誕生。當他用手夾住那顆小石子，在星盤上移動的時候，彷彿握住命運女神流動著的生命絲線，他的命運因此改變。
"The hydrogen in cinnamon comes from the sun; the carbon in lilies comes from the moon. Countless elements evaporate, volatilise, disappear from the planets, fall to the earth, and are absorbed by plants. Plants enter our bodies; the elements are broken down, forming the iron in our hair, the carbon in our teeth. In a sense, we are children of the universe." 「肉桂裡的氫元素來自太陽，百合裡的碳元素來自月亮。無數種元素從行星中蒸發、揮發、消失，落在大地上，被植物所吸收。植物進入我們的身體，元素被分解，構成我們頭髮中的鐵，我們牙齒中的碳。在某種說法中，我們是宇宙的孩子。」
He sat on the edge of the terrace, his pale body exposed to the moonlight, his hair fluttering in the wind. 他坐在天台邊緣，蒼白的身體暴露在月光下，髮絲在風中飄揚。
"But you cannot entrust yourself entirely to fate and the gods. Once your faith in them surpasses your own will, you will be controlled by fate. Even if you achieve results through your own efforts, you will give all the credit to fate." 「但是，你不能將自己完全託付給命運和神明。一旦對他們的信仰超過你自己的意志，就會被命運所控制。哪怕因自己的努力獲得了結果，也將功勞全部獻給命運。」
Arthur turned his face and found that Francis had turned his too, gazing at him. He trembled, caught by those eyes. As if bitten on the throat by some unknown beast of the dark side of the moon. 亞瑟轉過臉，發現弗朗西斯也轉過臉來凝視著他，他顫慄著，被那雙眼睛捕獲。彷彿被月之暗面不為人知的野獸咬住喉管。
"But..." 「但是……」
Arthur gathered his courage and cleared his throat. 亞瑟鼓起勇氣清了清嗓子。
"Why can't I say that meeting you was fate?" 「我遇見你，為什麼不能說是命運？」
Francis smiled silently, placed his hand on Arthur's head, and gently tousled his hair. Arthur turned his nose, seeking the pungent smell of smoke buried beneath the skin of that hand. The scent of the house's incense. Silver-white, symbolising the purity of snow. When Francis came to him, that ice-sculpted body made him feel the violence of the moon. He turned his face to see his eyes more clearly, but a hand covered his eyes. Francis looked into the distance. An ant crawled over his forearm; he lowered his lashes and pinched it dead between two fingers. His hand still covered Arthur's eyes. Under those newborn green eyes, everything happened in silence. 弗朗西斯無聲地笑了笑，將手放在他的頭上，輕輕地揉著他的頭髮。亞瑟偏過鼻子，找尋埋藏在他手背皮膚下的那股嗆人的煙味。房屋熏香的味道。銀白象徵著雪的純潔性，弗朗西斯來到他身邊，那冰雕一般的身體卻讓他感受到了月亮的暴力。他轉過臉想更清楚地看著他的眼睛，一隻手蓋在他的眼睛上。弗朗西斯看向遠方，一隻螞蟻爬過他的小臂，他垂下睫毛，用兩根手指將牠捏死。他的手仍蓋著亞瑟的眼睛，在那雙新生的綠眼睛下，一切的發生都悄無聲息。
That night, as gentle and cheerful as ever, Francis sang a hymn in a melancholy voice, while from the direction of the village came the faint, deep sound of a funeral bell. 那天夜裡他們和過去一樣溫和而且歡快。弗朗西斯用一種感傷的聲音唱起了一首祝歌，從村子方向則傳來微弱、低沉的喪鐘聲。
Moon, you wish to be a mother. But you cannot find the love that would make you mortal. 月亮啊，你想做母親。但你卻找不到，讓你變成凡人的愛情。
◇
Arthur pricked up his ears. The screams were drawing closer. The moon was being eaten by the night; a thin yellow hook hung in the sky. No starlight. The darkness was filled with a faint scent of osmanthus. The cries were like a thick, shapeless jelly, squeezing through the air. Perhaps it was only the wind. Arthur turned over. Dried cyclamens hung at his bedside, placed there when he came of age. Before that, they had mostly been freesias, jasmine, and other uniquely fragrant flowers. Light footsteps sounded from the ceiling above. He knew Francis had been working late again. Months earlier, he had told Arthur that he was going to brew a medicine to stop the plague rampaging through the villages. The plague had lasted nearly half a year. A few days ago, Arthur had gone to the general store to buy a goose-necked flask he had broken. Francis made him put on a cloak and smeared mint balm on his cheeks. His body temperature seemed higher than Arthur's; the balm began to melt as soon as his fingers touched it. Under the touch of those fingers, he once again felt a restless agitation. 亞瑟豎起耳朵，尖叫聲越來越近了。月亮被黑夜啃食，空中鑲嵌著細細的一道黃鉤。沒有星光，黑暗中充滿著一股若有似無的桂花氣味。叫聲像一股沒有形狀的厚重膠質，在空中擠壓。也許那只是風聲，亞瑟翻了個身，風乾的仙客來掛在他的床頭，那是在他成年時掛上去的。以前大多是小蒼蘭、茉莉一類的帶有獨特香氣的花朵。頭頂的天花板傳來輕輕的腳步聲，他知道弗朗西斯又工作到半夜。幾個月前，他告訴他他要熬製一種藥，以解決村中四處橫行的瘟疫。瘟疫已經持續了大半年，前幾天亞瑟去雜貨商那買他打碎的一隻鵝頸瓶。弗朗西斯讓他穿上斗篷，將薄荷膏抹在他的臉頰上。他的體溫似乎比他高，膏體一觸到手指就開始融化。在他手指的觸碰下，他再次感到一股不安的躁動。
How could he confess all this to him? How could he speak of love? How could he steal the moon? Even after living together for years, he still felt that one day Francis would throw his life away—the life he had given to him the day he was pulled from the swamp, which had once been his most precious thing. In the village where he went to buy the goose-necked flask, rats and insects ran rampant, sewage flowed everywhere. In the distant alleys, young men in white robes knelt on the ground praying. From the butcher shop next to the church came the screams of an amputee. A mother held her newborn child at the butcher shop's door, the child's eyes yellow and swollen, its belly distended. A young woman with grey eyes distributed lily-brewed medicine to an elderly neighbour, only to be burned as a heretic. Between the air, swollen with heat, and the dust rising upward, life was so precious. 如何向他袒露這一切？如何向他訴說愛意？如何將月亮偷走？即使他們共住多年，他仍覺得有一天弗朗西斯會把他的生命丟掉，那是他自被從沼澤拉起的那一天就獻給他的，曾是他最重要的東西。在購買鵝頸瓶的村莊，蟲鼠霸道，污水橫流。遠處的巷弄裡，身著白袍的男青年伏在地上禱告。教堂邊上的肉舖裡傳來被截肢者的慘叫。有母親抱著剛出生的孩子在肉舖門口排著隊，孩子睜著黃黃的雙眼，肚子漲得很大。有一雙灰眼睛的年輕女人將百合花熬製的湯藥分給鄰居家的老人，卻被當成異類燒死。在因高溫而膨脹的空氣和向上飄散的灰塵之間，生命如此可貴。
"Why do you want to help them?" 「你為什麼要幫他們？」
He had asked, confused, when he heard Francis's plan. 當他聽到弗朗西斯的計劃時不解地詢問。
"Because I love them. I love everyone in this world." 「因為我愛他們，我愛世上的所有人。」
Francis's gaze blazed; he raised a smile. His warm hand rested again on Arthur's head, stroking from the crown outward. It seemed to Arthur that he treated him as if stroking the antlers of a fawn. 弗朗西斯目光如炬，牽起一個微笑。他溫暖的手又放在他的頭上，自發旋向四周撫愛。他覺得他對待他的樣子，似乎在撫摸小鹿的角。
"I love everyone in this world." 「我愛這世上的所有人。」
"I can help you. If it is your wish." 「我可以幫你，只要是你的願望。」
Arthur looked up at him, wrapping his arms around his waist, pleading with him as he had as a child. If any change were to shatter his little world, he would pull the thread that kept the balance. 亞瑟抬眼看著他，雙手環抱著他的腰，像孩童時代一般對著他耍賴。如果任何變化將讓他的小小世界崩塌，他一定會拉緊那根維持平衡的線。
"You needn't worry." 「你不用擔心。」
Francis gently pushed his hands away. Arthur keenly sensed his hesitation. Was it rejection or protection? In his heart, there had always been two Francises—one who had saved him from the swamp, and another who pushed him away. The anger and gentleness of the moon, woven from his soul, formed a rope wound around his wrist at one end and around Arthur's neck at the other. 弗朗西斯輕輕推開他的手。亞瑟敏銳地察覺到他的猶豫。他所展現的是拒絕還是保護？在他的心裡一直有兩個弗朗西斯，一個將他從沼澤中救起，另一個又將他推開。來自他靈魂的，月亮的憤怒與柔和編成一根繩索，一頭繞在他的手腕，另一頭繞在亞瑟的脖子。
"Sleep." 「睡吧。」
Francis tucked in the corner of his quilt and added more flowers to his bedside. Then he turned and left, feeling Arthur's gaze fall gently on his back. He did not look back. He climbed upward, into the studio where the crucibles and distillation apparatus stood. In the thick notebook on the table, he kept the notes he had memorised from the books he had read in the cellar about plants, planets, and the body. Part of it was new discoveries he had made while living alone. Elderberries were haunted by ghosts and lightning; sunflowers brought the moon's blessing; cyclamens kept evil away from loved ones. Wearing gloves, he plucked various herbs, crushed them, and sprinkled them into the boiling spring water, as if they were magic flowing from his fingertips. When the steam carried the scent of the plants, he poured out the waste and placed the paper inscribed with prayers into the herbal water, letting it soak. He chose a method they could accept—a gentle way, the forgiveness of God. Beyond the window, dark clouds with glowing edges bred thunder and lightning. Autumn rain was rarely so fierce. He put out the light and rested his head on the table. A ghostly face reflected in the glass bottles. He rose and covered the glass instruments with red cloth. 弗朗西斯將他的被角掖好，又在他床頭添了些花朵。接著，他轉身離開，感到亞瑟的目光輕柔地落在他的背上，他沒有回頭，向上，走入擺放著坩堝和蒸餾裝置的工作室。桌上的厚厚筆記本裡，保存著他憑記憶默下的，在地窖裡讀過的那些關於植物、行星和身體的筆記。一部分是他獨自生活後新發現的。接骨木棲息著鬼魂和雷電，向日葵帶來月亮的祝福，仙客來讓所愛之人遠離邪惡。他戴著手套，將各種不同的草藥摘下，碾碎，撒入沸騰著的泉水，彷彿它們是他指尖流出的魔力。直到水蒸氣散發出植物的氣味，他將廢渣倒出，放入抄著禱文的牛皮紙，讓藥水浸潤紙張。他選擇能讓他們接受的方式，一種溫和的方式，神的寬恕。窗外邊緣發亮的暗雲孕育著雷電，秋雨很少如此凶暴。他熄了燈，趴在桌子上小憩。玻璃瓶倒映出他幽靈般的面容。他起身，將紅布蓋在玻璃器具上。
"I will save everyone." 「我會救下所有人。」
◇
Before Francis set out for the church, he dyed his hair yellow with water boiled from gardenia fruit. On the night of the new moon, he ascended the terrace as usual and made his wishes before the bronze astrolabe and the moonlight. Candles and verbena burned on the stone railing. Francis wrote incomprehensible characters on a piece of paper and slipped it into Arthur's pocket, telling him to keep it on him at all times. He told Arthur he would be gone for a few days, so that everyone, regardless of wealth or poverty, could receive treatment. 弗朗西斯動身前往教會前，用水梔子果熬的水染黃了頭髮。新月的夜晚，他照例登上天台，在銅星盤和月光前許願。天台的石圍欄上燃燒著蠟燭和馬鞭草，弗朗西斯在紙上寫著看不懂的文字，將他放進亞瑟的口袋，囑咐他隨身帶好。他告訴他他將離開幾天，以每一個人，不分貧賤，都能接受治療。
"Have you ever seen your own fate?" 「你曾看過自己的命運嗎？」
Arthur rolled the small stones back and forth, then suddenly asked. Francis was briefly startled, then shook his head. 亞瑟將小石子滾來滾去，突然發問。弗朗西斯短暫地愣神，接著搖搖頭。
"Then would you like to see mine?" 「那你想看看我的命運嗎？」
Arthur sat down beside him and laid his head on his thigh. 亞瑟在他身邊坐下，將頭枕在他的大腿上。
"Do you really want to know?" 「你真的想知道嗎？」
He could feel the other's hand running through his hair again. He rolled his eyes. 他能感到對方的手又在捋著他的頭髮。他轉了轉眼睛。
"Better not. I'm afraid my fate might say that one day I will be separated from you." 「還是不要了。我怕我的命運說我某一天會跟你分開。」
Francis laughed and tapped his forehead with his knuckles. His gaze passed over the dense forest, toward the invisible village in the distance. The shadow of a cloud fell on his pale face, like a monster from another dimension beheading him. Arthur grew drowsy on his knee. In a daze, he felt as if he had returned to the swamp eight years ago, walking into the mud, walking into those newborn green eyes, like a bird falling into flowing water. 弗朗西斯笑著用指關節敲敲他的額頭。他的眼神越過茂密的樹林，投向遠處他看不見的村莊。雲投下的陰影落在他蒼白的臉上，彷彿來自另一個維度的怪物將他斬首。亞瑟在他的膝蓋上昏昏欲睡，恍惚間，他覺得自己回到八年前的那片沼澤地，他走進淤泥，走進他那雙新生兒般純淨的綠眼睛，像鳥兒陷進流水裡。
◇
"Stick the prayer paper on the wound, and misfortune and plague will not come." 「將禱文貼在傷口上，厄運和瘟疫就不會找來。」
Francis, wearing gloves, handed the thick parchment to the bishop with the purple sash. He wore a white shoulder cape, claiming to be a priest who had come all the way from the Vatican. He had never owned a white garment. The shoulder cape clung to him like a patch of mould. The priest took the prayer paper, examined the forged documents, and asked him the source of the prayer. He invented the name of some ancient book, and the man believed him. There was a quality in his voice, in every subtle expression of his face, that made people lose themselves in it. A sense of detachment not of this world, like a storm happening on another planet. The priest arranged for him to rest in a room in the tower. He took off the shoulder cape, tilted his head back, and dipped his hair in a porcelain basin of hot water. The yellow gardenia pigment on his silver strands spread through the water in ripples. A few minutes later, he rose and dried his hair. The setting sun shone through the tall pale green windows. He saw a long queue forming before the church. The descent of God made the people ecstatic. Unknowingly, he fell asleep. He dreamed of Arthur sitting on the terrace. When he drew near, Arthur turned his face. Sunlight illuminated his face, shining in his eyes. But Francis—he walked toward him with the blackness of night, still draped in tattered, terrifying moonlight. His face was grey, his hair grey, as if he had just risen from the grey earth of a coffin. 弗朗西斯戴著手套，將手裡厚厚的牛皮紙交給繫著紫色腰帶的主教。他穿著一件白色的肩衣，謊稱自己自梵蒂岡遠道而來的教士。他從未有過一件白色衣服。肩衣黏在他身上，像一塊長滿菌絲的苔蘚。牧師接過禱文，查看他偽造的文書，詢問他禱文的來源。他隨口編了某本古書的名字，對方信以為真。他的嗓音，他所有細微的表情當中蘊含著一種特質，使人沉溺其中。一種不屬於這個世界的間離感，彷彿發生在另一個星球上的風暴。神父安排他在塔樓的一間臥房裡休息片刻。他脫下肩衣，向後仰著頭，將頭髮浸在裝滿熱水的瓷盆裡，沾在銀絲上的梔子黃素順著水的波紋在盆中擴散。幾分鐘後，他起身擦乾頭髮，夕陽透過淺綠色的高大窗戶，他看見教堂門前排起了長隊。神的降臨讓人事欣喜若狂。不知不覺中他進入夢鄉。他夢見亞瑟坐在天台上，他走近的時候亞瑟轉過臉來，陽光照亮了他的面孔，在他的眼裡閃耀。但他，他帶著夜的漆黑朝他走去，肩上還披掛著殘破的恐怖月光。他的臉是灰色的，他的頭髮是灰色的，好像剛從棺材的灰土中起身。
When he woke, it was already evening. After the night had gradually risen, Artemis drew her waxing moon bow and shot its light toward the earth, still warm from the day's heat. After the last person had pressed the parchment to his body, Francis put tobacco into his pipe and took a deep drag. Then he hurled the pipe violently at the pale green stained-glass window. The fragile glass shattered with a loud noise, as if someone's eyeball had been broken. No one could now notice anything happening around them. Francis stood at the window; the wind blowing through the gap stung his face. He could barely feel it. 等他醒來時已經是傍晚。漸漸升起的夜幕之後，阿耳忒彌斯張開她上弦月的弓，將光芒射向殘留著地面餘溫的大地。直到最後一個人將牛皮紙貼在身上，弗朗西斯將菸草放進煙壺，深深吸了一口。接著他將煙壺狠狠扔向那扇淺綠彩窗，脆弱的玻璃應聲而碎，發出巨大的聲響，彷彿打碎某人的眼珠。已經沒有人可以注意身旁發生的任何事。弗朗西斯站在窗前，缺口灌進來的風刺痛他的臉，他幾乎感覺不到。
"I will save everyone." 「我會救下所有人。」
He had added a large dose of mandrake to the medicinal broth. Through the blood, the hallucinations began to take effect. In the steaming evening breeze, Francis opened his arms, closed his eyes, and broke into a smile so gentle it was almost cruel. An invisible force separated the people, broke apart families, and muddled their minds. Mandrake could make one see their deepest fears. After he had accidentally ingested it, he saw Arthur die before his eyes—a pitchfork driven into his chest. 他在藥湯裡添加了大劑量的曼陀羅，穿過血液，幻覺開始生效。在熱氣騰騰的晚風中，弗朗西斯張開手臂，閉上眼睛，綻放出一個溫柔得近乎殘忍的微笑。一種看不見的力量分離了人們，拆散了家庭，攪混了他們的頭腦。曼陀羅能讓人看見最深的恐懼，在他偶然吸食後，他看到亞瑟在他眼前死去。他的胸前插著乾草叉。
How could one confirm the truth of fate? If the future was truly determined by God, why did the peasants still worry about their faith, their churches, their priests? Some began to scream in panic, swinging axes at their own wives. A child watched all this, seeming to keep his reason, saying nothing. His presence reminded Francis of himself—only he had been even weaker back then. Madness seized the village. It was too late to resist. Some had already fallen into insanity. They rushed into anyone's house and took whatever meat and bread had not been hidden. Some relatively lucid residents tried to defend themselves with sickles, but were cut down by axes. More sounds of flesh being chopped filled the air. A woman with a head injury, singing in a daze, was blinded by her own blood. Children fled in all directions, tripping over fences or falling into ditches. Horses, startled, broke free from their enclosures and trampled a fallen child. The holy statues were smashed to pieces; countless people hung from the village's freely growing trees. A man rushed out of his house; his arm was gone. By midnight, a strong wind shook the trees. The hanging bodies trembled, swinging in ever-widening circles. The watching villagers crossed themselves. Francis looked around, searching for a trace of Satan. He felt its breath in the air. It played among the oak branches, gently stirring the hanging figures while wrapping their bodies in spiderweb-like threads drawn from its own translucent body. He panted, feeling its approach, its delight. 如何確認命運的真實？假如真的是由上帝決定將來發生的事，那為什麼農民們還為他們的信仰、教堂和神父擔心呢？有人開始恐慌地大叫，將斧子砍向自己的妻子。一個孩子目睹著這一切，他似乎保持著理智，一言不發。他的存在讓弗朗西斯想到了自己，只不過他當年更加弱小。狂亂攫住了村子。要抵抗已為時太晚。有些人已經陷入瘋狂。他們隨便衝進任何誰的屋子，拿走那些沒有藏起來的肉和麵包。一些相對清醒的居民試圖用鐮刀保護自己，卻被斧子砍倒。更多的碎肉聲響了起來，一個頭部受傷的女人精神失常地唱著歌，被自己的血糊住了眼睛。孩子們瘋狂地東奔西逃，不是被籬笆絆倒，就是跌進溝裡。馬匹受了驚，掙脫圍欄，從一個跌倒孩子的身上踩了過去。神像被砸得稀碎，村中自由生長的樹上吊起無數的人。一個男人衝出屋子，他的手臂不見了。進入午夜，一陣疾風搖撼了樹木。那些吊著的人體顫抖著繞起越來越大的圈子來。旁觀的村民們在胸口劃了劃十字。弗朗西斯向四周張望，尋找撒旦的蹤影，他在空氣中感到了牠的呼吸。牠在橡樹枝間遊戲，一邊輕輕地拂動那些吊著的人，一邊用從牠那半透明的身體抽出的蜘蛛網一樣的細絲纏繞住他們的身體。他喘著氣，感受牠的接近，牠的欣喜。
"It is time. Take me to hell." 「是時候了，帶我下地獄吧。」
Francis heard hurried footsteps behind him. The door behind him was kicked open. The half-naked bishop rushed in with several strong peasants and butchers. They bound his hands behind his back. He thought of cyclamens and the scent of spring, and said nothing. 弗朗西斯聽到身後傳來急促的腳步聲。身後的門被踹開，衣不蔽體的主教帶著幾個強壯的農民和屠夫衝了上來。將他的雙手反綁在身後，他想念著仙客來和春天的香氣，一句話也沒有說。
Silver moon, what will you do with him? This white-skinned child, the child of the moon. 銀色的月亮，你要拿他怎麼辦。這個白色皮膚的孩子，月亮之子。
◇
When Arthur saw Francis again, he was hanging from a tree, his wrists bound, his feet dangling, stones tied to his ankles. The hemp rope was soaked with blood and holy water. A gash had been cut in the middle of his forehead, from which blood gushed. Two iron nails were driven into his sharp pale purple eyes. His pale skin was covered with sunburns. Late at night, with no one watching, Arthur trembled as he approached, feeding him water and broth, smearing ointment on his cracked lips. 當亞瑟再次見到弗朗西斯時，他手腕綁著被吊在樹上，雙腳懸空，腳踝上繫著兩塊石頭。麻繩上浸著血漬和聖水，額頭中間被劃開，鮮血汩汩流出。他那雙銳利的淺紫色眼珠裡插著兩根鐵釘。蒼白的皮膚上布滿曬痕。深夜時無人看守，亞瑟顫抖著靠近他，給他餵水和湯，將油膏抹在他開裂的嘴唇上。
"Arthur, I know it's you." 「亞瑟，我知道是你。」
He suddenly called out, his bound hands moving slightly. Arthur stood on tiptoe, but his head still could not reach his fingers. After several attempts, tears streamed down his face. 他突然叫住他，被綁住的手動了動。亞瑟踮起腳，頭頂依舊無法觸碰到他的手指。幾番嘗試後他的眼淚流了出來。
"Why?" 「為什麼？」
He stood on tiptoe, his lips against his ear. 他踮起腳，嘴唇貼著他的耳朵。
"Why didn't you run away with me?" 「為什麼，不跟我一起逃走？」
Francis did not speak. He lifted his head. Arthur distinctly felt the burning gaze from those bloody eye sockets. He threw himself on him, sobbing. 弗朗西斯沒有說話，他仰起頭。亞瑟分明感覺到了那鮮血淋漓的眼眶裡射出的灼熱目光。他撲在他身上哽咽著。
Why do you love everyone, but cannot love me alone? 為什麼你愛所有人，卻單單不能愛我？
"The full moon is so beautiful." 「滿月真美啊。」
Francis lowered his head and kissed his forehead again and again. Blood dripped into Arthur's eyes. 弗朗西斯低下頭，在他的額頭上吻了又吻。血滴進亞瑟的眼睛。
"Take it. Take my eyes. I don't want to see anything anymore." 「拿走吧，把我的眼睛拿走吧，我已經不想看見任何東西了。」
The next day, they tied him to a stake. The fire burned for two days and two nights, until his pale body was completely reduced to ash. After Francis left, Arthur pored over the words of Hebrew and Greek, deciphering the prayer he had left him before he went away. Years later, he succeeded. 翌日，他們將他綁在火刑柱上，大火燒了兩天兩夜，直到他蒼白的身體完全化為灰燼。弗朗西斯離開後，他查閱著希伯來與希臘的文字，破譯他走之前留給他的禱文。幾年後，他成功了。
"Look for me in the stars, look for me in the earth, look for me among the countless bodies." 「在群星中尋找我，在泥土中尋找我，在無數的屍體中尋找我。」
◇
Now he knew his fate. In the years that followed, he slept on beds of cyclamen, waiting for insects to burrow into his ear canal. In that rich, spring-like fragrance, he felt those hands again on his head—and he caught them, and together with him, toward the crack opening in the earth, together they fell. 現在知曉了他的命運。後來的無數年間，他睡在仙客來的花叢上，等待蟲子鑽進自己的耳道。在那陣濃郁如春的香氣中，他感到那雙手又放在他的頭上，而他抓住了它，和他一起，朝著大地裂開的縫隙，一起墜落。
