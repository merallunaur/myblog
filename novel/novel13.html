The Jordan River and the Golden Age 約旦河與黃金時代

◇
The sun had not yet risen. Sea and sky merged into one. The fierce sea wind seized the waves and shattered them against the barnacle-covered rocks, producing a sighing sound. The ocean breathed silently. In the embrace of Hope Bay, a brand-new fishing boat cut through the waves as lightly as a seagull. Dark-skinned fishermen took turns turning the winch, lowering the tens-of-metres-long trawl net into the deep water below. Sweat dripped between the men's hard bodies and their cold shirts. The silver harpoons, stacked together when at rest, bloomed like an anemone. The boat sailed fearlessly across the pitch-black sea. The men tucked damp cigarettes behind their ears, lit their lamps, and sat around the deck playing cards. Their carefree swearing and hearty laughter were like incantations. The dark shadow on the horizon gradually grew brighter, as if an invisible lady were holding up a bright lantern from beneath the sea. Golden foam floated on the water's surface. The sky gradually cleared, and seagulls darted among the clouds. The fishermen finished their card game, brought out a bottle of spirits, and passed it around, each taking two or three sips. Suddenly, one of the fishermen squinted, pointed into the distance, and shouted. The others followed the direction of his finger. In the morning mist that had not yet dissipated, a dinghy drifted toward them, carried by the current, drawing closer and closer. They gradually made out a ragged boy sitting in the dinghy. He could not have been more than nine years old. His bare arms were covered in bloodstains, and sand clung to his golden hair. In his hand, he clutched a jellyfish that bore signs of being gnawed. His violet eyes flickered restlessly, like a snake cut in two. 太陽尚未升起，海與天融為一體。激烈的海風捲挾住波浪，在長滿藤壺的礁石上震碎，發出嘆息般的聲響。海洋悄無聲息地呼吸。希望灣的懷抱中，一艘嶄新的捕魚船輕如海鷗在斬浪。皮膚黝黑的漁夫們輪流轉著輪軸，將幾十米長的拖網放進幽深的水下。汗水在男人們堅硬的身體和冰冷的上衣間滴淌。那些閃著銀光的魚叉，休息時壘在一起，像一朵海葵般綻放。漁船無畏地在漆黑的海面上行駛。男人們將受潮的香煙夾在耳朵上，點起燈，圍坐在甲板上打牌。他們隨心所欲的粗口和豪爽的大笑，彷彿咒語。海平線上那道幽暗的陰影逐漸變得明亮起來，就像有一位看不見的女士在海底用手托起明燈，海面上漂起金色的浮沫。天空漸轉清澈，海鷗在雲朵間穿梭。漁民們結束了牌局，拿出一瓶酒，按順序一人兩三口。突然，其中一個漁民瞇起眼睛，指著遠方大喊。其他人順著他手指的方向望去。尚未散去的晨霧中，一艘皮艇被洋流推動，朝著他們漂來，越來越近。他們逐漸看清了，皮艇裡坐著一個衣衫襤褸的少年。他不會超過九歲，裸露的手臂上佈滿血漬，金髮中黏著沙子。他的手裡抓著一只有啃食痕跡的水母。一雙紫色眼睛不安地跳動，彷彿一條被砍成兩半的蛇。
Out of instinctive kindness, the fishermen steered closer. Their leader, the captain, was a bearded bachelor in his fifties. Smiling kindly, he stepped over the side of the boat and into the dinghy. He soothed the child, whose clear eyes gradually stopped trembling under the captain's deep voice. He lifted the child into his arms, raised him up, and, after making sure his companions had hold of him, climbed back onto the fishing boat. They hauled in the net first. The child, still clutching the mutilated jellyfish, stood rooted to the spot, staring at the sardines, ribbonfish, and swimming crabs thrashing in the old net. A younger sailor patted his shoulder and tried to ask about his background. The child shook his head listlessly, saying only that his parents had been sucked into a whirlpool and had died, but they had managed to save him. 出於本能的善意，漁民們驅船駛近。他們之間領頭的船長是個留著大鬍子的單身漢，五十歲上下。他一邊和善地微笑，一邊跨出船舷，踏進那隻皮艇。他安撫著孩子，孩子那雙澄澈的眼睛在他低沉的嗓音中逐漸停止顫抖。他將孩子抱在懷裡，托起來，確認同伴接住他之後，爬上漁船。他們先收了網。孩子攥著那隻殘缺不全的水母，呆立在一旁，盯著舊漁網裡翻騰著的沙丁魚、帶魚和梭子蟹。一個年輕一些的水手拍拍他的肩膀，試著詢問他的身世。孩子沒有精神地搖了搖頭，只說自己的父母被捲進漩渦死去，他們拼命救下了他。
"Do you remember your name?" 「你還記得自己叫什麼名字嗎？」
The captain pulled the driest, cleanest blanket from the cabin and wrapped it around the child's small body—like a sail hoisted on a short, thin mast. He brought him dried fish and a water flask and watched the boy swallow. 船長從船艙裡拽出最乾燥而潔淨的一塊毯子，蓋住他瘦小的身軀——彷彿又短又細的桅杆上掛著一片帆。他為他拿來了魚乾和水壺，看著孩子嚥下去。
"Francis Bonnefoy." 「弗朗西斯·波諾弗瓦。」
The child's voice was soft and light. As the boat docked, the captain took him by the hand, registered him at the police station, and placed a notice in the local newspaper, The Golden Age, in case someone came looking for him—though he doubted anyone would. Victims of shipwrecks were notoriously difficult to identify. He took the boy into his home and loved him like his own child. The sun rose and set, and time pushed relentlessly forward, like an alluvial island at the mouth of a river. Over the course of more than a decade, the kind-hearted old man taught him to dive, to fish, to steer a boat. When he turned eighteen, he passed the weathered fishing boat down to him. He grew into an upright and handsome young man. When his foster father died, he wept bitterly. 孩子的聲音又柔又輕。隨著漁船靠岸，船長拉著他的手，帶他去警局備了案，又在當地的報紙《黃金時代》上也刊登了簡訊，以便有人找來。雖然他覺得沒有人會來找他——海難的遇難者一向身份難尋。他把他安置在家裡，像自己的孩子那般疼愛。日月東升西落，歲月不顧一切地向前推去，如同河流入海處的沖積島。十幾年的時光裡，這位好心腸的老人教會他潛水、捕魚，教會他如何駕駛船隻。在他十八歲時，他將那艘飽經風霜的捕魚船傳給了他。他逐漸成長為了一位正直而瀟灑的青年。在他養父去世時，他泣不成聲。
"Look at the sea, child. Look at the sea." 「看看大海吧，孩子。看看大海吧。」
Before the tide went out, he scattered his foster father's ashes over the sea. The tide line receded, leaving behind round holes in the intertidal zone where shellfish had breathed. He returned to the dock and sailed his foster father's boat out to the calm waters. He went too far; the sea turned dark blue, like a thick piece of blue velvet. He killed the engine, tied one end of a long rope to the side of the boat and the other end around his waist, and plunged headfirst into the endless water. He swam forward. When he felt the tug of the rope at his waist, he closed his eyes and heard the song of a whale calling to its companions. 他在退潮前將養父的骨灰撒向海面。潮線向後退去，潮間帶上嵌著貝類呼吸留下的圓孔。他回到碼頭，駕著養父留下的船駛向風平浪靜的海面。他開得太遠，海水變成深藍色，彷彿一塊厚重的藍絲絨。他停了發動機，將一條長繩的一端繫在船舷上，另一端綁著自己的腰，一頭扎進永無止境的海水。他向前游著。當腰上傳來拉扯感時，他閉上眼睛，聽見了鯨魚呼喚同伴的歌聲。
◇
That morning, a young woman was found collapsed on the beach. She had been dead for some time. Her body was bloated, lying face-down in a lagoon with the waves lapping at it. Crab pincers had left marks on her wrists, and jellyfish stings covered her thighs. The police turned the body over. Bloody fluid trickled from her nostrils—Francis had heard as a child that this was proof the soul had departed. A few minutes later, the body was wrapped in a white cloth and taken away for further examination. That was the scene he had dreamed of his own mother's death. He had not been born by the sea. On the contrary, he had grown up in the mountains. Before the age of nine, his concept of the ocean did not extend beyond what was described in Twenty Thousand Leagues Under the Sea. He had been irresistibly drawn to the world in those pages. In that wondrous world, the Nautilus became his small bed, his little cabinet, the dining table where his mother set down citrus tarts; Captain Nemo became his father, who shared his longing for the sea. One day, his father, returning from work in the mines, mysteriously brought him a piece of limestone. Under his father's expectant gaze, he tapped the stone with a small hammer—and inside the stone, perfectly preserved, was an ammonite fossil. His father spoke with the excitement of a child discovering that morning glories could change colour, telling him that this place might once have been a vast ocean, that the evidence of life's transformation was all condensed within this small stone. That night, he carefully scrubbed the dirt and dust from the fossil with a toothbrush. Before going to sleep, he placed it beside his pillow, letting the moonlight fall upon it, stroking its intricate, spiralling patterns. They flowed beneath his fingertips like a row of living keys. That fossil became his talisman—until the shipwreck, when it slipped from his pocket into the depths of the sea as he struggled. 今早一個年輕女人被發現倒在沙灘上，已經死去多時。屍體浮現巨人觀，隨著海浪，臉朝下地睡在潟湖裡。手腕上有螃蟹的鉗印，水母的蟄痕佈滿大腿。警察將屍體翻過來，屍體的鼻孔裡流出鼻血——弗朗西斯曾在小時候聽說那是靈魂離開的證明。幾分鐘後，屍體被裹上白布拉走，進行下一步的檢查。那是他夢到的自己母親死去的場景。他並非出生在海邊。相反，他在高山上生長。在九歲前，他對海洋的概念不會超過《海底兩萬里》中所描述的樣子。他無法克制地被書中的世界吸引。在那個奇妙的世界裡，鸚鵡螺號變成了小床、小櫃子、母親擺上柑橘派的餐桌，尼莫船長變成了他的父親。他和他一樣嚮往著大海。他的父親一次從礦洞工作回來時，神秘兮兮地帶給他一塊石灰石。他在父親充滿期待的注視下用小鎚子敲開那塊石頭——石頭中央保存著一塊完好無損的菊石化石。他父親的語氣像一個第一次發現牽牛花會變色的孩子般，告訴他他們這兒以前可能是一片汪洋，有關生命變遷的證據全部凝結在這塊小小的石頭裡。那個夜晚，他小心翼翼地用牙刷洗刷乾淨化石上的泥土和塵埃。臨睡前，他把它放在枕頭邊，讓月光照著它，撫摸它精密的、螺旋狀的紋路。它們在他的指腹下流動，彷彿一排有生命的琴鍵。那塊化石像是他的護身符。直到那次海難中，在他掙扎時從口袋滑進海底。
"In this vast ocean, a person is never alone, because it can feel the life teeming around it. The sea is a marvellous supernatural existence. It is movement. It is love. It is countless lives, as one of your poets put it." 「在這浩瀚的大海中，人並不孤獨，因為它能感受到身邊湧動的生命。大海是一個奇妙的超自然存在，它是運動，是愛。它是無數的生命，猶如你們的一位詩人所說。」
The sea is love. 大海是愛。
He tried to remember the past. It was like a vague dream. Just before he turned nine, his parents had finally saved enough money from selling minerals to plan a long journey. He remembered all the excitement, the sleepless conversations on the train, the sight of the sea through the carriage window, the smell of sunscreen rubbed on his nose and ears. They rented a sailboat and headed out to the deep sea, singing and laughing all the way. Hours later, when they realised it was time to turn back, they had lost their bearings due to inexperience. The sea at night, where only plankton glowed with fluorescence. He felt that the sea was like a moist fruit held in the mouth of the goddess of night. In the second half of the night, they were hit by a storm. The sky suddenly cracked open; the small boat, robbed of its support, swayed precariously; lightning blazed within the clouds. Torrential rain poured down, drowning all visible outlines. The boat was like a coffin. Before the rain grew even heavier, his parents placed him in the dinghy and pushed him as far away as they could. They were crushed beneath the capsizing sailboat, their outstretched arms like pieces of withered coral. Before the storm ended, he clung desperately to the edge of the dinghy. After the night of death, the sun rose as usual. His face stung from the sunlight. The sea was calm. He was too hungry and too tired. He caught a jellyfish and put its bell into his mouth, chewing. He could not die yet. 他試著回憶從前，有點像一個模糊的夢。在他將要九歲時，他的父母終於靠礦物買賣攢夠了錢，計劃來一次長途旅行。他記得所有的興奮與在火車上的徹夜長談、透過車窗所看到的海，抹在他鼻子和耳廓上的防曬油味道。他們租了一艘帆船，朝著海的深處航去，一路上歡歌笑語。幾個小時後，當他們意識到自己該返航，卻因為缺乏經驗而迷失方向。夜晚的海面，只有浮游生物閃爍著螢光。他感到大海彷彿是一枚濕漉漉的果子，銜在黑夜女神的口中。後半夜，他們遭受了風暴。天空遽然崩裂，失去依靠的小船搖搖欲墜，閃電在雲層中熊熊燃燒。暴雨傾瀉下來，淹沒了所有可見的輪廓。小船彷彿一口棺材。在雨更大前，他的父母將他放在皮艇上，盡力將他推遠。他們被死死壓在傾倒的帆船下，伸出的手像一截枯萎的珊瑚。在暴風雨結束前，他一直死死地抓著皮艇的邊緣。死亡的夜晚後，太陽照常升起。他的臉被陽光刺痛，海面風平浪靜。他實在太餓又太累，抓住一隻水母，將牠的傘蓋放入嘴裡咀嚼。他還不能死。
Hope Bay was mysterious. From time to time, it would swallow a person, a boat, a family, sometimes even careless tourists who were sucked off the flat rocks by the water. Most of the time, it would not give the bodies back. That was what had happened to Francis's parents. Yet he did not believe that their souls were trapped in this damp, deep prison. Their souls, and his foster father's soul, had all followed the currents to the dwelling place of the god of the sea—the origin of life. 希望灣很神秘，時不時的，它會吞掉一兩個人、一艘船、一個家庭，有的時候還有不太小心的遊客，從平坦的礁石上就被海水吸走了。大部分的時候，它都不會把屍體還回來。弗朗西斯的父母就是如此，他卻並不覺得他們的靈魂被困在這個潮濕幽深的監獄中。他們的靈魂，以及養父的靈魂，都隨著洋流去了海洋之神的棲處。那是生命的起源。
Now he poured cold water over his skin, his eyes closed. Years of exposure to the sun had turned his pale skin wheat-coloured, his muscles rippling. He opened his eyes and looked in the mirror. Sometimes he felt he did not recognise the person before him—those violet eyes showed no trace of unease or childishness. It was as if his parents' traces had vanished from within him, and he had become someone else. 現在他將冷水澆在皮膚上，閉著眼睛。十幾年的日曬讓他蒼白的皮膚變成小麥色，肌肉跳動著。他睜開眼睛，看著鏡子。有時他覺得自己不認識面前的這個人——這雙紫羅蘭色的眼睛已經絲毫看不見不安與稚氣，彷彿他父母的痕跡消失於他體內，他變成另一個人。
The knocking came just as he was about to trim his beard. The more urgent the knocking, the more slowly he put on his vest and shorts. A silent resistance. Dressed, he opened the door. Before him stood a young man in a long-sleeved shirt, around twenty years old, lean, a little shorter than himself. He was looking down, kicking the cobblestones at the entrance with the toe of his shoe, his golden crown of hair facing him. When he heard the door open, he looked up. His green eyes were as clear and bright as a fawn's. He glared at Francis with a hint of resentment, his hands fidgeting restlessly, as if waiting for him to speak first. 敲門聲響起時他正打算整理一下鬍子。敲門聲越是急促，他越是慢悠悠地套上背心與短褲。無聲的對抗。穿好衣服後，他打開門。門前站著一個穿著長袖襯衫的男青年，二十歲左右，身材瘦削，個子比他矮一點。他低著頭，用鞋尖踢著門口的鵝卵石，金燦燦的髮旋對著他。聽到他開門，他又抬起頭，綠眼睛像小鹿般清澈明亮。他略帶怨氣地瞪了弗朗西斯一眼，兩隻手不安地抓來抓去，似乎在等待他先說話。
"Are you the one who rented the boat for a month? Name is Arthur Kirkland?" 「你是租船租了一個月的那一位？名字是亞瑟·柯克蘭？」
After a brief recollection, he remembered why the other had come. 短暫的回憶之後，他想起了對方的來意。
"Yes. You said free room and board were also included." 「是。您說這裡還能免費提供食宿。」
"That's right. But seafood makes up most of it. Are you allergic to anything?" 「沒錯。但海鮮佔大多數，你有過敏的食物嗎？」
He naturally took Arthur's suitcase—the case was nearly weightless; he probably wasn't here for tourism. He walked and asked questions as he went. Arthur hurried after him. 他自然地接過亞瑟的行李箱——箱子幾乎沒有重量，他應該不是來旅遊的。他邊走邊提問。亞瑟快步跟在他身後。
"No, but I don't want to eat cephalopods. They look kind of disgusting." 「沒有，但是我不想吃頭足綱的軟體生物，牠們的樣子有點噁心。」
"Cephalopods..." 「頭足綱的軟體生物……」
Francis repeated his words, a faint smile flickering across his face. He placed the suitcase in his foster father's room—it was more spacious. 弗朗西斯重複著他的描述，露出一個微不可察的笑容。他將行李箱放在他養父的房間裡——這個房間更寬敞。
"Get some rest." 「你休息吧。」
He turned and noticed Arthur staring at his shelf. The shelf had once been lined with rum bottles, but now held his fossil collection: coral, trilobites, crinoids—but mostly ammonites. 他轉過頭，發現亞瑟正盯著他的架子。那個架子原先擺滿了蘭姆酒瓶，現在擺著他的化石收藏：珊瑚、三葉蟲、海百合，但大部分是菊石。
"Do you collect fossils? I'm a marine geology and biology student." 「你收集化石嗎？我是海洋地質和生物學的學生。」
Sensing his gaze, Arthur turned and gave a friendly smile. His skin was very pale, making his lips appear even redder. That was the extent of his first impression: a rather delicate-looking male university student. 察覺到他的目光，亞瑟轉過頭，露出一個友好的微笑。他的皮膚很白，顯得嘴唇更加紅潤。他對他的第一印象就止於此：一個長得有點嬌氣的男大學生。
"Just picked them up here and there." 「隨便撿來的。」
Francis smiled and shrugged. He drew the curtains for him, told him the mealtimes, and left the room, closing the door behind him. He calculated how long it had been since he had last had a conversation like this. The younger generation had long since left this harbour town, along with his foster father's old friends. The lighthouse railings rusted unattended. The local newspaper had ceased publication several times. The golden age of Hope Bay was long past, slipping away as quietly as fish fry escaping a net. Before he knew it, he had become alone in this world defined by the coastline and the horizon. 弗朗西斯微笑著聳聳肩，幫他拉上窗簾。告訴他三餐時間後，他退出了房間，關上門，計算著自己多久沒有跟人進行這樣的對話。青年一代早已離開這個港口小鎮，連同他養父當年的朋友一起。無人養護的燈塔欄杆生鏽。本地報紙幾度休刊。屬於希望灣的黃金時代早已過去，悄無聲息得像從漁網裡出逃的魚苗。不知不覺，他在這個海岸線與天際線組成的世界裡變成了孤身一人。
◇
Arthur woke up, his mouth dry. He did not know what time it was. The reversed day-and-night schedule of his journey had confused his sense of time. Sunlight streamed in, falling on his arm. He dressed, went downstairs, and smelled grilled fish. He did not know how he could help, and Francis did not seem to need his help. He sat at the table, idly fingering the petals of a canna lily. He thought about his research project—the tidal changes over the course of a month. On his way to his destination, almost everyone had spoken dialect, which he could not fully understand. They seemed to speak a language of the sea, mingled with sounds from underwater, the flapping of fish fins, the creaking of coral, and the muffled crash of waves breaking on rocks. He had also worried that he would not be able to communicate with the captain, but the man's accent was different from theirs. His accent seemed to come from somewhere more distant, like a light piece of music, making him want to pick up every note he dropped. 亞瑟醒了，口乾舌燥。他不知道現在幾點。日夜顛倒的行程讓他把時間搞亂了。陽光灑進來，照在他的手臂上。他穿上衣服，走下樓，聞到烤魚的香氣。他不知道自己能幫上什麼忙，弗朗西斯似乎也不需要他的幫助。他坐在餐桌前，用手指撥弄著美人蕉的花瓣。他盤算著研究項目——一個月內的潮汐變化。他到達目的地的一路上，所有人幾乎都說著方言，他不是全部能聽懂。他們說的彷彿是一種海的語言，裡面混雜著水下的聲音、魚尾鰭扇動的聲音、珊瑚咯吱咯吱的響聲，還有海浪撞碎在岩石上時發出的悶響。他還擔心無法與船長交流，好在他的口音與他們並不一樣。他的口音似乎來自某個更加遙遠的地方，像一陣輕鬆的音樂，引得他想拾取他丟下的每一個音符。
The steaming grilled fish was placed before him. He ate in small bites with his fork while listening to Francis describe the characteristics of the town. The other man still showed curiosity about him—the bus to and from here ran once a day, almost exclusively for departures. He explained that he did not like crowded places. Francis yawned, rummaged around under the counter, and finally produced a bottle of sunscreen, which he tossed to him. He said he did not need it. The other man simply said he did not want to see him get sunburned. 熱氣騰騰的烤魚擺在他面前。他一邊拿叉子小口吃著，一邊聽著弗朗西斯介紹這座小鎮的特性。對方仍對他展現出好奇——往返這裡的巴士一天一趟，幾乎只出不進。他解釋說自己不喜歡人多的地方。弗朗西斯打了個哈欠，在櫃檯底下四處翻找，找出一瓶防曬油扔給他。他說自己不需要。對方卻只是說自己不想看見他曬傷。
"The first time I went out to sea, I got so sunburned my skin peeled off. My foster father threatened to smear seagull droppings on my burnt skin." 「我第一次出海時，被曬得脫了一層皮。我的養父威脅說要把海鷗糞便塗在我曬傷的皮膚上。」
His words were like a spell, more of a threat than anything. So he listened to him, applying the sunscreen to his face and the back of his neck. Francis sat across from him, his eyes fixed on the same spot, seeming to think of nothing. When he finished eating, Francis told him to leave the plate where it was. Arthur rose and followed him to his boat. The boat had high sides. Francis stepped aboard first, then asked if he needed a hand up. He shook his head, grabbed the rail with both hands, and hoisted himself up. The other man patted his shoulder approvingly, raised the anchor, and went into the wheelhouse. Arthur stood at the stern. The wind blew his hair. The wake from the boat spread into the distance. The uninhabited island they were heading to was a forty-minute sail away. The sea was blue. But it changed—the blue of moonstone refracted, or cornflower blue; sometimes the blue of the sky at the moment the last rays of sunset met the darkening dome. On the face of the sea god, white reefs gleamed. If he stared at the surface too long, he would be sucked into the sea god's eyes. So he stopped looking and went into the wheelhouse. Francis freed one hand, a lit cigarette wedged between his fingers. Seeing Arthur, he stubbed it out. Arthur said nothing. He sat down beside him, trying to pick out his breathing among the calls of the seabirds, the sound of the waves, the wind. 他的話像是咒語，更多的是威脅。他於是聽了他的話，將防曬油塗在臉上和後頸處。弗朗西斯坐在他對面，眼睛看著同一個地方，似乎什麼都沒想。等他吃完飯後，他讓他把盤子留在原地。亞瑟起身，跟著他走向他的那艘船。那艘船的船舷很高，弗朗西斯先一步跨了上去，接著問他是否需要拉他一把。他擺擺手，用雙手拉著欄杆，將自己舉了上去。對方讚許地拍拍他的肩膀，拉起船錨，走進船長室。亞瑟站在船尾，風颳起他的頭髮，船行駛留下的水波向遠處擴散。離他們要去的那個無人島有四十分鐘的航程。海水是藍色的。但是這藍色會變——月光石折射的藍色，或是矢車菊的藍色；有時是夕陽將要落下時，餘暉與天幕交匯處的藍色。海神的臉上，白色的礁石閃爍著光亮。要是他盯著海面看太久，就會被吸進海神的眼睛。於是他就不看了，走進船長室。弗朗西斯空出一隻手，那隻手夾著一根點燃的煙。見他來了，他把煙按滅。亞瑟沒有說話，他在他身邊坐下，從各種各樣海鳥的叫聲、海浪聲、風聲中試圖分辨出他的呼吸聲。
"They say whales used to be active around here." 「據說這一帶曾經有鯨魚活動。」
Francis suddenly spoke, his eyes still fixed straight ahead, as if talking to himself. 弗朗西斯突然開口說道，眼睛仍然盯著前方，彷彿在自言自語。
"But I've never seen one. My foster father never saw one either. None of us ever have. On his bucket list, the only thing left unfulfilled was to see a whale breach. But that never happened either." 「但我沒有見過，我的養父也沒有見過，我們誰都沒有見過。他的遺願清單上，唯一的未完事項就是親眼看到鯨魚跳出水面。但是那也沒有實現。」
Hearing this, Arthur looked into his eyes. He had never seen such a look before—filled with anger, burning and sharp. The violet eyes were like shards of a mirror. A tingling sensation crept up his spine. He could not look away. After a moment, he forced himself to follow Francis's gaze forward—the sea was a vast blue, the outline of the uninhabited island appearing at the edge of the horizon, like a moth crouched in the water. 聽到他的話，亞瑟看著他的眼睛。他從未見過這樣的眼神——寫滿了憤怒，灼熱而尖銳。紫色的眼睛像兩塊鏡子的碎片。他感到一股酥麻爬上脊柱，他沒法不看他。過了一會兒，他強迫自己隨著他的目光向前看去——海水一片蔚藍，無人島的輪廓展現在海平面的盡頭，像一隻飛蛾趴在水裡。
When they docked, Francis jumped off first and held out his hand. Arthur placed his hand in that burning palm, jumped down lightly, and his legs gave way the moment he landed on the sand. Francis quickly grabbed his arm, keeping him from falling. The island was not large. Francis walked ahead of him, using a machete to cut through vines and plant roots until they reached an exposed cliff face. Arthur took out his bag and carefully examined the soil and rock fragments. At work, he grew even more taciturn than usual. With his hammer and chisel, he tapped cautiously on the different layers of rock, putting the fragments into glass bottles. His fingers traced the sharp edges of quartz, shale, and slate, seemingly indifferent to the pain. He was as agile as a cat, muttering to himself. When he ran out of hands, he held his notebook with his neck, his note-taking posture like a violinist playing. Francis stood behind him, his busy back moving in and out of his field of vision. In the centre of the back of his neck was a thin red sunburn line—a spot where the sunscreen had been missed, stark against his pale skin. On a whim, Francis reached out and touched it, startling the other man. He turned, his gaze flickering. Francis sighed and explained why he had reached out. Without a word, Arthur turned back. 靠岸後，弗朗西斯先一步跳下船，向他伸出手。他把手放進他灼熱的掌心，輕輕跳下來，踩在沙子上的瞬間腿軟了一下。弗朗西斯旋即拉住他的胳膊，使他不至於摔倒。這個島並不大。弗朗西斯走在他身前，用柴刀破開藤蔓和植物的根，直到他們到達一塊裸露的斷崖。亞瑟掏出包，仔細檢查著土壤和岩石碎片。他在工作時變得比平時更加少言寡語，拿著小鎚子和釘子，在深淺不一的岩層上小心翼翼地敲敲打打，碎片裝進玻璃瓶裡。他的手指撫摸過石英、頁岩、板岩尖利的邊緣，似乎對疼痛毫不在意。他像隻貓般敏捷，嘴裡嘟嘟囔囔。當他沒有多餘的手時，就用脖子夾著筆記本，他記筆記時的姿態彷彿拉著一把小提琴。弗朗西斯在他身後，他忙碌的背影在他的視野中晃動。後頸中央露出一道淺紅的曬痕——那是防曬油沒有塗好，在白皙的皮膚上額外顯眼。他鬼使神差地伸手去碰，把對方嚇了一跳。對方轉過身來，目光閃爍。他嘆了口氣，解釋他伸出手的原因。對方沒說話，又轉過頭去。
The sun set quickly. Francis reminded him that they had to return before dark. He packed up his things and went back to the boat with Francis. Francis brought out the first-aid kit. Arthur let him take his hand, let him apply ointment to his fingertips, covered with tiny scratches, and to his nearly peeled neck. Francis gave him a tangerine. An opaque curtain covered the sky's radiance. The sea before his eyes grew darker and darker, like a photograph slowly fading to grey in alcohol. The waves lapped against the hull, the sound hypnotic. He sat beside Francis, gazing at his focused profile, and before he knew it, he had slumped against him and fallen deeply asleep. His last memory was those violet eyes—when he looked at the sea, his eyes were so sad, as if the colour of the sea had seeped into them. 太陽很快就落下去了。弗朗西斯提醒他他們必須在天黑前返航，他於是收拾起了東西，和弗朗西斯回到了船上。弗朗西斯拿出藥箱。他任由他抓著他的手，把軟膏塗在他佈滿細小劃傷的指尖和幾乎脫皮的脖子上。弗朗西斯給他塞了一隻柑橘。不透光的幕布遮蔽了天空的光輝，他眼前的海面一點一點陰沉下去，彷彿一張在酒精中逐漸褪成灰色的相片。海浪拍打著船身，發出的聲音有如催眠。他坐在弗朗西斯邊上，凝視著他專注的側臉，不知不覺地倒在他身上沉沉睡去。他最後的記憶是那雙紫羅蘭色的雙眼——他看著大海的時候，眼神如此難過，就好像大海的顏色進到了他的眼睛裡。
◇
Whenever they did not go out to sea, Francis acted as his guide, taking Arthur to explore the area. Most nights, they went treasure-hunting in the receding tide. They would light a fire with a wooden plank and lay out their finds from the sea on the black stones in the sunset's afterglow—abalone gleaming with rainbow colours, fossils from the pebble beach, black conches, starfish, cuttlefish. Whenever he hauled up a 'cephalopod,' he would make sure to put it right in front of Arthur. The way he wrinkled his nose was adorable—he would turn his head away, waving his hands to make him take it away, like a child throwing a tantrum. 每當他們不用出海，弗朗西斯就盡他導遊的職責，帶亞瑟去四處遊玩。大多數的夜晚，他們在退潮時的海浪中尋寶。用木板點一堆火，然後在落日的餘暉中把從海裡撈出來的寶貝攤在黑色的石頭上——有閃著彩虹一樣光澤的鮑魚、鵝卵石海灘上的化石、黑色的海螺、海星、烏賊。他撈上一隻「頭足綱的軟體動物」，就一定要把它放在亞瑟的面前。他皺眉頭的樣子可愛極了——他會把頭扭向一邊，擺著手讓他趕緊拿走。像小孩子發脾氣。
Francis had once firmly believed that there was another world at the bottom of the sea, different from the one on land. Everything on land was moving away from him—the sun and stars setting, loved ones and companions leaving. But the tides kept surging toward him. Whenever he entered the sea, the waves surrounded him. He would row into the depths, feeling the lingering warmth of the sun in the water. He wanted to swim to the end of the sea, to the end of life, to the dwelling place of the sea god. He wanted to find the childhood he had lost. His family must be waiting for him. But now, whenever he surfaced, he could hear Arthur calling his name. He would turn, wanting to let Arthur look at him with those transparent, water-green eyes. Then the presence of his loved ones would grow faint, flowing toward a place beyond reach. After that, nothing. The end of the sea was forgetting. When Arthur looked at Francis, he never felt that he had entered the other's heart. It was like a ghost seen in a mirror, untouchable. If he broke that mirror, he would never see him again. Francis never revealed his past to him, so he could not know the source of his loneliness. Perhaps they had both been lonely for too long. But he would go far, go deep. He would look into his wide-open eyes. He would find the whale he had been searching for in his father's footsteps. He would make him hand over his past, filling his own heart with desire and pain, tying their fates together, like the sky shattered by the sea. 弗朗西斯曾堅定不移地認為海底一定有另一個世界，跟陸地上的世界不同。陸地上的一切都在遠離他——太陽和星星落下，親人與夥伴的離開。而海潮不斷向他湧來。每當他進到海裡，波浪就環繞著他。他向著深處划行，感受著海水中陽光的餘溫。他要游到海的盡頭去，游到生命的盡頭，海神的棲處。他要去找他遺落的童年，他的親人肯定在等他。但如今當他浮出海面，總能聽見亞瑟在叫他的名字。他轉過頭，想讓他用透明的、水一般的綠色目光看著他。接著，他親人的氣息開始變弱，流向一個無法超越的地方。這之後，什麼都沒有。海的盡頭是遺忘。亞瑟看著弗朗西斯的時候，從不覺得自己走入過他的內心。他像在鏡子中看到的幽靈，無法被觸碰。當他打碎了那面鏡子，就將再也見不到他。弗朗西斯從不向他袒露他的過去，他就無法得知他寂寞的來源。也許他們都孤獨了太久。但他要走得很遠、很深。他要去看他張開的眼睛，他要找到那頭他跟隨父親的步伐尋找的鯨，他要他把過去塞給自己，在自己的心裡注滿慾望和苦楚，將他們的命運連在一起，像天空被大海切成碎片。
Summer was drawing to a close. It was the season of forgetting. 夏季接近尾聲，這是一個忘卻的季節。
On the last day before his departure, Arthur had completed all his research. The air was tinged with a faint, sour scent. At dinner, Arthur suddenly proposed going out to sea to look for whales—whale activity peaked around seven or eight in the evening, and they had never been out at night before. Francis instinctively refused. The sea at night was unpredictable; he could not risk his life. After his refusal, Arthur fell into a silent dejection. Francis looked at his face—he had tanned a little since arriving, seemed a bit more alert. There was still sand on his face; he had missed a spot when washing. He sighed and told him that if they saw nothing, they would turn back immediately. Arthur lifted his lowered face and smiled at him. Francis reached out and wiped the sand from his face. 臨行的前一天，亞瑟的所有研究任務都已完成。空氣中瀰漫著一股淡淡的酸澀氣息。晚飯時，亞瑟突然提出要出海看看——鯨魚的活動在晚上七八點鐘達到高峰，而他們此前從未在晚上出過海。弗朗西斯下意識地拒絕了他的請求。夜晚的大海難以預料，他不能用他的生命冒險。亞瑟在他拒絕後陷入無聲的低落。弗朗西斯看著他的臉——他比剛來的時候曬黑了一點，似乎又機靈了一點。他的臉上還沾著沙子，洗臉的時候漏了一個地方。他嘆了口氣，告訴他如果看不到就要馬上返航。亞瑟低下的臉抬了起來，對他露出笑顏。他伸出手去，擦掉他臉上的沙子。
◇
When all things fell silent, the cry of the sea itself grew louder in their ears. At night, the sea seemed a sentient form—trembling, sobbing, screaming, suddenly shedding tears. In its tears, an old fishing boat danced, the sound of waves echoing in the solitary night. 當萬物陷入緘默，來自大海本身的吶喊漸漸響徹耳畔。在夜晚，大海彷彿是感性的形體，顫抖、哭號、叫喊，突然流下眼淚。在它的眼淚中，一艘老舊的漁船正在舞蹈，海浪聲迴盪在孤獨的黑夜中。
Arthur waited, still cold despite wearing a thick life jacket. He pressed himself against Francis like a frightened puppy. Their heavy breathing, the rustle of their clothes—each sound passed into the other's ears. Francis kept his hands on the wheel, concentrating on steering the boat. When they reached the legendary waters, he stopped. Waves pounded hard against the shore. The wind was calm, the sea flat, but the sea's hot tears kept falling. 亞瑟等著，穿著厚厚的救生衣還是覺得冷。他像一條受驚的小狗一般貼上弗朗西斯。兩個人粗重的呼吸聲、衣物的摩擦聲，互相傳進對方的耳朵。弗朗西斯將手放在方向舵上，專心致志地開著船。在靠近傳說中的海域時，他將船停了下來。海浪大力拍打著海岸，海風平浪靜，海熱淚不止。
"Time to go." 「該走了。」
He had expected this outcome. He turned and looked into Arthur's eyes. He noticed a green glint in them, the black specks around the pupils floating in a pool of icy water—the colour of the winter sea. He paused, sensing the other's disappointment, but spoke no words of comfort. They began to head back, silent all the way. 他已經預料到了這個結果，轉頭看著亞瑟的眼睛。他發現他的眼睛裡閃著一點兒綠光，瞳孔周圍的黑點漂浮在一汪冰涼的水裡——是冬天的大海的顏色。他頓了頓，感受到了對方的失望，卻沒有說出安慰的話。他們開始返航，一路沉默不語。
"I'll come back." 「我會再來的。」
Arthur suddenly grabbed his arm. His grip was not strong, yet Francis felt he could not break free from those slender fingers. He was forced to turn and look at him again. His voice was urgent, rapid. 亞瑟突然抓住他的胳膊。力氣並不大，他卻覺得自己無法掙脫這隻纖細的手。他被迫轉頭再次看著他。他的聲音又急又快。
"I'll come back. By then..." 「我會再來的，到那時候……」
Before he could finish, he fell to the deck. Francis steadied the wheel with one hand and reached for him with the other. Arthur had hit his ankle and could not stand for the moment—a gust of wind from the side had tilted the boat. A shrill sound went off in Francis's mind. The dark memories of the sea crawled through his brain. Dizziness, nausea, sharpness, intensity. Those clear, washed green eyes now blinked at him in pain. A rare, sudden change in the weather. High winds, rough seas. Clouds heavy with moisture skimmed the surface, shedding handfuls of raindrops. The boat began to list quickly. Waves mixed with cold wind and thin mist murmured in his ear, again and again. In a daze, he felt his parents' wet, cold hands grasping his arms. He was a piece of wood pushed and pulled by the waves, a seashell. He could barely see in the rain. The raindrops pelted his neck, and his waterweed-like hair whipped across it. 他還沒說完就摔倒在地。弗朗西斯用一隻手穩住方向舵，另一隻手去拉他。亞瑟撞到了腳踝，一時半會無法站起來——那是來自海面的一陣側風，把船吹斜了一些。弗朗西斯的腦子裡響起尖銳的聲音，關於大海的黑暗記憶在他的腦子裡爬行。頭暈、噁心、鋒利、強烈。那雙澄碧如洗的綠色眼睛如今對著他痛苦地眨著。航海中鮮有的氣候突變，風大浪高的天氣。飽含水汽的雲貼著海面浮過，拋下大把的雨點。船很快開始側翻，海浪夾雜著冷風薄霧，一遍又一遍在他耳邊喃喃絮語。恍惚間，他感到父母濕潤、冰冷的雙手抓住了他的手臂。他是被海浪推來搡去的一塊木頭，一隻貝殼。他在雨中幾乎失去了視力，雨點打在他的脖子上，像水草般的頭髮掃掠過他的脖子。
"Not yet." 「不是時候。」
He tasted a hint of blood on his tongue—blood seeping from his own lip. He took a deep breath, pressed Arthur tightly against his chest, and cut the life raft's rope with his knife. 他的舌頭嘗到了一點血腥味——那是他嘴唇滲出的血。他深吸了一口氣，把亞瑟死死地扣在懷裡，用刀砍下救生筏的繩子。
"Francis." 「弗朗西斯。」
He heard the voice calling his name. Sometimes it became his parents' voices, sometimes his foster father's, who had saved him. He pressed his fist against his eyelids, trying to keep the rain out of his eyes. 他聽見呼喚他名字的聲音。那個聲音有時變成他的父母，有時是將他救下的養父。他把拳頭按到眼皮上，不讓雨水流進眼睛。
"Francis!" 「弗朗西斯！」
Finally, he made out the voice. It was Arthur's. The other's slender fingers were clutching the fabric at his chest. 他終於聽清了那個聲音，是亞瑟的聲音。對方纖細的手指正抓著他胸口的衣服。
"Francis, I'm with you." 「弗朗西斯，我跟你在一起。」
He held Arthur so tightly he felt he might crush his bones. He said nothing, only gave a low laugh, the rain running into his mouth. He kissed Arthur's forehead, his messy hair, the corner of his lips. Then he placed him in the life raft. As he pushed the raft away, the boat capsized, pressing down on top of him. He protected himself with his arms, searching for the surface in the dark water. 他緊緊地抱著亞瑟，直到要壓斷他的骨頭。他沒有說話，只是低聲笑了笑，雨水流進他的嘴裡。他親吻了亞瑟的額頭、亂蓬蓬的頭髮邊、嘴唇邊。然後將他放在救生筏上。在他將救生筏推遠的瞬間，船體上下倒轉，壓在他身上。他用手臂保護著自己，在一片黑暗的水中尋找海平面。
"Francis!" 「弗朗西斯！」
Arthur shouted in the rain, shivering with cold. His ankle burned with pain. He could feel the cold raindrops falling, wetting the back of his neck. In the sand, the raindrops, the waves, something seeped into his body. The moment he saw the look in Francis's eyes, he had known his past. He had only just come to know him, yet now he had disappeared from his eyes—like a drop of seawater evaporating without a sound. 亞瑟在雨中大聲吼著，他冷得發抖。腳踝火辣辣地疼痛。他能感覺到冰涼的雨珠滴了下來，弄濕了後頸。在沙子里、雨滴裡、海浪裡，有什麼東西浸到了他的身體裡。他在看到弗朗西斯眼神的那一刻就知曉了他的過去。他才剛剛了解他，他卻在他眼睛裡消失了——彷彿一滴海水無聲無息地蒸發。
He dug his nails into his palm and forced his eyes to stay open. A long, resonant call came from afar. He turned towards it—through the curtain of silver-white raindrops, a massive creature lifted its head and breached the surface, its enormous body hovering momentarily in the air. As if sensing his presence, it called out again, then descended, the spray it raised gleaming like pearls. In some myths, such creatures guided the souls of the lost. 他將指甲掐進手心，努力睜著雙眼。遠處突然傳來悠長的鳴叫。他循聲望去——在銀白色雨滴交織出的帷幕中，體型巨大的生物昂首跳出水面，龐大的身軀在空中短暫滯留。彷彿感應到了他的存在，牠又發出一聲長鳴，身軀隨之下落，激起的水花閃著珍珠的光澤。在某些神話裡，牠們引渡著迷路的亡魂。
Arthur bowed his head, tears mingling with rain streaming down his cheeks. Until a hand suddenly gripped the edge of the life raft. He threw himself forward immediately, clutching it with both hands. Francis had a new gash on his face. His expression was blank, his eyes fixed on him with unwavering determination, like glass polished by the sea. He braced himself on the edge with both hands, rolled into the raft, and lay there gasping, utterly exhausted. Arthur held him, feeling strength being reborn within him. The fishing boat sank slowly before their eyes, until even its mast had vanished from sight. 亞瑟低著頭，淚水混合著雨水流過他的臉頰。直到一隻手突然抓住了救生筏的邊緣。他馬上撲過去，用雙手緊緊抓住它。弗朗西斯的臉上多了一道劃痕，他的臉上沒有表情，兩隻眼睛堅定地看著他，像是被海水拋光的玻璃。他用雙手撐住邊緣，翻身滾進救生筏裡，精疲力竭地大口喘氣。亞瑟抱著他，感覺到力量在他身上重生。那艘漁船在他們的面前一點一點沉下去，漸漸連桅杆也看不見了。
◇
"You don't have a boat anymore." 「你沒有船了。」
Arthur turned to look at Francis and suddenly began to laugh. The rain gradually stopped. The moon was so beautiful. He wrapped his arms tightly around Francis's arm and kissed the new wounds on his face. 亞瑟轉頭看著弗朗西斯，突然開始大笑。雨漸漸停了，月亮如此美麗。他用雙臂緊緊地抱住他的胳膊，吻他臉上新增的傷口。
"That whale—it's a shame I didn't see it." 「那頭鯨魚，真可惜我沒有看見。」
Francis cradled his face and pressed his lips gently against his. They lay side by side in the life raft. The starry sky was as deep as a goddess's eyes. The waves were slow and powerful. The sea was light again, soaked with starlight, the spray more dazzling than diamonds. 弗朗西斯捧起他的臉，嘴唇輕輕壓著他的嘴唇。他們並排躺在救生筏上，星空如女神的眼睛一般深邃，波浪緩慢而強大。大海又是輕盈的，浸滿了星光，浪花比鑽石更加耀眼。
"When my foster father died, he said to me: 'Look at the sea, child. Look at the sea. When you see the tide rushing toward you, that is me walking toward you.'" 「當我養父死的時候，他對我說：『看看大海吧，孩子。看看大海吧。當你看見海潮向你奔來，便是我朝著你走來。』」
He took Arthur's hand and pressed his still-bandaged fingertips to his own lips. 他拉過亞瑟的手，把他還纏著繃帶的指尖貼在自己的嘴唇上。
"I believe my parents' and foster father's souls have already departed. It's just me. I'm so clumsy, I imprisoned myself here. I've spent too much time just trying not to be lonely." 「我相信我父母和養父的靈魂已經離開了。只是我自己，我這麼笨拙，把自己囚禁在這裡。我花了太多時間，只為成為一個不寂寞的人。」
Arthur wanted to put his arms around his waist, but his fingers touched something wet and sticky. He jerked his hand back as if shocked, leaned in to look—an octopus was stuck to Francis's belt. 亞瑟想抱住他的腰，手指卻摸到一個濕濕黏黏的東西。他觸電般縮回手，將頭湊過去看——弗朗西斯的皮帶上黏著一隻章魚。
"My God, get rid of it right now!" 「天啊，馬上丟掉它！」
He roared like a little leopard. 他像隻小豹子一樣吼了起來。
"This cephalopod is a present for you." 「這隻頭足綱的軟體生物是給你的禮物。」
Francis pulled the octopus off and threw it back into the sea. He sat up lazily, patted Arthur's back, put his arm around his shoulder, and motioned for him to lie back down. 弗朗西斯扯下那隻章魚，將牠扔回海裡。他懶洋洋地坐起身，拍拍亞瑟的背，攬住他的肩膀，示意他一起躺下。
"Where should we go tomorrow?" 「明天該去哪呢？」
"Yeah, where should we go tomorrow?" 「是啊，明天該去哪兒呢？」
They chatted back and forth, and before they knew it, they had fallen asleep in each other's arms. The waves pushed them back to shore. Tomorrow, they would wake again in the sunlight. Not far from them, two enormous creatures leaped out of the sea together, their song like the blast of an angel's trumpet. They waved their great tails and swam toward the end of the sea. 兩個人你一言我一語地聊著天，不知不覺中抱在一起睡去。海浪將他們推回岸邊。明日，他們又將在陽光中醒來。在距離他們不遠處，兩頭龐然大物雙雙躍出水面，牠們的歌像天使吹響的號角。牠們擺動著巨大的尾鰭，朝著海的盡頭游去。
