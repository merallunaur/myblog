Romantic Confession of Sins 羅曼蒂克悔罪經

◇
He fell in love with a painting. 他愛上了一幅畫。
For the sake of gathering material, he had rented an attic room beside the artificial waterway in Delft—a room reeking of sewage, with poor lighting. The painting hung on the wall directly opposite his mattress. It depicted a thin young man leaning over a table, dressed in the clothing of centuries past, his head tilted to the left, resting against his shoulder. Sunlight filtered through dark green windowpanes, winding like waterweed around his eyes, which were as moist as a drowning man's. Slightly curled golden hair fell around his neck. His expressionless face conveyed a religious austerity and sanctity. His lips were as full and crimson as a peeled pomegranate. 他為了取材租下代爾夫特人工水道旁的一間閣樓，一個充滿下水道臭氣的，採光並不好的房間。這幅畫掛在他床墊正對的牆壁上。那是一位伏在桌子上的瘦削少年，穿著幾個世紀前的衣服，頭向左邊歪，靠著肩膀。透過暗綠色窗格的陽光，水草一般纏繞在他自溺者般濕潤的眼睛上。脖頸四周環繞著微鬈的金髮。他因面無表情而展現出宗教般的嚴謹與聖潔。嘴唇像剝開的石榴般飽滿殷紅。
"This house is haunted," the landlord warned him, urging him to acquire a crucifix. 「這屋子鬧鬼。」房東勸他求一個十字架。
He was neither a staunch atheist nor a devout believer. He was a defender of his own thoughts, an instinctive follower of his inner faith. He turned instead to inquire about the painting's history, but received no definitive answer. So he asked whether he might take the painting down to examine it more closely. The landlord waved his hand, telling him to do as he pleased. That night, he turned up the oil lamp, approached the painting, gripped its frame with both hands, and held it under the light. As he cut the painting from its frame with a knife, he seemed to hear a low, murmuring prayer. 他不是堅定的無神論者，更不是堅定的信仰者。他是自己思想的維護者，一個本能的信徒。他轉而詢問那幅畫的歷史，沒有得到確切的答案。他於是詢問是否能取下那幅畫仔細看看，房東擺擺手，表示隨他的便。夜晚，他擰亮油燈，靠近那幅畫，用雙手緊緊抱著畫框，將它擺在燈光下。用小刀裁下那幅畫時，他彷彿聽到低沉的禱聲。
Arthur Kirkland. 亞瑟·柯克蘭。
1665. 1665。
He traced the name with his fingertip. The young man in the painting—his name had been buried for more than two centuries. And he had been the first to discover that name, as if discovering an unnamed asteroid, as if being the first to lay eyes on America. He saw him die and collapse like billions of suns exploding, then struggle to be reborn from the dark blue amniotic fluid of the sea, climbing naked onto the silver-white shore. The first time he saw him, hot blood rushed to his cheeks; all his blood flowed more joyously; every pulse beat said, every breath whispered: it is he, it is he. His heart, too, recognised the one it had longed for, confirming to him: it is he. The world vanished from before his eyes. 他用指尖輕輕撫過那個名字，畫中的少年，他的名字被埋沒了兩個多世紀。而他第一個發現了這個名字，如同發現一顆未被命名的小行星，如同第一個看見美洲的人。他看見他如上億個太陽在爆炸中消亡、坍縮，又在大海深藍色的胎衣中掙扎著出生，赤裸著身體爬上銀白色海岸。當他第一次見到他，熱血便湧上他的臉頰，他的所有血液都流動得更加歡暢，每一次脈搏都在說，每一下呼吸都在低語：是他，是他。他的心也認出了它渴慕已久的人，向他證實道，就是他。世界從他眼前消失了。
He did not hang the painting back on the wall. Instead, he pressed it beneath his mattress, together with the treasures he had collected over the years of travel: a dried cornflower, a complete snakeskin, and an emerald necklace. While sketching in South Africa, a local witch doctor had insisted on giving him the necklace. Unable to communicate, he thought the man was trying to sell it to him, so he turned to leave. But the witch doctor wound the necklace around his wrist and pressed his finger against the birthmark on his wrist, then watched him go. His birthmark ran parallel to his lifeline, like a red scar. 他沒有把那幅畫掛回牆壁，而是壓在自己的床墊底下，與自己過去幾年在旅行中收集的寶貝一起。一枝矢車菊乾花，一張完整的蛇皮，以及一條祖母綠項鍊。他在南非寫生時，本地的巫醫非要把這條項鍊送給他。由於語言不通，他以為對方正試圖賣給他這條項鍊，於是轉身離開。但那巫師把項鍊繞在他手腕上，又按了按他手腕上的胎記，接著目送他離開。他的胎記與掌紋平行，像一條紅色的刀疤。
The first time he dreamed of Arthur, he saw him sitting on a table, his feet not touching the floor, his legs in white calf socks swinging like willow branches in the wind. When he saw him, he was paralysed by sleep, unable to move. After a moment, Arthur rose and walked toward him. With each step, more blood seeped from his mouth. Just as he was about to draw close, he struggled awake. 他第一次夢到亞瑟，他坐在桌子上，腳觸不到地，穿著白色小腿襪的腿像被風吹動的柳條，不停晃動。看見他時，他正因為睡眠癱瘓而無法動彈。過了一會，亞瑟起身向他走來，每走一步，他的嘴裡就滲出更多鮮血。就在快要貼近他時，他掙扎著醒了。
A few days later, he had a second dream. Arthur was still sitting on that same table, staring at him. He began to ask tentative questions: 幾天後，他做了第二個夢，亞瑟依舊坐在那張桌子上盯著自己，他開始試探性地提問：
"Arthur, how did you die?" 「亞瑟，你是怎麼死的？」
"I was murdered." 「被人殺害。」
"How did he kill you?" 「他怎麼殺了你？」
"He gripped my neck tightly until I stopped breathing." 「他狠狠掐住我的脖子，直到我斷氣。」
"Who killed you?" 「誰殺了你？」
Arthur did not answer. He only stared straight at him, his gaze like thick, dripping honey. Then he jumped down from the table and straddled his lap. He put his arms around Arthur's waist, and Arthur rested his head on his shoulder. He noticed two beauty marks on the side of Arthur's neck. He brought his lips close and sank his teeth into Arthur's skin. Arthur shrank back, trembling, then cupped his face and bit lightly at his lower lip. 亞瑟沒有回答，只是直勾勾地盯著他，他的眼神如傾瀉而下的黏稠蜂蜜。接著，他從桌上跳下，跨在他的腿上。他用雙手攬住亞瑟的腰，對方把他的頭放在他的肩膀上。他注意到他的側頸上分佈著兩顆痣，湊上嘴唇，將牙齒沒入他的皮膚。亞瑟縮了縮脖子，渾身顫抖著捧起他的臉，輕咬著他的下唇。
"Answer me. Answer me." 「回答我，回答我。」
He suddenly pressed his hand hard against the back of Arthur's head. Arthur held him even tighter around the waist, as if trying to melt the other into his own body. 他突然狠狠地按住他的後腦，亞瑟更用力地抱著他的腰，彷彿要把對方融進自己的身體。
"Francis." 「弗朗西斯。」
He struggled to wake from his trance, realising that he had never told anyone his name—not even the landlord knew it. He lifted the mattress. The painting lay still beneath it, its lips in the dim morning light seeming to fade like a painting lacking varnish. He was smiling—a sweet, newborn smile. 他掙扎著從恍惚中醒來，察覺到自己從未告訴過任何人自己的名字，甚至連房東都不知道。他將床墊掀開，那幅畫靜靜壓在原位，他的嘴唇在暗淡的晨光中，彷彿缺少光油般漸漸褪色。他在笑，新生兒甜美的笑容。
◇
He could no longer paint. Pigments had a life of their own—he had always believed that. Every completed painting was a sacrifice to an onrushing, ferocious beauty. He was a naturally fortunate painter; pigments rarely worked against him. Inspiration flooded his mind like intoxicating wine or irresistible opium. While praying in the kitchen, he dreamed of a murder. Murder—lips and eyeballs gouged out, the body face-down, discarded in the artificial waterway, crabs and lobsters biting at the corpse's neck. As he drew close to the body, it suddenly lifted its head, its bleeding eye sockets staring into his eyes. He staggered toward it, with no thought of avoiding it, until its teeth scraped against his lips. He slid his fingers into its wet golden hair. The first thing he did upon waking was to check the painting. Its lips were the colour of blood foam on a fish stall, revealing a sharp, jagged smile. 他再也畫不出畫來。顏料有生命，他一直相信，每完成一幅畫都是向來勢洶洶的美麗獻祭。他是個天生幸運的畫家，顏料很少與他作對。靈感湧入他的腦海，像醉人的酒或難以抵擋的鴉片。在廚房禱告時，他做了一個關於謀殺的夢。謀殺，嘴唇和眼珠被人挖走，屍體臉朝下，丟在人工水道裡，螃蟹和龍蝦正咬著屍體的脖子。當他湊近那具屍體，他突然抬起頭來，流著血的眼眶對著他的眼睛。他跌跌撞撞地走向他，他沒有任何躲開的想法，直到對方的牙齒磕著他的嘴唇。他把手指伸進他濕漉漉的金髮。他醒來時所做的第一件事就是檢查那幅畫，他嘴唇的顏色彷彿魚攤上的血沫，露出尖銳的笑容。
He longed for him. He would appear before him only to vanish abruptly, like a lingering, unforgettable silhouette behind a late-night traveller. Everything he evoked belonged to the deep of night. Inside his small forehead dwelled an obstinate will. His lips were like polished gems. The moon was digging out his eyes. In his empty eye sockets, two stars were preening their green feathers. Radiance, the spiral of wings, and a fierce beak. Now, his eyes were singing. 他渴望他，他難得地出現在他面前卻又驟然消失，彷彿深夜旅人身後的一個令人久久掛懷的倩影。他所喚起的均屬夜色深沉。在他小小的額頭裡，盤踞著執著的意願。他的嘴唇像打磨過的寶石。月亮在挖他的眼睛。在他的空眼窩裡，兩顆星星在梳理它們的綠色羽毛。光輝，翅膀的螺旋形和一個凶狠的喙。現在，他的眼睛在歌唱。
◇
When he dreamed of him again, they were entwined like a serpent and a tree. From his body came sounds like a drowning man calling for help. He would hold him tightly, forever. Sometimes he would take his hand and place it on his own slender neck, letting him feel the pulsing vein beneath his skin. His oil-paint skin was like soft, heavy cream soup. When he moved his hand away, the mark left on his neck was like a garland. He sometimes could not tell whether he wanted to protect him or to possess him in another way. His body was a vessel filled with every kind of desire. His desire for self-destruction. His seductive geometry. His terrifying, fatal vitality—like a tropical rainforest embraced by a ring of fire. 再次夢到他時他們如蛇與樹般糾纏在一起。從他身體中不斷發出溺水求救般的聲音。他永遠緊緊抱著他。他有時會握著他的手，將它放在自己纖細的脖頸上，讓他觸摸他皮膚下跳躍的脈搏。他油畫質感的皮膚如柔軟又厚重的奶油湯。當他把手移開時，在他脖頸上留下的印記宛如花環。他有時分不清自己是想愛護他，還是想以另一種方式佔有他。他的身體是一具盛滿了各種慾望的容器。他自毀的慾望。他煽情的幾何構造。他讓人毛骨悚然的致命生機，像烈火環抱下的熱帶雨林。
"Who killed you?" 「是誰殺了你？」
He gently stroked his defined spine, burying his nose in his wine-scented hair. 他輕柔地撫摸著他清晰的脊骨，把鼻子埋進他散發著酒香的頭髮。
"Fran... cis." 「弗朗……西斯。」
He panted in small gasps, reaching out his hands to him, gripping his shoulders with force, and heard the sound of his own ribs cracking. 他小口喘著氣，朝他伸出雙手，用力摟住他的肩膀，聽到自己的身體裡傳來肋骨碎裂的聲音。
"Francis, hold me." 「弗朗西斯，抱抱我。」
The dream took place in this very room. Francis's hand was on his neck. The afterlife was unbearably dull; most of the time, he slept. He had felt his love the first time they met—this was the second. Could two completely unrelated people truly look so alike? Francis had once been a portrait painter newly hired by his family, another romantic captive. His voice when he courted was as cool as a harp. Francis had killed him—at his own request. He had never received love in his own life; he would rather fall from the peak of love. Before killing him, they had just finished a skin-to-skin love. He groaned like a fly searching for a keyhole to escape the room, placing his hands on the fragile wall, then bursting into sobs. His clothes were new; he had reached the age for arranged marriages. After washing in the bathroom, he dragged Francis into the Delft night, murmuring meaningless words, their bodies rolling among the low moonflowers and grass. Then, as the raindrops broke free from the hot clouds, he sprang up and ran into the thunder rolling across a dark field, through bean fields and cornfields that reached his waist, the damp stalks and branches rustling as they brushed against his outstretched arms. The rain pierced their thin cotton clothes, beating against their muscles. Francis swept his wet hair back from his forehead. In a sudden sheet of lightning, Arthur, who had taken off his coat, let his body meet the rain directly. Until the next lightning flashed past, their tightly interlocked hands embraced a birch tree, their bodies leaning back, swaying in the rain. They moved slowly through the darkness, their hearts full of excitement. There was no moon in the sky, but the moonflowers possessed a faint precision, like a compass pointing to where the moon ought to be, so that they could recite poetry to the absent moon. Francis stared at him with burning eyes, pulled his arm, pressed his lips to his mouth, and reached his hand toward his neck. They arched like animals; Francis bent his neck back. He was in a state of ecstasy, unable to breathe. If he wanted his eyes, he would gouge them out and give them to him. If he wanted his lips, he would cut them off and give them to him. Some people inspire the desire to possess and toy with them; but this man made one long to die slowly under his gaze. 夢就發生在這個房間裡，弗朗西斯的手在他的脖子上。死後的世界極其無聊，大部分時間他都在睡覺。他的愛，相遇的第一次他就感覺到了，這是第二次了。真的會有兩個毫不相干的人長得一模一樣嗎？弗朗西斯曾是他們家新招的畫像師，又一個浪漫的俘虜。他求愛時的聲音像豎琴般冷清。弗朗西斯殺了他，是他自己要求的。他從未在自己的人生中收到過愛，寧願在愛的頂點向下墜落。他殺死他之前他們剛結束一場切膚之愛。他像一隻尋找著能藉以逃出房間的鑰匙孔的蒼蠅般呻吟著，把手搭在並不堅實的牆上，嘴裡接著爆發出哭聲。他的衣服是新買的，他到了相親的年紀。在浴室沖洗後，他將弗朗西斯拽進了代爾夫特的夜，嘟噥著沒有實意的字眼兒，身體在低矮的月亮花和草叢上滾著。然後，在雨點掙脫熱雲的封禁之際，他彈跳起來，跑進一片黑色田野上的雷鳴中，穿過高度齊腹的豆子田、玉米地，潮乎乎的莖葉枝稈，沙沙作響地摩擦著他伸展的手臂。雨點穿過他們輕薄的棉質衣服，敲擊著他們的肌肉。弗朗西斯將他濕掉的頭髮捋向後腦勺。在一道突然擲來的片狀閃電中，脫掉了外衣的亞瑟，讓身體直接和雨接觸。直到下一次閃電急速劃過時，他們緊緊勾在一起的雙手環抱著一棵樺樹，兩人身體仰向後方，在雨中搖搖擺擺。他們在黑暗中滿心激動地緩慢行進。天上沒有月亮，月亮花卻擁有著微弱的精確度，像指南針一樣指出月亮本該出現的方位，這樣他們就可以對著缺席的月亮朗誦詩歌。弗朗西斯用燃燒著的雙眸盯著他，拉過他的胳膊，嘴唇堵住他的嘴，手伸向他的脖子。他們像動物一樣弓著身，弗朗西斯把他的脖子向後扳。他處於興奮中，無法呼吸。如果他要他的眼睛，他就挖下來給他。他要他的嘴唇，他就割下來給他。有些人會引發佔有和玩弄他們的慾望：而這個人卻令人渴望在他的凝視下慢慢死去。
"You killed me." 「你殺了我。」
He watched the other's pupils slowly dilate, leaned close to his ear, and whispered. 他看著對方慢慢放大的瞳孔，貼在他的耳邊輕聲說。
He woke in a cold sweat, lifted the mattress, and saw that the lips in the painting had turned grey-white. The eyes of the portrait turned toward him, revealing a cruel smile. He threw himself forward and pressed his lips against the painting's lips. His body grew abnormally weak, as if someone had just drained his blood. He found his dust-covered pigments, poured vermilion onto his palette, and viciously stabbed the brush into the painting's lips. The lips did not change in the slightest. Then he bought other pigments, but no matter which he used, the lips remained as grey-white as a corpse's. He never dreamed of him again. 他在冷汗中驚醒，掀開床墊，畫上的嘴唇變成灰白，畫像的眼睛轉向他，展開殘忍的微笑。他撲過去，把嘴唇貼在那幅畫的嘴唇上。他的身體變得異常虛弱，彷彿有人剛剛抽走了他的血。他找出落了灰的顏料，把朱紅倒在調色板上，惡狠狠地將畫筆刺向他的嘴唇。他的嘴唇沒有絲毫變化。接著他又去買了其他顏料，無論用哪種，他的嘴唇依舊像屍體般灰白。他再也沒有夢到過他。
◇
When the will to live developed to its extreme, what followed could only be an even more magnificent and pathological pleasure in developing the will to die. He could already smell it—the sticky, sweet, fishy odour left behind after such desires had secretly flowed through him. In the early hours, he used the same knife with which he had cut the painting from its frame to slash open the birthmark on his wrist. He bought new clothes, cut off his long hair, glued it to the tip of a brush, and pinned the four corners of the paper to the floor with thumbtacks. He began to paint furiously and swiftly, as if copying a blueprint in a foreign land. Stealing close to a corpse, in the depth of such a night, he traced his contours from the painting. Sometimes the pressure of his brush was so heavy that it tore the paper. The brush moved against the grain and texture of the floor, as if painting a church fresco. He painted from memory or from guesswork. In the violent vineyard, his eyes bled wine-red blood. His deceptively beautiful melodies, his memories of a past life. His voice had a slight echo, lingering like an orchestra's enchanting notes. His beauty presented itself in lifeless lines. Every detail of his face signified an intensified emotion. His presence created an atmosphere and fragrance of lush, blooming roses. His existence was a blend of fact and fantasy, shrouded in an eternal, dreamlike hypnotic state—a state between consciousness and unconsciousness. Works of impulse and illusion, dazzling, terrifying. He was a rose among ordinary lilies, expensive silk among plain cotton. He was as sweet as nectarines, ragweed, honey, and sugarcane. His skin shimmered with a glowing, rich, luxurious sunlight; his eyes shone like moonlight and starlight; his voice was as romantic and obscene as poetry and erotic literature. He wanted him dead; he wanted himself dead, too—so that they might meet again. 當生的慾望發展到極致，接下來只會對發展死慾產生更輝煌的病態樂趣。他甚至已經嗅到了，這樣的慾望在暗自流竄後所遺留下來的一種黏膩甜腥的氣味。凌晨時分，他用裁下那張油畫的刀，劃開自己手腕上的胎記。他買了新衣服，將自己的長髮剪下，黏在畫筆的頂端，把畫紙的四角用大頭針釘在地板上。他開始猛烈又快速地繪畫，就像在異國他鄉抄繪一幅藍圖一般。偷偷接近一具屍體，在這麼深的夜裡，從畫中去探查他的輪廓。有時候筆力太重，會扯破畫紙。筆動時抵著地板的紋理、質地，像畫一幅教堂壁畫。憑著對他的瞭解或猜測，他在描摹。在暴力的葡萄園裡，他的眼睛流著紅酒般的血。他欺騙的美妙旋律，他前世的記憶。他的聲音有輕微的回聲，像管弦樂隊一樣迴盪著迷人的音符。他的美以一種毫無生氣的線條呈現出來。他臉上的每一個細節都意味著一種強化的情感。他的存在創造了鬱鬱蔥蔥、盛開的玫瑰的氛圍和香氣，他的存在是事實和幻想的混合體，籠罩在一種永恆的夢幻的催眠狀態中，一種介於意識和無意識之間的狀態。衝動和幻覺的作品，讓人心花撩亂，讓人心驚膽戰。他是普通百合花中的玫瑰，純棉織物中昂貴的絲綢。他像油桃、豚草、蜂蜜和甘蔗一樣甜美。皮膚散發著閃爍、豐富和奢華的陽光色調，眼睛像月光和星光一樣閃耀，聲音像詩歌和情色文學一樣浪漫而下流。他希望他死去，希望自己也死去，他們才能再次相見。
He painted the lips. With the brush made from his own hair. His whole body was cold, trembling from the loss of blood. He dipped the tip of the brush into his own wrist, soaking it with his blood. Then, propping himself on his elbows, he raised the brush and dyed the blank lips red. In that instant, he felt as if a hand were stroking his face from behind. He turned his head and noticed a burning gaze. Behind him, there was still no one. His strength exhausted, he lay prone on the floor, his forehead pressed against the painting's forehead. The brush slipped from his hand. He could not continue; an invisible force was carrying him away, dragging him toward the abyss. Just as he was about to lose consciousness, he saw Arthur's dark green eyes. Those eyes gazed at him with deep love, like a python about to strike in the rainforest. 他畫到了他的嘴唇。用他頭髮製成的畫筆。他渾身發冷，因為血液流失顫抖不已。他把筆尖伸向自己的手腕，將它浸滿血液。接著，他用雙肘撐著地面，提起筆尖，將他空白的唇瓣染紅。瞬間，他覺得彷彿有一隻手從背後撫摸著他的臉。他扭過頭，注意到某束灼熱的目光。背後依舊空無一人。他體力不支地伏在地上，額頭緊挨著畫像的額頭，畫筆從手中滑落。他無力繼續下去，看不見的力量正帶走他，將他抓向深淵。在他即將失去意識時，他看見他深綠色的雙眼。那雙眼睛飽含愛意地注視著他，彷彿雨林裡即將捕獵的蟒蛇。
"Arthur..." 「亞瑟……」
With the last of his strength, he pressed his teeth together hard. Feeling the sharp pain on his lips, he opened his eyes again. The painting before him smiled—a newborn's sweet smile. 他用盡力氣，用力闔上牙齒。感受到嘴唇上傳來的劇痛，他再次睜開眼睛，眼前的畫笑了起來，新生兒的甜蜜微笑。
"Arthur..." 「亞瑟……」
In the dim attic, the will to live vanished from within him. His shoulders were embraced. His hand was in his golden hair; he moved in the shadow of his shoulder. He could smell the wine in his hair. One of his wrists climbed up his arm, brushing past his sweat. His cheek pressed against the warm paper. His other hand, sweeping outward, touched the sticky blood. Arthur stroked the wound on his wrist, leaving a light kiss upon it. 幽暗的閣樓裡，生的慾望消失於他體內。他的肩膀被人抱住，他的手在他的金髮上，他在他肩膀的暗影裡移動著。他聞得到他頭髮中葡萄酒的氣味。他的一隻手腕在他手臂上爬升，擦過了他的汗水。他的臉頰抵在溫暖的畫紙上。他的另一隻手，向外揮去時碰到了黏膩的血跡。亞瑟撫摸著他手腕上的那道傷口，在傷口上留下了輕吻。
His strength was spent. He let out a deep, heavy laugh. His breath now was almost like a murmur, almost like speech. He breathed him in like an animal that had trudged out of the desert, back to its homeland, back to an oasis. His golden hair spilled across the wooden floor like a pool of water. He touched him, then opened his eyes—but before him was only that cruel painting. He smiled weakly, propping himself on his elbows, slowly leaning down. 氣力耗盡了，他發出沉沉的笑。他的氣息現在幾乎像低訴，幾乎像語言。他聞著他，像聞著一頭從沙漠中步履蹣跚地走回家園、走回綠洲的動物。他的金髮像倒在木地板上的一攤水。他觸摸著他，睜開眼，眼前卻只有那幅殘忍的畫。他無力地輕笑，用手肘撐著身體，緩緩俯下身。
"Ar... thur..." 「亞……瑟……」
As the light in the attic grew dim, only the white of the paper could be seen. Before his eyes was a sacred jewel; the blood on his lips, shifting with the smallest movements, was flowing into his body. 當閣樓的光線逐漸變暗，能看見的只有畫紙的純白。他眼前有一塊神聖的寶石，他嘴唇上隨著細小位移而晃動的血正流進他的身體裡。
"Arthur Kirkland." 「亞瑟·柯克蘭。」
He broke into a bloodied, mangled smile. Facing the painting, he pressed a kiss upon its lips—a kiss as light as a falling leaf. 他綻開血肉模糊的微笑，面對著那幅畫，在他嘴上落下如落葉般的一片吻。
