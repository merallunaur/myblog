1969
One night in early autumn, Francis woke Arthur from his sleep. He had heard on the radio that a comet would appear in the eastern sky a few hours before dawn. They climbed onto the roof of the orphanage. Francis, bare-chested, climbed the chimney ladder; when he reached the top, he reached down for Arthur's hand. His hands were like an adult's. Neither of them had started growing yet. He was only two years older, but a head taller. Among all the children, he was the oldest. 某个初秋的夜晚，弗朗西斯将亚瑟从睡梦中叫醒。他从收音机里听说，一颗彗星将在黎明之前几个小时的东方天空现身。他们爬上孤儿院的屋顶。弗朗西斯光着上身，顺着烟囱的梯子向上爬，到顶端之后向他伸出手。他的手像大人的手。他们都还没开始发育，他只比他大两岁，却高出一个头。所有孩子里，他是年龄最大的那个。
Sleepiness trailed behind Arthur like a comet's long tail. No one knew what a comet really looked like, nor could they be sure of its exact direction. In Francis's imagination, a comet dragged a long trail of light and would announce its arrival like angels blowing trumpets at the final judgment. He imagined a deafening roar and a blaze of light. He looked forward to the sky catching fire. He wanted Arthur to see it all.
