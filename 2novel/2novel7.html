On this breezy evening, Arthur was turning twenty-one. He carried two bottles of whiskey but didn't know whom to pour for. Under the Ashen Tree④ at the Longshore Stacks, he sat in silence, breathing in the stench of garbage. In Dogtown,⑤ they had no cemeteries, no columbaria. Sometimes bodies weren't even dumped anywhere—they were eaten by something. In the beginning, a Mexican man hung a photo of his dead brother on this tree, pouring him a drink each month. Later, the residents of Dogtown followed suit. There had to be some place for them to mourn the departed, since no Zen master came to preach here. He had seen that original Mexican man; most of the time he sat under the tree with a bottle of alcohol, repeating nearly the same words every day: 這個有微風的夜晚，亞瑟正在過二十一歲生日。他拎著兩瓶威士忌，卻不知道該倒給誰。在長灘堆料場的骨灰樹④下，他靜坐，感受著撲面而來的垃圾氣味。在狗鎮⑤，他們沒有墓地，也沒有骨灰龕。甚至有時候屍體都不知道被丟在什麼地方，被什麼東西吃掉了。最開始，一個墨西哥人將自己死去兄弟的照片掛在這棵樹上，每月為他斟酒。後來，狗鎮居民紛紛效仿。總得有一個地方讓他們祭奠往生者，畢竟禪學家也不在此布道。他見過那個最初的墨西哥人，大多數時候他都坐在那棵樹下，拿著一瓶酒，每天重複幾乎一樣的話：
"You could talk for three days and three nights about the stories of the past, but it's been ten years now."⑥ 「過去的故事聊三天三夜都聊不完，但這都十年了。」⑥
Arthur came here to talk to his parents. In truth, he didn't know if his parents were among these photos. But since childhood, the orphans who grew up together had chosen their parents from among the unattended photographs. Unattended photos were easy to distinguish—their corners were always flat. His father was a young man with long hair and a melancholy air; his mother was a short woman with a cheerful expression and brown eyes. They both had golden hair. They were also the parents of many others, he knew, so he always avoided the times when others came to mourn, coming alone late at night. What he needed was a place to set down his anxiety and longing, a place to strip away the ruthlessness and rationality needed to survive in Dogtown. These things had grown with him, almost like detachable cyberware. But he had to face his longing for warmth—like a lost animal, like an abandoned dog. 亞瑟來到這裡，與自己的父母說說話。事實上，他並不知道這裡面有沒有他的父母。不過自小時候，他們在一起長大的孤兒就在無人祭奠的照片中選定了自己的父母。無人祭奠的照片很好區分，邊角總是平整的。他的父親是一個留著長髮，氣質憂鬱的年輕男人，母親則是一個身材矮小，神情快活的棕眼睛女人。他們都有著金髮，他們同時還是很多人的父母，他知道，因此他總避開其他人祭奠的時間，在深夜獨自前往。他需要的是一个安放自己焦慮與渴望的地方，一個將在狗鎮生存需要的狠辣與理性完全脫去的地方。這些東西伴他而生，幾乎像一件可拆卸的義體。但他卻不得不正視自己對溫暖的渴求，如迷路的動物，如被拋棄的狗。
Dogtown. 狗鎮。
On nights without lullabies, Dogtown was filled with gunfire, the clash of metal, and the cracking of breaking bones—one of his companions had died on such a night; he never saw the body. His friend had been fourteen, the same age as him. In a more distant memory, he was fighting some kid in Dogtown, pinning him down and punching his nose until he could no longer cry. Then the child's father arrived. Arthur remembered how easily the man lifted him and threw him against the wall, as if picking up a feather. Broad hands, muscles, and a pair of sharp eyes. He held his groaning son in his arms, stroking his head. In that narrow, dark alley, the tall man curled up, protecting his son. That night, he walked a long way and lay down at the checkpoint between Dogtown and Night City.⑦ Discarded guns, fungal hyphae crawling over bones, chemical waste—piled high, stinking. But when he looked up at the window—past the spinning rotors of military helicopters and the cyan lights of the Heart of Duty club shooting into the sky—he saw that symbol. Lightning struck Obsidian, and he fell from the top of Obsidian. That was his revelation, an enigmatic sign. Later, he asked a famous Dogtown medium about it. It was the Tower, she said. A tarot card. Only those with souls could see it. 狗鎮。
"Tragedy, final judgment, and self-destruction." 「悲劇，最終審判和自我毀滅。」

Leaving Dogtown. 離開狗鎮。
He never felt unease over the lives that had ended by his hand. His own life would never be peaceful; he would surely go to hell. On a distant container, a drenched man was shouting, but he didn't hear his voice until the man burst into flames. He had been doused in gasoline. 他從未因在他手下逝去的人命而不安。自己的生命永無寧日，一定會下地獄裡去。遠處的貨櫃上，一個渾身濕透的人正在大喊，他沒聽到他的聲音，直到那個人渾身燃起火焰。他身上澆的是汽油。
"Hey, Arthur, there's a big job here. Pull this off, and you might just leave Dogtown." 「嘿，亞瑟，這兒有個大活，成了這單，你說不定能離開狗鎮。」
Amid the man's screams, his phone rang. The fixer didn't wait for an answer before continuing. 在那個人的慘叫聲中，他的手機響了起來，中間人沒等他答話就說下去。

He carefully reviewed the information the fixer sent about the target: a thirty-year-old Frenchman, a representative of Kiroshi⑩ sent to negotiate with the Barghest.⑩ Three combat cyberware pieces: one monowire,⑪ two unscannable. A Trauma Team⑫ platinum member. He had long hair, and his eyes were confident yet melancholic. Then, then Arthur turned off his phone. Obsidian—he thought of that distant revelation. Would he fall, or would he fly? 他仔細審閱著中間人發過來的暗殺對象信息，三十歲的法國人，作為歧路司⑩的代表來和幽冥犬⑩和談。三件戰鬥義體，一件是單分子線⑪，兩件無法掃描。創傷小組白金會員⑫。他有一頭長髮，眼神自信又憂鬱。然後，然後亞瑟就關上了手機。寶石青，他想起那個遙遠的開示。他會墜落嗎，還是飛行？
Dogtown. 狗鎮。

They had spoken a few times; the doctor's mother was also far away, in another distant land. 進入寶石青之前，他找那個亞裔義體醫生修好了自己早已過時的義眼。像往常一樣，義體醫生沒有收他的錢。他與他談過幾次話，他的母親也不在身邊，在某個遙遠的國度。

The stage glittered with gold, like a pack of leopards competing in a room. He stood up, turned his glass upside down, and lingered by the stage. In an instant, enormous red wings unfurled behind the singer, like cutting open a person's skin to reveal throbbing muscles and fascia, or like the sunset burning in the dark room. As if sensing his gaze, his target paused his conversation. The spectacular fireworks blazed behind him, and Arthur could not make out his face. Through the blurry smoke and the light spots filling the banquet hall, the man raised his glass toward him and drained it in one go. 似乎感受到了他的注視，他的暗殺對象停下了與身邊人的交談，聲勢浩大的煙火便在他身後流竄，亞瑟看不清他的面容。透過模糊不清的煙霧與充滿宴會廳的光斑，那個人向他高舉手中的高腳杯，接著一飲而盡。
Arthur felt dizzy and got up to go to the bathroom. All the lights, the music, the smell of Black Lace,⑱ the strutting dolls⑲ at the banquet, even the smell of tequila—all of it made him sick. He hadn't been a mercenary long enough to get used to all this. He leaned over the toilet, dry heaving. A piece of charcoal, red-hot with longing, slid down his throat, burning his insides. He injected himself with an expired MaxDoc, opened the stall door, and stumbled to the sink, splashing cold water on his burning face. 亞瑟感到一陣眩暈，起身走進了廁所。所有的燈光和音樂、黑蕾絲⑱的氣味、宴會上大搖大擺的性偶⑲，甚至龍舌蘭的味道都讓他噁心。他做傭兵的時間還沒那麼長，尚未習慣這一切。他扶著馬桶乾嘔，一塊被渴望燒紅的木炭順著喉嚨吞下去，灼傷了他的內臟。他為自己打了一劑過期的傾力治，打開隔間門，跌跌撞撞地走向水池，將冷水潑向發熱的臉龐。
"Are you alright?" 「你還好嗎？」
The man who spoke had a distinctive accent. He offered him a handkerchief. Arthur didn't take it. As he looked up, he felt a moment of dizziness. It was his father. No, it wasn't. Just as his hand, thin and slender, reached toward the man's face, he quickly withdrew it. It was his target. Minutes ago, they had looked at each other across the fireworks. He had unaugmented violet eyes. Those eyes were looking into his, lingering in his gaze like a drug, refusing to fade.
