◇
"Eurydice, while walking with her companions in the grass by a stream, was bitten on the thigh by a venomous snake hidden in the thicket and died in the arms of her terrified companions."① 「歐律狄克和她的夥伴在溪邊草地上散步時，被一條藏在草叢裡的毒蛇咬傷了大腿，死在她驚恐萬分的夥伴的懷裡。」①
◇
Francis Bonnefoy. His original memories were constructed from dialects he could not master. These patchy memories were deeply imprinted in his mind, like a rabbit caught in a trap—within reach the moment he opened his eyes. He remembered it clearly: the winter of 1745, when he was seven years old. The rain was incessant, the cold biting. He lived with his family in Saint-Pierre-Quiberon②, which faced west toward the Celtic Sea, beyond which merged into the Atlantic. Most people spoke Breton; only a handful understood French. The first word he learned after moving from Paris was "kazh-koad"—squirrel. But literally, the word meant "cat of the forest." From this unfamiliar dialect, he pieced together a historical fact from the Plantagenet era.③ "The Fair Maid of Brittany." "The Pearl of Brittany." Eleanor of Brittany,④ who was said to have deep golden hair, shining dark blue eyes, and the Plantagenet aquiline nose. She was proud and resolute. Yet she was imprisoned by her uncle until her death, unmarried and without issue. The locals held a particular fascination and reverence for this tragic princess. Everywhere—in religious sites, municipal buildings, galleries—one could see her portrait. Various pigments were scraped into patches and spread on wooden panels, compressing her entire life into fifteen inches by ten. 弗朗西斯·波諾弗瓦。他原初的記憶由掌握不了的方言構築而成。這些斑點狀的記憶深印在他腦海深處，如被捕獸夾夾住的兔子，只消睜眼便唾手可得。他記得很清楚，1745年，他七歲的冬天，陰雨連綿，嚴寒刺骨。他和家人住在聖皮埃爾基伯龍②，那裡向西對著凱爾特海，再遠就匯進大西洋。大多數人都說布列塔尼語，極少數的人懂法語。從巴黎搬來後他學會的第一個單詞是「kazh-koad」，松鼠。但根據構詞法，這個詞的字面意思是「森林裡的貓」。接著，他從他並不熟悉的方言中拼湊出一個金雀花王朝③時期的歷史事實。「布列塔尼的美少女」「布列塔尼的珍珠」。布列塔尼公國的埃莉諾④，傳說中她有深金色的頭髮、閃亮的深藍眼睛和金雀花家族的鷹鉤鼻。她高傲和堅決。但她卻被叔父囚禁至死，未婚無後。當地人對這位悲情公主有著特別的迷戀與崇拜，在任何宗教場所、市政或是畫廊，隨處可見這位公主的畫像。各種顏料被刮刀鏟成一片一片，抹在木板上，把她整個人生壓成十五寸長，十寸寬。
"Deep golden hair." "Dark blue eyes." These were utterly foreign words to him. His father was a renowned portrait painter, most famous for his mastery of skin texture and the colours of flesh and blood. But he could not see. He could not see. He had a dream, so old it seemed to have been part of his thoughts since floating in his mother's warm, soft amniotic fluid—he walked toward a fir tree, transformed into an element, and flowed through the tree's veins. After a long darkness, at the end of the leaf veins there was only a grey-scale interplay of light and shadow. That leaf became his eye. From birth, he could not distinguish any colour. The boundaries of all objects shimmered with a blurred gloss. Through shades of lightness and darkness, he separated one thing from another. This was his way of understanding the world. What was crueller was that the talent passed down by his father had not disappeared—for him, it was also a curse. He carried over thirty pencils in his bag, which he used to sketch the scenes around him. That was the only form of painting he could enjoy. The first time he used pigments, he was still in Paris. He placed his palette on the floor, melting the oil rich with mineral particles with his warm fingertips. Then he pressed his pigment-coated fingers onto a portrait he had just drawn. This new sensation thrilled him. He used various shades of grey to shape his own flesh and blood. The pigment, mixed with ground silver clam shells, shimmered on the canvas with a pearly satin sheen. He had just finished filling in this part when his father pushed open the door. He remembered his father's terrified, despondent expression, his pupils dilated, dark and deep. He felt a chill swing down and pierce his spine. 「深金色的頭髮」「深藍眼睛」，這些是對他而言無比陌生的詞彙。他父親是遠近聞名的肖像畫家，以其對肌膚紋理與血肉的色彩把握最令人稱奇。但他看不見。他看不見。他有一個夢，久遠到彷彿在母親溫暖而柔軟的羊水裡就已成為他思想的一部分——他走向一棵冷杉樹，化為一種元素，在樹的血管裡流動。長久的黑暗後，葉脈的盡頭只有一片黑白灰交織的光影。那片葉子成了他的眼睛，他自出生後，便識別不出任何顏色。所有物體的邊界閃著模糊的光澤。他通過濃淡，將一種東西從另一種東西身上剝離下來。這是他認識世界的方式。更為殘忍的是他父親傳給他的天賦沒有消失，對他而言，亦是詛咒。他的包裡放著三十多支鉛筆，以便他速寫下身邊的場景。那是他唯一能夠享有的繪畫方式。當他第一次使用顏料，那時他還在巴黎。他把調色盤放在地上，用溫熱的指腹融化顏料中飽含礦物顆粒的油脂。接著，他把沾著顏料的手指壓在他剛剛畫的一張肖像上。這種新奇的體驗讓他驚喜萬分，他用深淺不一的灰色塑造自己的血肉。那種顏料一碰到畫布，就閃著珍珠似的緞光——顏料裡加了磨碎的銀錦蛤。他剛填完這一部分，父親便推門進來。他記得他驚恐萬分的沮喪神色，放大的瞳孔又黑又深。他感到寒意一揮而下，刺進了他的脊骨。
"You painted a person with blue skin. Only someone poisoned by metal would have such disgusting skin." 「你畫了一個藍皮膚的人。只有金屬中毒的人會長這樣噁心的皮膚。」
He did not understand. In his eyes, everyone had the same skin. Only some were darker, some lighter. Darker skin reflected light; lighter skin emitted light. They were all good skin. Everyone had roughly the same, good skin. But after that day, his father never allowed him to use pigments again. He was trained only in drawing, in which he became accomplished. When he was ten, they could no longer afford to live in Paris and moved to this coastal town with its incomprehensible language. He collected silver clam shells on the beach as raw material for his father's pigments. After his parents died, what had been a routine became a pastime. As an adult, he rarely went to the beach anymore. The gap between him and the world grew wider and wider, until it could no longer be crossed. 他對此感到不解。在他眼中，所有人都長著一樣的皮膚。只不過有的深一些，有的淺一些。深色的皮膚會反光，淺色的皮膚會發光。這些都是很好的皮膚，所有人都長著差不多的，很好的皮膚。只不過從那天之後，他父親再也沒有允許過他使用顏料。他只被訓練了素描能力，並對此頗有造詣。他十歲時，他們再也負擔不起巴黎的生活成本，搬進這座語言不通的濱海小鎮。他在海灘上撿拾銀錦蛤，作為父親顏料的原材料。父母相繼去世後，從例行程序變成了一種消遣。成年之後，他便很少再去海灘上了。他與整個世界的間隙越來越寬，直至無法跨越。
Now it was 1760. He was twenty-two years old. This winter was the same—so cold that dripping water froze instantly. He looked up at the sparrows huddled together on the branches outside the window. Birds trap air between their feathers to ward off the cold. But today, even the sparrows were silent. A distant sound of collapse and hammering echoed. His gaze drifted past the sparrows. The manor on the hilltop was about to welcome a new owner; restoration work was nearing completion. He stood up, walked to the mirror, and wiped his face. He could barely recognise his own features. His face was utterly familiar to him, yet difficult to name. His identity floated outside his body, like a butterfly, out of reach. His room was filled with various plaster busts. Sometimes he would lower his lashes, press his forehead against those plaster casts, and search for similarities between himself and them. In his heart, he was kin to these things. 如今是1760年，他二十二歲了。這個冬天一樣，滴水成冰。他抬頭望向窗外枝條上挨在一起的麻雀。鳥類會把空氣打進羽毛之間以抵禦嚴寒。只是今天麻雀也不再叫。一陣遼遠的坍塌與敲擊聲響起。他的目光越過麻雀的頭頂。山丘頂上的莊園即將迎來新主人，修繕工作陸續完成。他站起身，走到一邊，對著鏡子擦了把臉。他難以認清自己的面容，他那張臉對他而言無比熟悉卻難以被叫出名字。他的身份游離在他身體之外，彷彿蝴蝶，觸不可及。他的房間裡擺滿了各種各樣的半身石膏像。有時，他會垂下睫毛，將額頭抵在那些石膏像上，尋找他與它們之間的相似性。在他心中，自己是這些東西的同類。
At this moment, he opened the window and let the cold, dry wind rush into the room. Water droplets clung to his skin, drawing heat from his body. In the distance, the construction crew descended the hillside. They walked in rows, like a flock of sheep returning to their pen. He picked up his pencil, closed his left eye, and held the pencil up before him. A few dots, horizontal lines, vertical lines—a scene. He reproduced what he saw on paper without any subjective colouring. The workers had all gone. A carriage, ornate and draped in black curtains, drove through the high fence of the manor. He put down his pencil and closed the window. Night deepened, and lights appeared in the manor. Through his retina, they looked like a swarm of fireflies flying to his window and stopping still. 此刻，他打開窗子，讓又冷又乾的風灌進屋子。皮膚上掛著水滴，從他身體裡帶走熱量。遠處，施工隊從山坡上下來。他們排成排，宛如一群羊正在回欄。他拿起鉛筆，閉起左眼，將鉛筆舉在自己眼前。幾個點、橫線、豎線，一個場景。他把自己所見不帶任何主觀色彩地複刻在紙上。工人走完了，一輛裝飾華麗，罩著黑色幕布的馬車開進了莊園高高的圍欄。他放下筆，闔上窗子。夜色漸濃，莊園亮起燈火。用他的視網膜看，像是一群螢火蟲飛到他的窗口，停下不動了。
"Hello." 「你好。」
An unreasonable greeting escaped his lips, not knowing for whom it was intended. 他的口中響起一句毫無理由的問候，不知該傳達給什麼人。
◇
Francis did not think he would ever have the chance to set foot in this tall, dark building. Only... only when the lady of the house asked who was the best painter in town, everyone would speak his name and address with a slightly hesitant tone. They would not forget to add, "But he only paints in black and white. Sculpture as well, though sculpture is expensive." 弗朗西斯以為自己不會有機會踏入這座高大的黑色建築。只是……只是當這裡的女主人問起城中最好的畫家是誰，所有人都會用略帶猶豫的語調說出他的名字和住處。也不忘補上一句「但是，他只畫素描。雕塑也可以，不過雕塑收費高昂。」
Clutching his toolbox tightly, he crossed the long corridor hung with sparkling sun-shaped decorations and was led by the steward into the drawing room. A young woman sat before him, her ankles resting on a velvet footstool, her head slightly raised as she spoke to him. He sat on a lower-backed chair, listening quietly. Someone brought him a cup of flower tea, took his toolbox from his hand, and placed it by the fireplace. 他緊緊攥著自己的工具箱，穿過掛滿亮晶晶太陽裝飾的長走廊，在管家的帶領下來到會客廳。一位年輕婦人坐在他的面前，腳踝擱在絲絨矮凳上，微微昂著頭對著他說話。他坐在比她低一點的靠背椅上靜靜聆聽，有人給他送上一杯花茶，拿走他手裡的工具箱，放在壁爐邊上。
"I want you to paint a portrait of my son. And he must not know about it, or he will surely resist." 「我要你為我的兒子畫一張像。而且，他不能知道這件事，不然他一定會反抗的。」
"Why me? With your means, there must be many portrait painters with better qualifications and lower demands." 「為什麼是我？以您的條件，比我條件好，要求低的肖像畫家，應當比比皆是。」
"That child is somewhat special. I cannot trust the painters from the big city." 「那孩子有些特殊。我信任不了大城市的畫家。」
"Very well. May I ask why you need this portrait?" 「好吧，能請問您為什麼需要這一張肖像嗎？」
"For marriage, of course. He is already twenty." 「當然是用於婚配。他已經二十歲了。」
"I understand." 「我明白了。」
"So, will you accept this commission?" 「所以您願意接下這一單嗎？」
"May I meet the young master before making a decision?" 「我能否見見這位少爺，再做決定？」
"Very well. Please do. Remember, he must not know that you are painting his portrait." 「好吧，請便。記住，他不能知道您在為他畫像。」
Francis asked to meet him, but in truth, he was destitute and had no choice. The lady rang a bell. A short maidservant came over and motioned for Francis to follow. He took his toolbox from the fireplace and climbed the stairs. Standing on the landing, he looked down and saw the lady's forehead for the first time. She still held her head slightly raised. A maidservant knelt at her left hand, soaking her hand in hot water mixed with rose petals and sage. Francis passed through another darker, narrower corridor to the room at the very end. The doorframe of this room was painted with countless birds in various poses, their beaks open in song. He gazed at them with interest for a while before raising his hand and knocking softly on the door. The echo reverberated through the corridor, as if he were standing in an infinitely long leaf vein. 弗朗西斯提出要見一見他，但實際上他已窮困潦倒，別無選擇。婦人搖了搖鈴，一個矮小的女傭走過來，示意弗朗西斯跟上。他從壁爐旁拿走自己的工具箱，順台階而上。他站在樓梯中段，向下望去，第一次看見婦人的額頭。她仍保持著微微昂首的模樣。一個女傭跪在她的左手跟前，把她的手浸在放了玫瑰花瓣與鼠尾草的熱水裡。弗朗西斯又穿過一條更加黑暗、狹窄的長走廊，走到最盡頭的房間。這個房間門框上畫著無數隻形態各異，張嘴歌唱著的小鳥。他饒有興致地觀賞了一番，才舉起手在門板上輕輕敲了敲。回聲在走廊裡四處迴響，他彷彿置身於一條無限長的葉脈。
"Please come in." 「請進。」
It was a cold, young voice, like the sound of water and pebbles colliding beneath the ice. It made one involuntarily want to press an ear against it, to drag it out of the deep lake and lock it inside one's own body. Francis straightened his back, entered the room, and was momentarily blinded by the sudden bright light. The room had large bay windows that projected outward in a trapezoid. Before the windows sat a silent figure, a thin frame draped in a loose silk robe, like a bird's skeleton hidden beneath its feathers. When he sensed someone enter, he turned his head, his eyelashes trembling slightly, his eyes gazing at him in silence, as if an invisible brush were painting those moist eyes in his sunken sockets, giving Francis the illusion that he was about to weep. The feeling those eyes gave him was more painful than visual—as if he had fallen into a trap of wolf teeth, pierced all over with no escape. Francis took a deep breath, forcing his gaze away from those eyes to the area around them. He noticed a large grey mark around his eyes, spread across his shimmering, silver-flecked skin. In that moment, he imagined a flower with two-toned petals. 這是一個冷冰冰的、年輕的聲音，彷彿冰面下水流與卵石互相碰撞的聲音。使人情不自禁地想要把耳朵貼在上面，將它從幽深的湖底扯出來，關在自己的身體裡。弗朗西斯挺了挺腰，走進門，被突如其來的強光刺得睜不開眼。這個房間有巨大的落地窗，向外凸起呈梯形。窗檯前一個身影沉靜地端坐，纖薄的骨架上掛著寬大的絲綢睡袍，彷彿鳥的骨骼掩藏在羽毛之下。見有人進來，他轉過頭，睫毛輕顫，一雙眼睛無言地向他凝望，彷彿有一支看不見的畫筆，正在那深陷的眼眶中描繪那一雙濕潤的眼睛，使他產生了泫然欲泣的錯覺。他的眼睛，給他的感受，痛覺效果大過視覺。彷彿他掉入一片狼牙陷阱，渾身被刺穿卻無法逃離。弗朗西斯深吸了一口氣，強迫自己的視線離開那雙眼睛，落在他眼睛周圍。他注意到他的眼眶周圍有一大塊灰色的印記，覆在他閃著細碎銀光的肌膚上。在這個瞬間，他想像著一朵有著雙色花瓣的花。
"Excuse me." 「不好意思。」
As if noticing his speechlessness, the young boy reached out, took a piece of jewellery from the windowsill, and wrapped it around his forehead. A thin chain, a few gemstones. The light filtered through the exotic forehead ornament, shifting angles with the movement of his head, flowing ceaselessly across his face, like cold water silently passing over his skin. The grey mark became momentarily indistinct. He then stepped down from the windowsill, walked toward him, looked up at him, and extended his hand. 像是注意到對方的失語，年輕的男孩伸出手，從窗檯上抓起一件首飾，將它繞在額頭上。一條細鏈、幾顆寶石。光線透過那來自異域的額飾，隨著頭部的晃動而不斷變換著角度，在他臉上不停流轉。彷彿冷水無聲地掠過他的肌膚。那塊灰色的印記霎時間變得難以分辨。他旋即走下窗檯，走向他，抬頭望著他，同時向他伸出手。
"I've heard from my mother. Mr. Francis Bonnefoy. You are my new valet." 「我聽母親提過了。弗朗西斯·波諾弗瓦先生。您是我的新男僕。」
"Yes, that's me. Thank you for remembering my name. What shall I call you, young master?" 「是我，謝謝您記住我的名字。請問怎麼稱呼您，少爺？」
"I have a name. It's Arthur Kirkland." 「我有名字，我叫亞瑟·柯克蘭。」
"Very well, Master Kirkland." 「好的，柯克蘭少爺。」
"My name is not 'Master,' it's Arthur." 「我不叫少爺，我叫亞瑟。」
"Very well, Arthur." 「好的，亞瑟。」
Francis reached out and shook his hand. Arthur looked at his hand, then back up at him, breaking into a smile. The forehead chain slipped off with the movement of his facial muscles. He let go and lunged forward, trying to catch it before it shattered. But Francis caught it first. He stroked it, feeling the angular textures set in the metal base, a flicker of confusion in his pale eyes. 弗朗西斯伸出手與他交握。亞瑟看著他的手，接著又抬起頭來看他，綻放出一个笑容。那條額鏈隨著面部肌肉的運動不慎掉落，他鬆開手，向前撲去，試圖在摔碎之前接住它。弗朗西斯卻先他一步將額鏈捏在手裡。他撫摸著它，撫摸嵌在金屬底座上棱角分明的紋理，顏色淺淡的眼中困惑乍現。
"Why do you wear this?" 「您為什麼戴著這個？」
"Because... are you asking knowingly?" 「因為……你在明知故問嗎？」
Arthur frowned. His expression was both annoyed and puzzled. He had never heard this question before, because the answer was plainly visible on his face. He was a person born with a defect, a vase missing a piece, a garment with a pulled thread. Now he looked at Francis's face, searching for the sarcasm he expected to find. 亞瑟皺起眉頭。臉上的表情既像厭煩、又似疑惑。這個問題他從未聽到過，因為答案在他的臉上昭然若揭。他。他是一個天生帶有缺陷的人，一個缺了一塊的花瓶，一件勾絲上衣。此刻他看著弗朗西斯的臉，想從他的臉上找到他預想中的譏諷情緒。
"No." 「不。」
Francis turned his head, gazing deeply at the grey mark. He stepped closer, raised his hand—those eyes, that skin, exposed defenselessly beneath his calloused fingertips. 弗朗西斯轉過頭，深深凝眸於那片灰色的印記。他靠近了他，抬起手，那雙眼睛，那塊肌膚，毫無防備地暴露在他長有薄繭的指尖下。
"I simply think your face is perfect. Especially here." 「我只是認為，您的臉長得很完美。尤其是這裡。」
"Oh... thank you for saying so." 「哦…謝謝你這麼說。」
Arthur lowered his head, touching his earlobe to cool the burning skin. 亞瑟低下頭，把手放在耳垂上，給那塊滾燙的肌膚降溫。
"Young master... Arthur, I should go to my room and settle in." 「少爺……亞瑟，我得回房間安頓一下了。」
"Mm. Farewell." 「嗯。再會。」
Francis waved his hand and walked out. At the door, he turned back to look at him for no reason. Arthur stood beneath the bay window, his face melting into the shifting afternoon sunlight. The gaze from those eyes flowed toward him, like wet, unfixed watercolour on canvas. Seeing Francis turn, he waved at him. Francis waved back, smiling, then disappeared into the dark, narrow depths of the corridor. 弗朗西斯擺了擺手，走出門去。在門口，他沒來由地回過頭去看他。亞瑟站在落地窗下，面容融進正在偏移的午後陽光中。那雙眼睛所投射出的目光一直向他匯聚著，彷彿畫布上流動著的，未乾的水彩。見他回頭，他向他招了招手。他也笑著揮手，接著消失在黑暗狹窄的走廊深處。
◇
Francis was placed in a room at the other end of the same corridor, separated from Arthur's room by countless other rooms and a wide, endless staircase. He had asked to stay there, to "deepen his understanding" of the young master, "so as to capture his charm in every meticulous detail." His mother readily agreed. If the final result was perfect, any effort was worthwhile. At first, they did not have much time together. Arthur preferred to go out at night and walk in the garden. Francis, on the other hand, needed to paint by candlelight at night. A black-and-white portrait did not require much time, but he painted with particular care and difficulty. Sometimes, through the window, under the midnight moonlight, he could see Arthur sitting on the garden swing. At such times, Arthur would remove the ornament hiding his birthmark, then stand on the swing and swing higher and higher. Sometimes he swung too high—the moon would turn him into a black silhouette, a bird about to take flight. Francis would close the window tightly, as if trying to shut out the throbbing that beat against his pane. At other times, he would see Arthur's gaze fall on his window. Then he would cover his candle with a glass shade and pretend to be asleep. His perception of light made him never realise how bright his room actually was. 弗朗西斯被安排在這一條走廊另一個盡頭的房間裡。與亞瑟的房間還隔了無數個房間，和一條寬大的、無盡的樓梯。他提出要住在這裡，對這位少爺「加深了解，以便於巨細無遺地表現出他的魅力」。他的母親欣然應允。只要最終能得到一個完美的結果，任何努力都是值得的。他們在最開始並沒有獲得什麼相處的時間。亞瑟喜歡在晚上出門，去花園裡走走。而弗朗西斯晚上總需要點著蠟燭作畫。一幅黑白肖像畫，並不需要很長時間，但他畫得尤為細緻與艱難。有時他能透過窗子，在午夜的月光下，看見亞瑟坐在花園的鞦韆上。這時，他會取下遮蓋他胎記的裝飾，接著站上鞦韆，越盪越高。有時他盪得太高了——月亮會把他變成一片黑色的剪影，一隻振翅欲飛的鳥。弗朗西斯閉緊窗戶，彷彿要將正拍打他窗子的一部分悸動拒之門外。有時他又會看見，亞瑟的目光落在他的窗戶上，他會在那時給蠟燭罩上玻璃，假裝自己已經睡下。他對光線的感知令他總意識不到自己的房間有多亮。
"It's strange. You're my personal valet, yet you live farthest from me." 「這很奇怪，你是我的貼身男僕，可你住得離我最遠。」
"Perhaps your mother has her own considerations." 「可能您母親有自己的考量。」
Arthur stopped speaking and lowered his head, turning his gaze to the distant beach. This was one of his rare moments out on a sunny day. He had gained some genuine days of breathing under the sun. A large group of walking fiddler crabs moved into the light, their enormous right claws waving in greeting. He reached out, trying to catch the biggest one. The crab's sharp pincer pinched his index finger, then released it and continued along the tide line. 亞瑟停止了說話，低下頭。將目光投向遠處的沙灘。這是他為數不多的，在大晴天出門的時刻。他獲得了一些真正在陽光下呼吸的日子。一大片行走著的招潮蟹走進光線裡，巨大的右鉗在空中舞動致意。他伸出手，想抓住最大的那一隻。螃蟹鋒利的鉗子在他的食指上夾了一下，又鬆開，繼續沿著潮汐線前進。
"Ah!" 「啊！」
Arthur looked at his bleeding finger and could not help crying out. Francis had been picking up shells, but his eyes had followed him the whole time. He knew the moment Arthur's gaze met his. He ran over quickly and carefully took his wrist. He squeezed the small wound with his thumb and forefinger, and blood dripped like ink onto the empty sand. 亞瑟看著自己流血的手指，忍不住驚呼出聲。弗朗西斯正在撿貝殼，但他的眼神一直跟著他，知道亞瑟也和他視線相對。他快步跑過去，小心翼翼地執起他的手腕。他用食指和拇指擠壓那塊小小的傷口，血像墨汁一般，滴在空白的沙灘上。
"Why are you doing this?" 「你為什麼要這麼做？」
"If you were infected by some parasite or bacteria, you could die." 「如果你感染了某種寄生蟲或是細菌，你很有可能會死掉。」
Francis frowned, looking at Arthur's face as if reproaching him for his carelessness. He then released his hand and reached into his own pocket. He always kept a bottle of alcohol in his pocket, as if always prepared to wipe something away. He lowered his head, poured alcohol onto his handkerchief, and gently wiped Arthur's injured fingertip. 弗朗西斯皺起眉頭，看向亞瑟的臉，似乎在責怪他為什麼如此不小心。他復而鬆開他的手，將手伸進自己的口袋。他的口袋裡總放著一瓶酒精，彷彿他一直為擦去什麼做著準備。他低下頭，將酒精倒在手帕上，在亞瑟受傷的指腹上輕輕擦拭。
"Let me do it myself." 「我自己來。」
Francis shook his head almost imperceptibly, ignoring Arthur's request. The alcohol evaporated quickly on the skin, leaving a pungent smell in the air. Arthur looked down at the hand holding his wrist until it finally let go. Feeling awkward, he asked Francis what he had been picking up. 弗朗西斯微不可察地搖了搖頭，對亞瑟提出的請求置若罔聞。酒精在皮膚上迅速揮發，空氣中一股刺鼻的氣味。亞瑟低頭看著握住他手腕的那隻手，直到它從自己身上移開。他覺得有些尷尬，便開口問弗朗西斯剛剛在撿什麼。
"Seashells. I've picked up quite a lot." 「貝殼，我撿了很多了。」
Francis opened his pouch to show him. About twenty or thirty pure white moon shells were neatly stacked inside, completely covering the unopened pigments and pencils beneath. 弗朗西斯打開手袋向他展示。大概二三十個純白色的月亮貝整整齊齊地碼放在手袋中，把下層從未開封過的顏料與鉛筆遮得嚴嚴實實。
"Why are you picking these up?" 「你撿這些做什麼？」
"I just felt like it." 「只是想撿而已。」
Arthur pursed his lips, turned, and walked into the sea. He rolled up his trousers and placed his shoes beyond the tide line. The winter seawater was bone-chilling cold; wet clothes clung tightly to his body. 亞瑟撇了撇嘴，轉身向海裡走去。他捲起褲腿，脫下的鞋子放在潮汐線以外。冬天的海水冰冷刺骨，濕衣物緊緊貼在他的身上。
"The sea is especially blue today." 「今天，海格外藍。」
He turned back seeking confirmation, but saw a flicker of confusion in the other's eyes. So he turned away again, holding up one hand—the injured hand—to keep the wound from being contaminated by plankton. Francis stood on the shore, watching all this with interest. He was more accustomed to being an observer than a participant. Just as colour perception had been stripped from him, he stripped away any people or events that might connect with him. His gaze slid between the grey sea and Arthur's retreating figure; his thoughts drifted into the air, like a bird flying into a black-and-white, withered oasis. It was not until the tide began to rise that he snapped out of it and called Arthur back. 他回頭尋找認同，卻在對方的眼中看到了一閃而過的困惑。於是，他又轉過頭去，高舉著一隻手，那隻受傷的手，以防傷口被浮游生物污染。而弗朗西斯站在岸邊，饒有興致地看著這一切。他比起參與者習慣了做一個旁觀者。像色覺剝離他那般，剝離任何可能與他發生聯繫的人與事。他的視線在灰色的海面和亞瑟的背影間滑行，思緒飄進了空氣中，像一隻鳥飛進黑白枯萎的綠洲。直到漸漸漲潮，他方回過神，呼喚亞瑟回去。
"Coming." 「就來。」
Arthur replied softly, his voice seeming to come from the other end of the ocean current. He turned his head, and the chain on his forehead snapped and sank into the water, disappearing. He stood stunned for a few seconds, then raised his head and walked toward the shore. He still held his injured hand high. Like a swan gliding across a desolate lake—melancholy, serene. His eyes curved slightly, revealing a smile that cast itself into the sudden silence between them. One moment he was still wading slowly through the sea. The next instant, he was beside him, letting Francis dry his hair, his legs, his feet, and wrap him in a blanket. His wet hair plastered to his cheeks; he looked like a faded, weary statue. He gazed steadily into those silent violet eyes, as if trying to reach through that closed window of the soul, seize the other's heart, and force him to pour all his thoughts into his body. As his body gradually warmed, he belatedly raised his injured hand, trying to cover the now inescapable birthmark on his face. 「就來。」
"Let's go." 「走吧。」
His wrist was again taken. Francis pulled him toward the manor. The pressure on his wrist now was a more intense pain than the fiddler crab's pincer. Even after he lay in bed, wrapped tightly in his quilt, that phantom pain did not fade. That night, he did not fall asleep until late. Candlelight flickered in the room at the other end of the corridor—Francis was painting in the night. He had already finished the body and clothing. But the face—he could not bring himself to put down the brush. In this world full of countless people, most had to paint others, write about others. But Arthur had stirred something newborn in him, like a child discovering himself in a mirror for the first time—discovering that he existed in this world. He felt a responsibility to reproduce Arthur in art, even the smallest gestures or ways of speaking. But at this moment, he opened the box of pigments he had never used, ground up the shells he had picked up that afternoon, and added them in. The lifeless pigments instantly took on a soft, pearly lustre, shimmering in the flickering candlelight. He was searching for the colour of Arthur's eyes. His hand trembled, and he added too much water. The pigment, close in shade to Arthur's grey eyes, pressed onto the wet paper, then dripped from the paper into the gaps of the wooden floor. As if his eyes—and the rare skin around them—were melting before him. In a daze, he reached out, let the pigment on the paper fall into his palm, and raised his hand to the candle. The drop of pigment trembled, instantly shattering into countless tiny reflections of himself—frowning or smiling at him. He shook his hand away in panic, trying to rid himself of his traces. Then he picked up his brush and frantically laid down lines and shadows on the paper. He finished the face. That night, he extinguished his candle early and could not sleep. 亞瑟低低地應了一聲，聲音彷彿來自洋流的另一端。他轉過頭，眉心的鏈子應聲而斷，沉入水底消失。他呆愣了兩秒鐘，接著抬起頭向岸邊走來。他仍舊高舉著那隻受傷的手。彷彿一隻天鵝游弋在荒涼的湖面上，憂鬱、寧靜。那雙眼睛輕輕彎了彎，露出一個微笑，朝他們之間這一陣遽然而至的靜默中投去。上一秒，他還在海水裡緩步前行。頃刻間，他又來到了他身邊，任由他擦乾自己的頭髮與腿腳，裹上毛毯。濕漉漉的頭髮貼著面頰，他看起來像一尊褪色的、疲累的雕像。他安定地凝望眼前那雙默默無語的紫色眼睛，彷彿要透過那扇緊閉的心靈之窗，抓住對方的心臟，進而逼他把所有想法都傾注到他的體內。身體漸漸回暖之後，他後知後覺地抬起受傷的那隻手，想要擋住自己臉上如今無處遁形的胎記。
◇
"The lamentation and wailing of this water nymph echoed continuously through the high mountains and valleys. Orpheus's cries and songs mingled with them, his mournful verses pouring out his grief. Birds and creatures of all sizes, both spiritual and mundane, joined the solitary man in mourning. But his prayers and weeping could not summon back his beloved." 「這位水神的悲鳴和哀號不停地在高山峽谷裡迴盪。俄爾普斯的痛哭和歌唱也夾雜在其中，他哀婉的歌曲傾訴著他的悲痛。小鳥和有靈性的大小動物跟這位孤獨男子一起舉哀。但他的祈禱和哭訴並沒有喚回他的愛人。」
◇
Francis woke from a dream to the sound of birdsong. He calculated by instinct that it was early morning. He had finished the portrait a week ago but had not yet handed it over. It was not the Arthur he had imagined. Using the pretext of being a valet, he continued to interact with Arthur. But the more he tried to understand him, the more his image blurred, like the pigments that had been too wet that day. All memories of him lost their shape, like water slipping through his fingers. 在一陣鳥叫聲中，弗朗西斯從夢中醒來。他靠本能計算著時間，應該是清晨。他早在一周前完成了那幅畫，但卻遲遲沒有把它交出去。那並不是他想像中的亞瑟。藉著男僕的身份與由頭，他繼續與亞瑟進行著接觸。只是他愈想了解他，他的形象卻愈發模糊，宛若那天太過濕潤的顏料。與他有關的一切記憶都失去了形狀，如掌中流水。
This afternoon, he was gazing at the thin layer of sunlight on the windowsill while smoking a cigarette. Among the few things whose colours he could confirm were smoke, light, shells, and shadows. He felt his body was like a black shadow in chains. He had to chase the strong light, or he would be destroyed. He held his cigarette between his middle and index fingers, inhaling the spicy energy of the tobacco, swallowing it deep into his throat. After a tour of his lungs, he exhaled it again. He hoped the tobacco would weaken—better yet, erase—the fear and timidity buried deep in his brain. That kind of fear often appears in those who seek utopia. An obsessive longing for a distant, blurred horizon. 這個午後，他正凝望著窗檯上那層薄薄的日光，邊吸著煙。他為數不多的可以確認顏色的東西：煙、光、貝殼、影子。他覺得自己的身體像是一個披上了鐐銬的黑影。他必須追尋強光，只有這樣才不會被消滅。他的煙夾在中指和食指間，吸進了菸草的辛辣能量，一直把這股能量深深吞進喉嚨裡。在肺裡繞了一圈後，再將它呼出來。他期盼著菸草能減弱，最好能去除深植於他腦子裡的那層恐懼與膽怯。那種恐懼，常常在一個追尋烏托邦之人身上顯現。對於遠方的迷離的痴狂。
When Arthur burst in, he was about to light another cigarette. Arthur's gaze first fell on him, then moved to something beside him. His eyes widened in that instant. Francis followed his gaze and realised he was looking at the portrait he had painted. The night before, he had taken it out again for a careful look and had forgotten to cover it. Arthur stepped forward, took the canvas in his hands. His eyes were sad and angry, as if pregnant with the judgment of a betrayer. After a moment, he put down the canvas and looked at Francis with a questioning gaze. 當亞瑟闖進來時，他正要點燃下一根煙。亞瑟的目光先是落在他身上，又落在他身邊。他在那一瞬間瞪大了雙眼。弗朗西斯順著他的視線望去，意識到他正看著自己為他而作的肖像畫。昨天晚上他又把它拿出來細細端詳，忘了蒙上蓋布。亞瑟向前走了幾步，將畫板拿在手裡，他的眼神悲傷而憤怒，彷彿孕育著對一個背信者的審判。過了一會兒，他放下畫板，對弗朗西斯報以探詢的目光。
"What is this?" 「這是怎麼回事？」
"Your mother asked me to paint your portrait, young master. For marriage." 「您母親讓我替您畫像，少爺。用於婚配。」
Francis had no intention of deceiving him. On the contrary, he was even curious about his reaction. What expression would those objectively so delicate eyes hold? The more expressions he could see on his face, the better. He was curious about him—he had never been curious about anyone before. After hearing his words, Arthur picked up the canvas again, frowned, and examined it more closely. 弗朗西斯沒有半點欺瞞他的意願與企圖。相反，他甚至對他的反應感到好奇。那雙在客觀上如此柔弱的眼睛將會是什麼神色？他的表情，對他而言見得越多越好。他對他感到好奇，此前他從未對誰感到過好奇。聽了他的話後，亞瑟重新拿起畫板，皺著眉頭，又更加仔細地端詳了一番。
"Does this painting move you? If it cannot move you, it cannot move me either." 「這幅畫能打動你嗎？如果他不能打動你，也就無法打動我。」
Whether it was Francis's imagination or not, when Arthur was angry, the grey mark near his eyes would quiver slightly, like a goldfish flicking its tail fin, swimming across his porcelain face. Francis looked into his eyes, his thoughts drifting away again in speculation about their colour. 不知是否是弗朗西斯的錯覺，亞瑟在發怒時，眼睛旁邊那塊灰色的印記會微微顫動，像一條金魚抖動著尾鰭，在他陶瓷般的臉上游動。弗朗西斯看著他的眼睛，又在對它顏色的猜測中思緒漸遠。
"You're right. It doesn't move me." 「您說得對。它無法打動我。」
Francis erased the face from the paper with an eraser. He picked up the broom by the wall and swept away the ash and eraser dust. After finishing, he reached out to the lush lemon mint growing on the windowsill, plucked a leaf, put it in his mouth, and crushed it with his teeth. The fresh fragrance charged through his nose and throat. He used this method to remove the smell of smoke. He stepped down from the windowsill, sat down on the sofa, and gestured for Arthur to sit beside him. After sitting down, Arthur rested his head on Francis's shoulder and closed his eyes, like a kitten dozing. The anger gradually faded from his brow, replaced by a contemplative look. 弗朗西斯用橡皮將畫紙上的臉擦去。他拿起牆邊的掃帚，將菸灰與橡皮掉下的灰一掃而淨。做完這一切之後，他將手伸向窗檯上茂盛生長著的檸檬薄荷，摘下一片薄荷葉放進嘴裡，讓牙齒磨碎它。清香在鼻腔和喉嚨裡橫衝直撞。他用這種方式去除煙味。他走下窗檯，在沙發上坐下，示意亞瑟坐在自己旁邊。亞瑟在坐下之後把頭擱在他的肩膀上，閉上眼睛，彷彿一隻小貓正在打盹。他的眉宇間怒氣漸散，取而代之的是一種沉思時特有的神色。
"Only, why is it a drawing? Everyone uses oil paintings for matchmaking now." 「只是，為什麼是素描。大家現在相親都用油畫了。」
"Because I cannot see." 「因為我看不見。」
"What did you say?" 「你說什麼？」
Arthur sat up straight at once, turning his head, trying to find an answer in Francis's face in advance. His pupils trembled slightly in Francis's colourless vision, like a cicada vibrating its wings on a spiderweb. Francis lowered his head, tucked the unlit cigarette behind his ear. He exhaled deeply, then raised his head, pouring a thousand words into those eyes full of mystery that looked back at him. He curved his lips in a self-deprecating smile. 亞瑟頃刻間坐直了身子，轉過頭，想在弗朗西斯的臉上提前尋找一個答案。他的瞳孔在他無色的視野裡輕輕顫抖，彷彿在蛛網上振翅的蟬。弗朗西斯低下頭，把沒點著的那支煙夾在耳朵後面。他深深呼出了口氣，又抬起頭，把千言萬語傾注在那雙正對著他的，充滿謎團的眼睛上。牽起嘴角，露出一個自嘲的微笑。
"I said, I cannot see colours. I have been like this since birth. I have no concept of what you call 'the sea is so blue.' I cannot see it. I don't know. I'm sorry." 「我說，我看不見顏色。從出生開始就這樣了。我沒有你們所說的，那種『海很藍』一類的概念。我看不見，我不知道。對不起。」
He lowered his head, lost in a long recollection of the past. Arthur was also silent. But after a moment, he suddenly reached out his hands and quietly closed them beside Francis's, which were much larger. His hands were small and cool. 他低下頭，陷入對過去的，長久的追憶裡。亞瑟也沉默著。但是，過了一會兒，他突然伸出雙手，在弗朗西斯那隻比他大很多的手旁邊悄然合攏。他的手又小又涼。
"Francis, look at me." 「弗朗西斯，你看看我。」
Francis turned to look at him. Arthur had not worn his forehead ornament for a long time. Now he raised a finger, pointing to his own eye. His fingertip rested on his skin, moving gently. 弗朗西斯轉頭看向他。亞瑟很久沒有戴額飾了。如今他伸出一根手指，指向自己的眼睛。指腹點在皮膚上，輕輕游移。
"I have this mark. It's purple. You might not know, but it's the same colour as your eyes." 「我有這個印記，它是紫色的，你可能不知道，但它和你的眼睛是一個顏色。」

"And what you just chewed—this is the same colour as my eyes." 「還有，你剛剛嚼的這個，跟我的眼睛是一個顏色。」
His tone was as emotionless as ever. After saying this, he immediately covered his ears and walked out. At the door, he turned back. 他的語氣一如既往，幾乎不帶任何感情。說完這些話之後，他馬上摀住自己的耳朵，走出門去。走到門扉時又回過頭來。
"If that portrait cannot move you, I won't let my mother pay you. And I'll kick you out." 「如果那幅畫打動不了你，我是不會讓母親給你付薪水的。而且，我還會把你趕出去。」
After a burst of birdsong, his figure disappeared into the dark, deep corridor. 一陣鳥叫聲後，他的身影消失在幽深黑暗的走廊深處。

So he made an unprecedented decision: to descend into the terrible underworld and beg Hades and Persephone to give Eurydice back to him. From Taenaron he entered the gates of the underworld. The shadows of the dead drifted eerily around him. But he strode past all the terrifying scenes of the kingdom of the dead, all the way to the ashen-faced thrones of Hades and Persephone. There, he took up his lyre and sang to the beautiful music: "O great rulers of the underworld, grant my plea, deign to listen to my wish! It is not curiosity that drives me down to visit the underworld, nor is it to seize the three-headed watchdog for amusement. I have come to your side for my beloved. She brought me joy and laughter for only a few days before she was bitten by a serpent, and her youth returned to the underworld. Behold, I must bear this immeasurable pain! As a man, I have struggled for many years, but love has torn my heart apart. I cannot live without Eurydice. I beg you, fearsome, sacred gods of the underworld. In this place of terror, in this silent wilderness of your domain: give her back to me! Give my beloved back to me! Give her freedom, let her prematurely withered life regain its youth! Without her, I can never return to the surface." 於是他作出了聞所未聞的決定：下到可怕的地府裡去，請求冥王哈迪斯和冥后珀耳塞福涅把歐律狄刻還給他。在泰納隆他從地府的入口走了下去。亡靈的影子陰森恐怖地飄浮在他的周圍。但他大步流星地從死人王國的種種恐懼場面中走過去，一直走到面無人色的冥王哈迪斯和冥后珀耳塞福涅的寶座前。在那裡，他操起七弦琴，隨著優美的琴聲唱道：「哦，冥界的偉大統治者啊，請恩准我的訴說，請賞臉傾聽我的願望！不是好奇心驅使我下來參觀冥界，也不是為了抓住三頭看門狗好玩。我是為了我的愛人來到你們的身旁。他給我帶來快樂和歡笑沒有幾天，就被毒蛇咬傷，她的青春年華便歸了陰間。瞧，我要承受這無法測度的痛苦呀！作為一個男人，我奮鬥了多年，但愛情撕碎了我的心，我不能沒有歐律狄克。我祈求你們，可怕的神聖的冥界之神。在這充滿恐怖的地方，在你們轄區的這片沉默的荒野：請你們把她，把我的愛人，還給我！還她自由，讓她過早凋零的生命重獲青春！如果不能這樣，那就把我也歸入亡靈的行列，沒有她我永遠也不能重返地面。」

He was not sure when love began. In this manor, in this world, he was an outsider. But Arthur was too unique to him. He observed him attentively; he was a small town scene, an ocean. There was something in him that Francis could not grasp—emptiness and light both present in his eyes, like two shards of a vase that could not be joined. He moved him, convinced him to paint his portrait. He even felt that it was Arthur's existence that made him a painter. And he could only control this adoration by creating art about him. One afternoon, they lay by the valley, sunbathing. Arthur's hand crept over, parting the withered yellow grass with a rustling sound. His little finger pressed against Francis's little finger and stayed there, as if that were enough to satisfy him. Francis chuckled softly, pressed his finger against the side of Arthur's, and slid his own between them. Their hands clasped tightly, like two facing pages in a book. Then he turned to look at Arthur, felt that the tips of his ears were somehow a different colour, and reached out to touch them. At the unexpected touch, Arthur flinched with closed eyes, then opened them again. Francis read annoyance in his eyes. Starting from his ear, his fingertips moved across his temple toward his eyes. His burning fingertips pressed against his eyelids, brushing his lashes. 他並不清楚，愛情開始的時刻。在這個莊園乃至這個世界，他是一個外人。只是，亞瑟對他而言太過獨特。他悉心觀察著他，他一整個人就是一小幅小鎮圖景，一片海洋。他的身上有他無法追尋的一些東西，空虛與光芒同時存在於他的眼中，像花瓶的兩塊碎片，無法拼合。他讓他動容，說服他為他作畫。他甚至覺得，是亞瑟的存在把他變成畫家。而他只能通過創造關於他的藝術來控制這種崇拜。某個下午，他們躺在山谷邊上曬太陽。亞瑟的手悄悄貼過來，撥開枯黃的草葉，一陣窸窸窣窣的摩擦聲。他的小指壓在他的小指上，不動了，彷彿這讓他心滿意足。弗朗西斯輕笑一聲，貼著他的指側，將手指擠過去。他的手緊緊扣住他的手，像一本書裡挨著的兩頁紙。接著，他扭頭看向亞瑟，感覺對方耳尖的顏色有些不一樣，便伸出手去觸碰。突如其來的觸碰使亞瑟閉著眼睛輕顫了一下，又睜開眼。他從他的眼睛裡讀出氣惱，指尖以他的耳廓為起點，擦過太陽穴，向著他的眼睛移動。他滾燙的指腹貼在他的眼皮上，輕挨著他的睫毛。
"You are beautiful, Arthur." 「你很漂亮，亞瑟。」
"Beautiful." 「漂亮。」
Arthur tilted his head, as if needing a moment to understand the implication of his words. 亞瑟的頭歪了一下，似乎需要一點時間來理解他話裡的隱含意義。
"Perhaps that expression isn't quite right. Arthur, you are... perfect." 「也許這種表達不太恰當。亞瑟，你很……完美。」
His fingertip was now touching the mark by his eye, as if solemnly touching a medal. Arthur's eyes darted uneasily a few times, then he turned his head away, covering his eyes with his arm. 他的指尖此時正在觸摸他眼邊那塊印記，彷彿鄭重地觸摸著他的勳章。亞瑟的眼睛不安地快速轉動了幾下，扭過頭去，用胳膊擋著眼睛。
"I know." 「我知道。」
Francis smiled and said no more. That night, and countless nights thereafter, Arthur sat in his room, before him. The moment their eyes met, they would both break into smiles. And Francis would pick up his brush and add details to the portrait. But that painting had lost its objectivity. The more he tried to paint him, the further he seemed to recede from him. In the cold, silent winter room, they sat facing each other in the flickering candlelight, touching his birthmark, observing a tear stain at the corner of his eye. A person so difficult to understand, looking at each other so candidly. Arthur sat there, needing no words, only wanting the emotions on the canvas to flow into each other's bodies. Soon, that face would be kissed by Francis. When he opened his eyes again, the birds would begin to sing once more, and his eyes would become a vast green ocean. 弗朗西斯笑了笑，沒再說話。這個夜晚，以及之後的無數個夜晚。亞瑟在他的房間裡，坐在他面前，目光相觸的瞬間，他們便都綻開微笑。而他拿著筆，在那幅肖像畫上添加細節。只是那張畫，它已經失去了客觀性。他越是想畫他，他反而離他越來越遠。冬日寒冷靜默的房間裡，他們在搖曳的燭火中對坐，輕觸他的胎記，觀察他眼角邊的一道淚痕。一個難以被他理解的人，他們就這樣率真地看著對方。亞瑟坐在那裡，不需要什麼語言，只想讓畫紙裡的情感流淌進彼此的身體裡。很快，這張臉就會被弗朗西斯親吻。當他再次睜開眼，鳥會重新開始鳴叫，他的眼睛變作一片綠色的汪洋。

The queen of the underworld said: Hades has granted your request. Saying this, she summoned Eurydice, who staggered to Orpheus's side. "Take her away," the queen said. "But remember, before you pass through the gates of the underworld, you must not look back at her. Only then will she be yours. If you look back too soon, she will never be yours." 冥后說道：冥王答應了你的祈求。說著，把歐律狄克招呼過來，歐律狄克搖搖晃晃地走到俄爾普斯的面前。「你把她帶走吧，」冥后說，「但你要記住，在你穿過冥界大門之前，絕不能回頭看她，她才屬於你。如果你過早地回頭看她，她就永遠不屬於你了。」

He began to use oil paints again—like a knight picking up his lance and shield, or a singer warming up his voice. At first, it felt awkward and unfamiliar. But after a while, the pigments came to life in his hands. At first, he dipped his brush in the pigment mixed with shell powder, letting it drip from the animal-hair tip onto the canvas. Later, he gradually no longer needed a brush; he would dip his fingers in the pigment and smear it casually on any blank space he saw on the canvas. Sometimes he applied too much force—his fingers would bleed. The blood mixed with the pigment. The portrait he was to deliver to Arthur's mother remained unfinished. He no longer knew how to paint him—this person he had only just met but still felt utterly unfamiliar with. Once he took him as inspiration, all creation became difficult. He only wanted to know him more, deeper, greedily turning everything about him into his own possession. Including the scar by his eye, like a butterfly wing breaking free from deep within his skin. But... but this life eventually angered his mother. She said she would find someone else. Now, this became his deepest fear. He had died of thirst on the road to find an oasis. 他重新使用起了油彩——就像一個騎士重新拿起長槍和圓盾，一個歌唱家重新開始吊嗓子一般。剛開始只覺得不適應和生疏。但一段時間後，顏料在他手中變得有了生命。最開始，他把含有貝殼粉末的顏料沾在畫筆上，讓顏料從死去動物的毛尖滴到畫布上。後來，他逐漸變得不再需要畫筆，他用手指蘸著顏料，將它隨意塗抹在他所見的，畫布上任一個空白的地方。有時他在畫布上施加了過大的力量——手指會流出血。血與顏料混合在一起。要交給亞瑟母親的那張肖像畫，他遲遲沒有完成。他已經不知道該如何畫他，畫這麼一個他剛剛認識，卻仍舊令他倍感陌生的人。一旦以他為靈感，所有的創作都變得困難。他只想更多地、更深地了解他，貪婪地把他的一切都變成自己的囊中之物。包括他眼睛周圍那塊瘢痕，彷彿從他肌膚深處破繭而出的蝴蝶翅膀。只是……只是這種生活最終招來他母親的憤怒。她說她將另請高明。現在，這成了他最深的恐懼，他渴死在了尋找綠洲的路上。
"But please, let me help him one last time. What if the next person has a loose tongue?" 「但是，請讓我幫他最後一次。萬一來人是個口舌不乾淨的呢。」
He pointed to his own eyes, hinting. The lady's raised head dipped slightly, then lifted again. He bowed, then took his toolbox and went to Arthur's room. A new forehead ornament had arrived. Arthur was holding it up to his face, comparing it in the mirror. Seeing Francis enter, a flash of panic and surprise crossed his eyes. Francis walked toward him in silence and sat down beside him. He opened his paint box and placed it before him. 他指了指自己的眼睛，做出暗示。他母親昂起的頭微微朝下點了幾寸，又抬起來。他鞠了一躬，旋即拿著工具箱來到亞瑟的房間。新的額飾訂好了，亞瑟正把它掛在臉上，對著鏡子比對。看到他進來，他的眼中閃過一絲慌亂和驚訝。弗朗西斯沉默不語地走向他，在他身邊坐下。他打開自己的顏料盒，放在他面前。
"Tell me, which one is the colour of your skin?" 「告訴我，哪個是你皮膚的顏色。」
He watched Arthur's expression shift from panic to anger, finally settling into silence. Those eyes returned to how they had been when they first met. He felt himself drowning again in those clear yet unfathomable eyes, forever imprisoned in that greyness, like a mime who cannot break character. In that instant, he seemed to see the colour of those eyes—waterweed from the beach, winding around him. 他眼看著亞瑟的眼神從慌亂，變得憤怒，最後又歸於沉默。那雙眼睛恢復他們初見時的樣子。他又感覺自己被溺在那雙澄碧如洗卻深不可測的眼睛中。永遠被禁錮在那片灰色裡，就像是一個無法從角色中抽離的啞劇演員。一瞬間，他似乎看到了，那雙眼睛的顏色，似海灘上的水草將他縈繞。
"What are you going to do?" 「你要做什麼？」
"Don't resist, Arthur." 「不要反抗，亞瑟。」
"Do you want me to resist?" 「你希望我反抗嗎？」
His sudden question made Francis pause. He lowered his head, struggling to distinguish the similar colours on his palette. 他突如其來的問句使弗朗西斯愣了愣神。他低下頭，在調色盤上努力分辨著那些差不多的色彩。
"This one." 「這個。」
"Thank you." 「謝謝。」
Francis held a brush in one hand and gently lifted Arthur's chin with the other, making him raise his head. Those eyes gazed deeply into his face. His first stroke landed at the corner of his eye, the edge of the birthmark. If only, at this moment, something could be recorded—unfading memories. How wonderful that would be. As the grey mark was slowly covered by pigment, it seemed as if a part of Arthur's self—a beating part of him he had handed over—was buried along with it. Tears flowed from his watercolour eyes, washing away some of the pigment, forcing him to start over repeatedly. Still, he could not tear his eyes away from that fading face. He knelt, holding Arthur's shoulders, silently adding brushstrokes to that face. After a while, he finished. In his eyes, Arthur's face had become a uniform, greyish white. That face felt strange to him. But he could not suppress his feelings. Like a bird that cannot refuse to return to its nest, he leaned down and pressed his lips to that face's lips. Like a kiss between two statues. Now, those eyes—hidden behind the oil paint—stared at him intently, then reacted with shock. 弗朗西斯一隻手握著畫筆，另一隻手輕輕捏著亞瑟的下巴，使他抬起頭來。那雙眼睛深深凝望著他的臉。第一筆畫在他眼角，那裡是胎記的邊緣。假使有可能，在這一刻，一些東西能被記下來——永不褪色的記憶。那該多好。當那塊灰色的印記慢慢被顏料蓋住，彷彿亞瑟的一部分自我，他交到他手裡的一部分跳動著的自我，也跟著被掩埋了。他睜開的水彩雙眼裡流出淚水，沖掉了一部分顏料，使他不得不不斷重新開始。他仍舊無法把眼睛從那張不斷消失著的臉上轉移。他跪著，按住了他的肩膀，一言不發地將筆觸添加在那張臉上。過了一會兒，他完成了。亞瑟的臉在他看來變成了一模一樣的，灰白色。這張臉使他感到陌生。但是，他無法按捺住自己的心情。就像一隻鳥無法拒絕回到巢窠。他俯下身子，將嘴唇壓在這張臉的嘴唇上。彷彿兩尊雕像間的親吻。現在，那雙眼睛，藏匿於油彩後的那雙眼睛，緊緊注視著他，然後做出錯愕的反應。
"I... take care..." 「我…保重……」
He took his toolbox and stepped across the threshold toward the stairs. 他拿起工具箱，跨過門扉，走向台階。
"Francis, you are no different from me." 「弗朗西斯，你與我並非不同。」
He did not react. 他沒有反應。
"Turn around." 「回頭吧。」
Driven by intense curiosity, Francis turned his head and collided with those eyes throbbing with pain. He saw them—deep green eyes bleeding. This was no one else's memory; it was his instinct seeing, those eyes opening inside his own mind. Arthur stood at the top of the stairs, the half of his face Francis had restored hidden in shadow. He wore his robe, his elbows resting on the stair railing. Like a frozen bird perched on a cold branch, about to fall. 懷著極度的好奇，弗朗西斯轉過頭，撞進那雙疼得正在跳動的眼睛裡。他看見了，流著血的深碧眼睛。這不是任何人的記憶，而是他的本能在看，那雙眼睛正在他的腦子裡睜開。亞瑟站在樓梯頂端，被他修好的那一半臉隱沒在陰影裡。他穿著那件睡袍，手肘搭在樓梯扶手上。彷彿寒冷樹枝上站著的，一隻搖搖欲墜的，凍僵的鳥。

Orpheus, with his beloved, hurried silently up the terrifying, dark path shrouded in night. Suddenly, an indescribable longing arose in Orpheus's heart: he secretly tilted his ear, trying to hear the sound of his beloved's breath or the rustle of her dress. He heard nothing. All around him was deathly silence. Overcome by fear and love, unable to control himself, he boldly glanced back. Ah, what misfortune! In that instant, Eurydice's eyes, full of sorrow and tenderness, stared at him fixedly as she drifted back into that terrifying abyss. In utter despair, he stretched out his arms toward the fading Eurydice. It was no use! She had died a second death, without complaint—if she could complain, it could only be that their love had been too deep. She had already vanished from Orpheus's sight. "Farewell, farewell!" came a faint, weak voice from afar, gradually fading into the depths of the underworld. 俄爾普斯帶著他的愛人，默默地快步沿著籠罩夜色的恐怖的黑暗的路上向上攀登。俄爾普斯心裡突然產生了一種無法形容的渴望：他偷偷側耳試了試，看能不能聽到他愛人的呼吸或她裙裾的聲音，結果什麼也聽不到，他周圍的一切是死一般的寂靜。他被恐懼和愛情所壓倒，無法控制自己，就壯著膽子迅急朝後看了一眼。啊，真不幸呀！就在這時，歐律狄克兩隻充滿悲哀和柔情的眼睛死死地盯著他，飄然墜回那令人毛骨悚然的深淵。他無比絕望地把手臂伸向漸漸消失的歐律狄克。一點用處也沒有！她又遭遇了第二次死，沒有哀怨，——假如他能抱怨的話，那也只能是她們的愛實在太深了。她已經在俄爾普斯的視線中消失了。「再見，再見了！」從遠方傳來這樣低沉微弱的聲音，漸漸地消失在冥界的深處。

In Breton historical memory, it symbolises power, foreign rule, and the myth of aristocratic bloodlines. ③ 金雀花王朝（House of Plantagenet）是12至15世紀統治英格蘭的王室，亦稱安茹王朝。該家族源於法國安茹伯爵若弗魯瓦五世，1154年亨利二世加冕後建立跨海峽的安茹帝國，版圖涵蓋英格蘭、諾曼底及阿基坦等地。王朝八位正式君主統治至1399年，其後分裂為蘭開斯特和約克兩系，最終因玫瑰戰爭導致統治終結。在布列塔尼歷史記憶中象徵權力、外來統治、貴族血統神話。
④ Eleanor, 5th Countess of Richmond (1184 – 10 August 1241), was the eldest daughter of Geoffrey II (son of King Henry II of England) and Constance, Duchess of Brittany. Renowned for her beauty, she was called the Fair Maid of Brittany, the Damsel of Brittany, the Pearl of Brittany, or the Beauty of Brittany. As the heiress to vast territories including England, Anjou, and Aquitaine under non-Salic law from 1203, Eleanor posed a threat to the thrones of her uncle King John and her cousin King Henry III. She was imprisoned from 1202, becoming the longest-imprisoned English royal. Because she was a prisoner, she could not inherit the Duchy of Brittany. Like Empress Matilda and later Elizabeth of York, Eleanor's claim to the English throne received almost no support from the English barons, even though there were legal provisions for a female monarch, kings were expected to be male. Some consider her imprisonment to be "King John's most unjust act." ④ 埃莉諾，第五代里奇蒙女伯爵（1184年－1241年8月10日），是英格蘭國王亨利二世的第四子若弗魯瓦二世（英語作喬弗雷）和布列塔尼女公爵康斯坦絲的長女，因美貌出眾而被稱作布列塔尼的美少女（Fair Maid）、布列塔尼的少女（Damsel）、布列塔尼的珍珠或布列塔尼的美人。作為自1203年起在非薩利克法下包括英格蘭、安茹、阿基坦在內的廣大領地的女繼承人，埃莉諾是對叔父無地王約翰和堂弟亨利三世兩位國王王位的威脅，故她自1202年起被囚，成為被囚最久的英格蘭王室成員。因淪為囚徒，她也無法繼承布列塔尼公國。和馬蒂爾達皇后和之後的約克的伊麗莎白一樣，埃莉諾對英格蘭王位的權利幾乎得不到英格蘭諸侯的支持，即使有關於女性君主的合法條款，君主卻都被期望是男性。一些人認為她被囚是「約翰王最無理的行為」。
