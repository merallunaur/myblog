Nine 九

◇
Panem nostrum supersubstantialem da nobis hodie.② Panem nostrum supersubstantialem da nobis hodie.②
Grant us this day our supersubstantial bread. 祈求上帝賜予我們每日維生的麵包。②
◇
I once witnessed an execution. Of course, not on television or in any newspaper—the children of the Capitol can no longer see such horrors; they are well protected. It was at the end of the Dark Days,③ when the rebellion of the thirteen districts was about to be crushed. The Capitol began to try and execute the rebels from the districts. I remember pushing through the crowd, the strong smell of vomit filling my nostrils, and a wall of thighs blocking my view. I could barely see anything. That day, the rebels from District 9 were executed—thirteen people, the youngest just twenty, men and women, hanged together. Hanging, they say, the moment the condemned's head is looped into the noose, they feel a rush of heat to the brain, then their consciousness fades, as if drowning. Seconds later, the suspended body begins to convulse—the arms flailing, like a drowning person's desperate struggle, the feet walking in the air, swinging incessantly. Soon after, the hands and feet begin to twitch, then the whole body stiffens and trembles, like a fish on a cutting board. After the convulsions, they enter a state of suspended animation—the eyes bulge, breathing stops. At that point, the heart is still beating. It continues to beat for about ten minutes, and then death arrives. Some of the condemned had weights tied to their ankles, intended to break their cervical vertebrae instantly with the force of the fall. 我曾目睹過一場處決。當然，不可能是在電視，或是什麼報紙上，都城的孩子如今看不到這些慘象，他們被保護得好好的。那是在「黑暗時代」末期③，13個區的叛亂將要終結時。都城開始對各區的叛軍加以審判和處決。我記得穿過人群，鑽入我鼻腔的濃烈嘔吐物氣味和擋在我眼前的成片的大腿。我幾乎什麼也看不見。那天，被執行的是第九區的叛軍，十三個人，最年輕的二十歲，有男有女，被同時絞死。絞刑，據說在被執行人腦袋被掛上去的一剎那，他們會感到頭腦發熱，接著意識模糊，彷彿溺水。幾秒後，被吊起的身體就會出現痙攣反應——手臂的揮舞，就像溺水的人的奮力掙扎，腳部則像在走路，不停地擺動著。過後不久，雙手雙腳開始抽動，緊跟著全身會挺直起來，顫動著，如同砧板上的魚。痙攣反應後，就進入了假死狀態——眼球突起、呼吸停止。這時候，仍有心跳。心臟還會持續跳動一陣子——大約十分鐘的樣子，接著死亡才會降臨。有的被執行人腳踝上還拴著重物，那是為了藉助墜落的衝擊力，在一瞬間折斷他們的頸椎。
All of this exists in my imagination. I was too short to see the gallows. But nevertheless, I witnessed an execution. 這些都存在於我的想像中，我太矮了，看不見行刑台。但是，總之，我目睹了一場處決。
After the Capitol quelled the rebellion, my father became a hero of the war. The first Hunger Games① were designed by him. But he only served as designer for that single Games before being reassigned to command and train the military of District 2. My mother and I lived in the Capitol until the second coup. My mother was a designer. I loved to sing. By the time I fell in love with singing, the Dark Days had already ended, and I could sing freely, my voice ringing to the heavens, without fear of being discovered by anyone. Many children who died in the war did not have such good fortune. 我父親在都城平定叛亂後成為了功臣，第一屆飢餓遊戲①便由他設計。不過他只當了這一屆設計師，便被調去指揮和訓練第二區的軍隊。我和母親一直住在都城，直到發生第二次政變。母親是一位設計師，我喜歡唱歌。在我愛上唱歌時，黑暗時代已經結束了，我得以自由地歌唱，音量響徹雲霄，不用擔心什麼人發現我。很多戰爭中死去的孩子沒有這種好運氣。
The first Hunger Games took place when I was eight. As the inaugural Games, they were more magnificent than any I remember. At that time, each district's tribute had a sponsor—a rule that was gradually abolished later; I don't know if it was ever reinstated. On the day of the reaping, I was at home helping my mother cut fabric. The effects of the war still lingered. The school I had once attended was now a ruin, with only wild cats scavenging among the rubble. The education system had not yet been fully restored, so I, like other Capitol children, stayed at home, waiting for the day when light would return. On the crackling television screen, the tributes' blurred expressions flickered in the static. From District 1 to District 12, as the numbers grew larger, their clothes and faces grew shabbier. That was because all their strength had been exhausted from the moment of their birth. In the years to come, even if they did not have to participate in the Hunger Games, they would still have to labour until death. Now they had turned from numbers into people, yet they still seemed like numbers—lifeless. I watched the numbers roll slowly. When it reached District 9, my mother raised the needle in her hand and pointed it at the screen. That child would come to live in our house. I raised my head. My eyes were pulled from near to far, aching and stinging. That child looked about eighteen, much older than me. He held his head high, his bright, wheat-golden hair falling in front of his face, hiding his expression. I could feel the rage surging beneath his powerful body. His hands were bound in shackles, his wrists bleeding. 第一屆飢餓遊戲進行時我八歲。由於是首屆舉辦，那屆飢餓遊戲比我記憶中的任何一屆都更加隆重。那時，每個區的選手都會有一名贊助商，這項規則後來慢慢被廢止了，是否被重新提起，我便不得而知。選手抽籤的那天，我在家裡幫助母親剪裁布料。戰爭的影響依然存在。我曾經上學的那座學校如今淪為廢墟，只有野貓在上面覓食。教育系統尚未全面復原，我和其他都城孩子都待在家裡，等待光明到來的那一天。滋滋作響的電視屏幕上，選手模糊的表情在電流聲中搖晃。從第一區到第十二區，隨著數字變大，他們的衣服和臉孔愈加破舊。那是因為在他們出生時，所有的力氣便已被耗盡。今後的數年，即使不參加飢餓遊戲，他們也不得不勞作致死。如今他們從數字變成人，卻彷彿還是數字，沒有生命。我看著數字慢慢滾動，到第九區時，母親舉起手中的針指向電視，那個孩子將住進我們家來。我抬起頭，眼球由近到遠被拉扯，發疼發酸。那個孩子看起來十八歲了，比我大上很多。他昂著頭，如麥穗般的亮金色長髮擋在臉前，看不清表情。我能感受到他那充滿力量的軀體下湧動著的憤怒，他的雙手戴著鐐銬，兩腕滲血。
"Francis Bonnefoy." 「弗朗西斯·波諾弗瓦。」
He walked onto the stage, raised his head toward the camera, and revealed a pair of violet eyes, silent and chilling. His eyelashes trembled irregularly, like a halting, choked sob. I remembered that surname. That day at the execution ground, Bonnefoy—his father had been among them, likely lying somewhere among the Capitol's piles of corpses. I had not seen the face of any of the executed, but he must have looked very much like his father. I tried to watch further, but could not. He threw his head toward the camera. A sharp sound of shattering glass, the crack of whips through the air, two or three seconds of black screen. Then the reaping for District 10 began to play. I looked at my mother. She lowered her head, as if she had never raised it. 他走到台上來了，朝著攝像機抬起頭，一雙沉默得令人膽寒的紫羅蘭色眼睛。睫毛不規律地微微顫動，似某種斷斷續續的哽咽。我對這個姓氏留有記憶。那天在處刑場上，波諾弗瓦，他的父親位列其中，很可能在都城的某堆屍體裡。我沒見過任何一個被執行者的臉，但他一定很像他的父親。我試圖接著觀察，但難以繼續。他一頭撞向鏡頭。一聲玻璃碎裂的脆響，幾聲鞭子揮過空氣的響聲，兩秒三秒的黑屏。接著第十區的抽籤開始播放。我看向母親，她低下頭，彷彿從未抬起過。
◇
I held my mother's hand as I watched Francis step off the train. He was wearing the same clothes as on reaping day—a flat cap, a linen shirt, and a pair of khaki trousers that were too short. Nine.④ The number that ends every sequence. The number that signifies transcendence of personal gain and loss, the number of selfless love and sacrifice. The number that represents ending, culmination, and the cycle of life. District 9 was responsible for grain production and processing, one of the poorest districts. Half the people worked the fields, the other half in factories. It was harvest season, and everyone had to labour more than twelve hours a day. Some died of pneumonia, others from pesticide poisoning. Because they were so far from the Capitol, they received almost no protection or medical care. After the farm or factory work ended, they slept in huts on the ridges, their porches painted Haint Blue.⑤ An ancient legend, meant to mimic the sky or water, so that ghosts could not pass through. I had never seen such huts with my own eyes. When my mother and I still lived in the Capitol, we stayed in a three-story house. At night, the outer coating of the walls would glow blood-red, so that even if I played outside too late, I would never lose my way. I waved to Francis, but was almost ignored. He held his head high, staring straight ahead. Just as he was about to pass me, I grabbed his sleeve—the patch soaked in bloodstains. He had only left his district a few days ago, but his identity still clung to him. Family of rebels. He had not been chosen by lot, but pushed into this position. District 9 had only one tribute left; the other girl had committed suicide on the train. I saw them carry down her body. She had smothered herself with her skirt, her cheeks turned livid. 我握著母親的手，看著弗朗西斯走下火車。他穿著跟抽籤那天一樣的衣服，一件平頂帽，亞麻襯衫和短了一截的咔嘰布褲子。九④，這個每一列序數結尾的數字。代表著超越個人得失喜樂，大愛的奉獻的數字。代表著結束、最高以及生命輪迴的數字。第九區負責穀物生產和加工，是最為貧窮的一個區之一。一半的人在田間耕種，另一半在工廠裡。此時正值收穫的季節，每個人不得不進行每日超過十二小時的勞作。他們當中有的人死於肺炎，有的人死於農藥中毒。因為離都城太遠，幾乎得不到防護和醫治。農耕或是工廠生活結束後，他們便睡在地壟上的棚屋裡。門廊漆成海特藍⑤。那是幾百年前的古老傳說，為了模仿天空或是水，使鬼魂無法穿過。我此前從未親眼見過過這種棚屋，我和母親還在都城時，住在一座三層洋樓裡。一到晚上，牆面的外層塗料會發出如血一般的紅光。這樣，哪怕我在外面玩得太晚，也永遠不會迷路。我向弗朗西斯招手，幾乎遭到了無視。他昂著頭，目視前方。在他就要經過我身邊時我拉住了他的袖口，補丁浸滿血漬。他離開故鄉不過幾天，身份仍附著其上。叛軍的家眷，他並非通過抽籤，而是被推上這個位置。第九區僅餘一個參賽者，另一個女孩在來時的火車上自殺了。我看見有人抬下了她的屍體，她用裙擺悶死了自己，臉頰變成紺色。
"You have to come with us." 「你得跟我們走。」
I looked up at him, reading mockery and scorn in his eyes. A stranger with sharp violet eyes, wearing a layer of wheat fragrance mixed with the smell of rust. He curled his lips, reaching his hand toward my face, the wounds on his wrists impossible to hide. 我仰頭看著他，從他眼睛裡讀出嘲諷和譏笑。一個有著銳利紫色眼睛的異鄉人，他穿著一層混合著鐵鏽味的麥香。他勾起唇角，手伸向我的臉，手腕上的傷痕無處可遁。
"Sponsor?" 「贊助商？」
My mother nodded silently, holding my hand tightly as she fled the dusty platform, unconcerned whether he could follow. I turned my head. The space between us had already been filled by the crowd. I stood on tiptoe, trying to find him among the children dressed in similar clothes. His sly smile and whistle, suspended above the crowd, now fluttered like a bird's thin wings, beating weakly in the sulphur-tinged air of the Capitol. The moment I turned, he suddenly quickened his pace, shoved something into my palm, then lowered his eyes and fell back behind us. It was a hard, angular object. It felt like holding a sharp, narrow blade in my hand. It did not cut my skin, but it came close. 我的母親無言地點了點頭，緊緊拉著我的手，逃出塵土飛揚的站台，不在意他是否能跟上。我扭過頭去，我們中間的空隙已被人群填滿。我努力踮著腳，從一眾衣著打扮相似的孩子中找尋他的身影。他那懸吊在人群上方的狡猾微笑和口哨聲，此刻正如鳥的纖薄羽翼，在都城充斥著硫磺氣息的中無力拍打。我轉頭的剎那間，他突然快走幾步，將什麼東西塞進我的手心裡，旋即低垂著眉眼退到我們身後。那是個硬梆梆的，有稜角的東西。我的手中如同握著一把尖銳的窄刀片。它沒有刺破我的皮膚，但是已經快了。
He was placed in the basement, which had once been the servants' quarters, but most of them had died of the flu, and the rest had been dismissed. Few had survived the Dark Days. The first thing he had to do was bathe. He took off his shirt and walked into the bathroom. I would gaze at his back, the muscles covered in whip marks, old scars never fading before new wounds were added. He had to avoid those ghastly wounds while washing, as if avoiding monsters clinging to his back. Infection was deadly at any time. As he did all this, I stood beside him, separated only by a fogged glass door. I took the thing he had given me out of my pocket and held it in my palm. It was a medal engraved with the emblem of Panem—wheat, blades, and a soaring griffin.⑥ I turned it over to observe the small, worn indentations on the reverse side. 他被安置在地下室裡，那裡曾是傭人住的地方，只不過他們大部分死於流感，另一部分被遣散了。幾乎沒人挺得過黑暗時代。他要做的第一件事是洗澡，脫下襯衫走入浴室。我會凝視他的脊背，肌肉上佈滿鞭痕，甚至舊傷疤從未消減便又添新傷。他不得不在沖洗時避開那些猙獰的傷口，宛如避開附生在他脊背上的怪物。感染在任何時候都是致命的。他做著這一切時，我站在他身邊，只隔著一扇水汽氤氳的玻璃門。我將他塞給我的東西從口袋裡掏出來，擺在手心裡。那是一枚雕刻著施惠國國徽的勳章。麥穗、利刃與展翅高飛的獅鷲⑥。我將它翻過來，以觀察背面已被磨平的細小凹痕。
"District 9." 「第九區。」
"Bonnefoy." 「波諾弗瓦。」
His father's name. As I was deep in thought, he came out of the bathroom, the scent of wheat rushing toward me. Seeing me standing at the door, he crouched down to my eye level and gave my cheek a hard pinch. 他父親的名字。他在我沉思時走出浴室，麥穗的香氣撲面而來。看到我站在門口，他蹲下身子與我平視，在我的臉頰上重重一捏。
"What are you doing here, little one?" 「小不點，你在這裡幹什麼？」
"My name isn't 'little one.' It's Arthur Kirkland." 「我不叫小不點，我叫亞瑟·柯克蘭。」
"Alright, Arthur. What are you doing here?" 「好吧，亞瑟，你在這裡幹什麼？」
"Mother is afraid you'll run away. She wants me to keep an eye on you." 「母親怕你逃走，要我看著你。」
A nearly exaggerated smile spread across his wheat-coloured face, the corners of his eyes sharply upturned, his pupils shaped like crescent moons. His gaze wandered and rolled over my face, like a peal of mocking laughter. His irises were silent as inorganic matter. In the cage of muscles and capillaries, two birds bared their sharp beaks, preening their violet tail feathers. His gaze, flowing like cold water, poured into my eyes. The birds screamed as they lunged at me. My eyes ached, as if they had just been gouged out. I covered my eyes, shaking my head from side to side, trying to shake off the phantom pain. 他那接近小麥色的臉上泛起一個近乎誇張的微笑，高高翹起的眼尾，彎月般的眼瞳。他的目光在我臉上游移翻卷，如一陣譏諷的笑聲。無機質般沉默的虹膜。肌肉和毛細組成的牢籠裡，兩隻鳥亮出鋒利的喙，細細梳理它們紫羅蘭色的尾羽。他那冷水般不斷流淌著的目光旋即灌進我的眼睛。鳥尖叫著向我撲來，我的眼睛感到疼痛，彷彿眼球剛剛被剜去。我捂著眼睛，左右搖晃腦袋，試圖甩開那陣幻痛。
"I won't run away, Arthur." 「我不會逃走的，亞瑟。」
He spoke close to my ear, his voice both soothing and coaxing. At that moment, my mother came in and led him away. She would design his costume for the parade, and now she needed to take his measurements. This was the first Games; the Capitol's majesty and prosperity had to be displayed. He lay on the table like a bow drawn taut. Many people walked around him—my mother's assistants. From then on, all I remembered again was the wall of thighs blocking my view. I was too small to see the scene of him being deconstructed and reconstructed like a corpse. I only knew that the costume would be beautiful. 他貼著我的耳朵，既似安撫，又似誘哄。我的母親在這時走進來將他帶離。她將為他設計參與遊行的衣服，此刻要他去量尺寸。這是第一屆遊戲，都城的威嚴和繁榮必須體現。他躺在桌子上，像一張蓄勢待發的弓。很多人在他身邊走來走去，那是母親的助手。自此，我的記憶中又只有擋在我眼前的成片的大腿。我太小了，看不見他如屍體般被解構和再造的場景。我只知道，那件衣服會很漂亮。
◇
The costume my mother made was of thick, pleated brown cotton, like torn corrugated cardboard. The upper body was strung with crystals of decreasing size, like raindrops falling from him. In addition, he had to wear a corset. The night before the parade, I sneaked down to the basement, carrying ointment. The smell of dust and mould made my head heavy and drowsy. He lay on his side on the bed, facing the wall. I opened my mouth to call him, but he woke up before I could touch him, instinctively reaching under his pillow. If this were still the Dark Days, his knife would be there, ready for Peacekeepers⑦ bursting into the room at any moment. A keen sentinel in the darkness, his deep purple irises trembled slightly, like detuned sunlight, shimmering. 母親所製的這件衣服，厚重打褶的棕色棉布，如同被撕碎的瓦楞紙。上身串著由大到小的水晶，宛如雨滴正從他身上掉落。除此之外，他還必須要穿束腰。遊行的前一天晚上，我悄悄溜進地下室，帶著藥膏。灰塵和黴菌的氣味讓我的頭腦如醉般昏沉。他側躺在床上，面對著牆壁。我張開嘴想要叫他，他卻在我的手觸碰他之前就醒來，下意識摸向枕頭底下。如果還在黑暗時代，他的刀就放在這裡，以應對隨時衝進房間裡的維和軍⑦。一個在黑暗中的敏銳哨兵，他深紫色的虹膜微微顫晃著，如同變調的日光，熠熠閃爍。
"What are you doing here?" 「你來幹什麼？」
I did not answer, pointing to his wrists and back. He grabbed the hand holding the ointment—a habit he had developed during the war. These past few days, he had barely been allowed to eat, so that my mother's clothes would look even better. Even now, his strength could still easily smother me in this room. In the war years, on any given night, the murder of children took place throughout the twelve districts. They were unarmed, sacrificed to politics. Some held family photographs in their hands. Now, he snatched the thing from me, opened the lid, and coughed, choked by the herbal smoke. 我沒有回答，指指他的手腕和後背。他抓住我拿著藥膏的那隻手，這是他在戰爭中養成的習慣。這幾天，他幾乎不被允許吃飯，這樣，母親的衣服就會看起來更漂亮了。他如今的力氣依舊可以輕而易舉地把我悶死在這個房間裡。在戰爭年代，任何一個這樣的夜晚，對於孩童的謀殺都在十二個區的各處進行。他們手無寸鐵，成為政治的犧牲品，有人手中握著家庭合影。而此刻，他奪走我手裡的東西，打開蓋子，被草藥的熏煙嗆得一陣咳嗽。
"Turn around." 「轉過身去。」
He gave me a deep look, then turned his back to me. The whip marks had sapped part of his strength, exposing his fragile wounds and skeleton to me. I gazed at the red, bleeding scars. Scenes of flogging and execution had rarely reached the Capitol since the rebellion was quelled, but one of the wounds had a thorn embedded in it, about a knuckle long. I carefully dipped my finger into the ointment, spread it on the back of my hand, warmed it with my body heat, and then applied it to his back. His whole body trembled, a muffled groan escaping him. His tall body tightened against my shoulder, making me bear part of his weight. He was indeed in terrible pain; sweat beaded on his smooth forehead. It was years before I saw that Games. When the first Games took place, I was too young to be allowed to watch any bloody scenes. Political murder never leaves traces. 直到經過長久的廝殺和搏鬥，大概過去了兩三天，錄影帶中這些時間被剪掉了。只剩下了兩個孩子，他和一區的男孩。他那曬成小麥色的臉上浮出一絲惡作劇的笑容，酒窩若隱若現。這時，一個可怕的爆裂聲響起，蓋住了音樂聲，競技場的吊燈越來越暗，火苗在顫動。第一區的孩子尖叫起來，並不知道自己在叫些什麼，然後向競技場外面跑去。一邊跑，一邊口述著自己的叛國罪行，他的父親曾被拉去拷問過，幾乎要被流放了。他被那些記憶嚇得發狂，不過，在接近競技場邊緣時，有人開槍打死了他。我看向弗朗西斯，他瞇著一隻眼，手槍的槍口冒著煙。他活下來了。我驚喜地跳起來大叫，直到我聽見第二聲槍響。他的身體，從高地上落下來，像一隻被箭射傷的鳥兒，自由地墜落。那把槍從他的手中脫去。不知道他是否感應到了什麼，他看向鏡頭，那雙飽含著無限憤怒的紫色瞳孔，驟然收縮，接著四處響起的豐饒號角聲中，靜默地渙散。他在笑。這即是第一屆飢餓遊戲的結局。沒有勝者。我甚至沒有見過他的屍體。政治性的謀殺向來不留痕跡。

① The Hunger Games: A national punishment and control system established in Panem after the Dark Days. Its origin was to punish the districts for their rebellion against the Capitol, maintaining political order through annual public violence. The system requires each district to select one male and one female tribute between the ages of 12 and 18 by public lottery, who are then placed in a man-made arena controlled by the Capitol. They must engage in a survival competition broadcast nationwide until only one survivor remains. Any form of killing and alliance is permitted during the Games. The arena environment and events are constantly manipulated by Gamemakers to ensure conflict and viewing appeal. Spectators can send supplies to tributes through a sponsorship system, linking survival to public image. The victor receives material privileges and is shaped into part of the national narrative, while the deaths of the fallen are ritualised as a collective warning to the districts, giving the Hunger Games the triple function of punishment, deterrence, and entertainment. ① 本篇設定為飢餓遊戲，施惠國在「黑暗時代」後建立的一項國家級懲罰與統治制度，其起源旨在懲戒各區對都城的叛亂，並通過年度重複的公開暴力維持政治秩序。制度規定，每年由每個區通過公開抽籤選出一名12至18歲的男性與女性作為「貢品」，被送入由都城控制的人工競技場，在全國直播下進行生存競賽，直至僅剩一名倖存者。比賽過程中允許任何形式的殺戮與結盟，競技場環境與事件由遊戲製作人隨時操控，以確保衝突與觀看效果；觀眾可通過贊助系統向貢品投送物資，使生存與公眾形象直接掛鉤。勝者將獲得物質特權並被塑造成國家敘事的一部分，而失敗者的死亡則被儀式化為對各區持續有效的集體警告，從而使飢餓遊戲同時承擔懲罰、威懾與娛樂三重功能。
② Panem nostrum supersubstantialem da nobis hodie. Latin, from the Lord's Prayer. Literally "Grant us today our supersubstantial bread." In theology, "supersubstantialem" refers both to daily necessity and spiritual sustenance. "Panem" is also the etymological root of the nation Panem in The Hunger Games (Latin for "bread"). ② Panem nostrum supersubstantialem da nobis hodie.拉丁文，出自《主禱文》。直譯為「賜給我們今日超實質的麵包」，其中 supersubstantialem 在神學中既指日用之需，也指屬靈的糧。「Panem」亦為《飢餓遊戲》中國家 Panem 的詞源（拉丁語「麵包」）。
③ The Dark Days: In The Hunger Games universe, the period of war after the thirteen districts rebelled against the Capitol. This period was characterised by public executions, collective punishment, and military repression. District 9 was responsible for grain production and processing, remaining in extreme poverty for a long time, bearing the burden of basic survival supplies while lacking political voice. ③ 黑暗時代（The Dark Days），《飢餓遊戲》世界觀中指十三個區反抗都城失敗後的戰爭時期。該時期以公開處刑、集體懲罰、軍事鎮壓為特徵。第九區負責穀物生產和加工，長期處於極端貧困狀態，承擔基礎生存物資卻缺乏話語權。
④ "9" in many cultures represents ending, completion, cycle, and sacrifice. In Christian mysticism, nine is the number of the angelic hierarchy. ④ 「9」在多種文化中代表終結、完成、循環與獻祭。在基督教神秘主義中，九為天使階級之數。
⑤ Haint Blue: Derived from Southern American folk tradition, porches or huts painted light blue to ward off ghosts (haints). ⑤ 海特藍（Haint Blue）源自美國南方民間傳統，門廊或棚屋漆成淺藍色，用以驅趕幽靈（haint）。
