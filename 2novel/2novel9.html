The farthest the plane could go was Ushuaia.② To reach Antarctica, one had to take a ferry operated by local fishermen. Summer in this hemisphere ended hastily, and the waters around Cape Horn③ entered a long fishing moratorium. In their spare time, most fishermen made extra money by ferrying travellers to Antarctica. A few headed to the northern hemisphere to spend another summer. The rest crossed the mountains to help build the north-south railway in Chile. Under the relentless blast of polar easterlies, these fishermen developed fiery cheeks, thick beards, and rough skin. They were more taciturn than anyone else. They never asked about any traveller's past, nor were they interested—like a silent, barnacle-covered reef. Unlike fishing, passenger transport was a job that required constant alertness, so there was no Torrontés④ on the boat. 飛機最遠只能到達烏斯懷亞②，要前往南極洲，則需要坐當地漁民所運營的擺渡船。這個半球的夏季匆匆終結，合恩角③附近的海域進入長久的休漁期。閒暇時光裡，大多數漁民靠將旅行者送往南極賺取外快，少部分前往北半球，再次度過一個夏天。剩下的則翻山越嶺，協助智利南北鐵路的修建。在極地凜冽東風不間斷的吹打中，這些漁民變得雙頰如火、鬍鬚茂盛、皮膚粗糙。他們比任何人都更加少言寡語。對於每一個旅行者的過去，他們從不過問，也不感興趣，如同一塊佈滿藤壺的、沉默的礁石。相對於打魚，客運是一項需要時刻保持清醒的工作，因此現在船上沒有特濃情④。
"Arthur Kirkland. Travelling." 「亞瑟·柯克蘭。旅行。」
He arrived in early April, leaping from the warm air of the northern hemisphere into the polar night. After presenting his dark blue passport for customs clearance, a tall fisherman approached him, reaching out to take his suitcase. He refused. This weather-beaten, battered case contained almost nothing—just a few clothes and a diary. 他在四月初抵達，自北半球溫暖的空氣，縱身一躍進極夜之中。向邊檢出示了深藍色的護照清關後，一個高大的漁民向他走來，伸出手想要接過他手中的手提箱，他拒絕了。這個久經風霜、磨損嚴重的箱子裡沒有什麼東西，除了幾件衣服和一本日記。
"Where are you going?" 「您去哪兒？」
"Near Vinson."⑤ 「文森山⑤附近。」
Even though he could barely make out the fisherman's heavily accented English, Arthur knew the question was merely polite. Sure enough, the man nodded and fell silent. They walked forward. As he stepped onto the rickety whaling boat, he once again declined the fisherman's outstretched hand. 即使聽不清對方夾雜著晦澀口音的難懂英語，亞瑟也能知道對方僅因禮貌發問。果然，對方點頭後便不再說話，他們向前走去。在踏上那艘搖搖晃晃的捕鯨船時，他再一次婉拒了漁民向他伸出的手。
"Do you still hunt whales now?" 「你們現在還在捕鯨嗎？」
"Of course not. This boat is at least thirty years old. To be honest, it's a 'ghost ship.' More than twenty years ago, my father and his crew found it drifting on the sea, with no one on board." 「當然不了。這艘船至少有三十年了。實話說，這還是一艘『幽靈船』。二十多年前我父親他們發現它在海上漂著，上面一個人都沒有。」
The fisherman said a little more about topics that interested him, but the conversation did not last long. On the deck, Arthur stood with his legs slightly apart to adjust to the waves. The smell of fish filled his nostrils. When he was five, bad children had pushed him into a fresh seafood tank at the market. Pink, sticky fish eggs crawled up his arms like measles. A shop assistant quickly grabbed his arm and pulled him out, but the culprit had long since run away. For days afterwards, his body reeked of dead fish and shrimp, forcing him to stay home from school. Lost in memory, he felt his left eye twitch and a dizziness spread through his head. 漁民對感興趣的話題多說了點，但這種對話並沒有持續多久。在甲板上，亞瑟微微分開雙腿站著以適應著海浪的強度。鼻腔被魚腥味所侵占。五歲時，他曾被壞孩子推進市場的生鮮缸裡。粉紅的、黏糊糊的魚籽爬上他的手臂，如同麻疹。一個售貨員很快抓住他的胳膊把他拔了出來，犯人早已跑遠。之後的幾天，他渾身都散發著魚蝦死去的臭氣，因此不得不請假在家。陷入回憶，他感覺到自己的左眼正在抖動，腦中一陣眩暈。
"It's rare for someone to travel to Antarctica alone in autumn or winter." 「秋冬季節獨自來南極旅行的人還挺少見的。」
"Mm. I don't like sunny seasons." 「嗯，我不喜歡陽光充足的季節。」
"Have you found a place to stay?" 「您找好住處了嗎？」
"A small inn. But the owner doesn't live there. Someone comes every two months to deliver supplies. I don't really care what I eat." 「一家小旅館。不過老闆不住在那兒，每兩個月會有人來送一回物資。我不太在意吃什麼。」
The fisherman fell silent again. They weighed anchor in the darkness and set off with a rumble into the polar night. After crossing the Scott Seamounts,⑥ they kept accelerating. Killer whales would begin their hunting within hours. Animals, unlike humans, can always judge time and direction in any situation, clearly knowing what they need to do. One breeding season, a blue whale strayed into these waters and was attacked by more than seventy killer whales. After hours of brutal fighting, the fishermen witnessed nature's most savage scene. The entire sea turned blood-red, a layer of blubber floating on the surface. The fishing boat cut through the waves like an elephant's foot stepping on newborn pink mice. Throughout it all, people kept vomiting. A resident with a camera recorded the scene. That photograph now hangs in countless taverns along the coast. A fisherman named it "Primitive Beauty." 漁民恢復了沉默，他們在一片黑暗收起船錨，極夜中隆隆啟航。跨過斯科特海嶺⑥後他們便一直加速，虎鯨會在幾小時內開始游獵。動物不似人，牠們在任何狀況下都能判斷好時間與方位，清楚地知道自己該做的事。某一年繁殖季，一頭藍鯨誤入了這片海域，遭到了七十多頭虎鯨的圍攻。幾小時的苦戰後，漁民見到了自然界最殘忍的景象。整片海水都被染成血紅，海面上漂浮著一層鯨脂。漁船在其中破風斬浪，如同象腿踩上剛出生的粉色小老鼠。這期間有人一直有人嘔吐。隨身帶著相機的居民記錄下了這一幕。這張照片現在懸掛在海岸邊的無數酒館裡。一個漁民給它起了名字，《原始的美麗》。
He moistened his parched throat. Arthur crawled into the cabin and wrapped himself in the damp bedding. Whether at sea or in the place he was about to go, any carelessness that caused hypothermia could kill him. The fishermen wore even thicker clothing, leaving only their eyes exposed. Footsteps sounded above Arthur's head. There were still a few hours to go, but for now, he could not sleep. He lit an oil lamp and freed his diary from the suitcase. The further they travelled, the colder the air became; the ink in his pen was nearly frozen. He wanted to breathe, to sleep easily, to imitate a young, bold sailor writing a ship's log—but he had nothing to say. He closed his eyes and let the hum of the ship fill his ears, feeling the tiny vibrations of his eardrums, like insect wings. Hours of noise—this might be the last mechanical sound he would ever hear. Then he opened his eyes again, shaking his pen up and down. A few drops of ink splattered on the back of his hand, dripping onto the date he had just written. He frowned, tore out that page, and started a new line. 給乾渴的喉嚨補了點水。亞瑟鑽進船艙，裹緊被水汽浸透的被褥。無論是在海上還是他接下來要去的地方，任何粗心大意造成的失溫都會要了他的命。漁民穿得更厚，只露出眼睛。亞瑟的頭頂傳來腳步聲，還有幾個小時，但他暫時無法入睡。點上油燈，將日記本從行李箱中解放出來。愈向遠方空氣就愈冷，鋼筆裡的墨水快要凍上了。他想呼吸，想輕鬆地睡去，想模仿年輕大膽的水手寫一篇航海日誌，卻無話可說。閉上眼睛，他將輪船行進的嗡嗡聲統統灌進耳朵，感受鼓膜的微小振動，就像蟲翅。幾小時的嘈雜，這可能是他最後聽到的機械聲。接著，他又睜開眼，上下搖晃著鋼筆。幾滴墨水在他的手背上濺落，順著指尖滴在他剛剛寫好的日期上。他皺起眉頭，撕下那一頁，重啟了一行。
"8 April 2002." 「2002年4月8日。」
"Everything is normal." 「一切正常。」
"I cannot stand the cold." 「我對寒冷無法忍受。」
Through the porthole, something leaped out of the sea. More drift ice floated by; the hull made a sound of collision. The fisherman shouted to reassure him that it was just small ice. Sometimes the propeller would make a loud noise—perhaps some creature had been crushed inside it. The ship was unstoppable in the darkness. He blew out the oil lamp and closed his eyes, imagining the mysterious, uninhabited land of his dreams. Antarctica, the Antarctic, the Antarctic continent—like a white tooth knocked from the warm blue body of the Earth. Ice smooth as enamel. Ice and ice shelves. Long nights long enough to burn and melt a person away. There, he would see the aurora. And then, in those colourful, chaotic, electroshock-like particle collisions, under those light bands like nooses, he would strip himself naked and walk barefoot into deeper places. His spirit would be contaminated, corrupted, and then become part of nature. That would be the reshaping of his own life. 「我對寒冷無法忍受。」

With both feet on the ice, Arthur struggled to raise his left hand and wave to the fisherman, his right hand gripping a walking stick tightly. There was no strong wind today, but it was still hard to keep his balance. The moment he entrusted his entire weight to the stick, he had the illusion that he had grown old. Now he walked alone in the darkness, as if adrift in a void. The inn was far from the coast; a car was waiting not far away. When he had contacted the innkeeper, he was told that the car was driven by another guest. This guest had grown up here, at least twenty years, almost a native. In later years, the mysterious guest had left—walked far away—but his footsteps had eventually returned here, like a satellite unable to escape gravity. In the cold wind, Arthur walked slowly, his hands gripping his suitcase tightly. Within a few steps, his boot soles felt something that was not an ordinary stone. He bent down to look carefully. The light changed as his shadow moved. As if witnessing an eye open, a piece of sea glass lay at his feet. He found it strange but picked it up and put it in his pocket, like finding a talisman in a foreign land. He walked a few more steps, coughing. This place was so cold he could not believe anyone else was here, but the car was there, the smell of diesel filling his nostrils. He watched the engine's heat condense into a mist in the air, then fall like feathers. The engine roared like thunder in the silence. A tracked vehicle—and the person inside was smoking at the same time; the window was coated with a sugar-crust of ice. He walked over. The other did not notice him, so he knocked on the window. The man sat up from a reclining position, stubbed out his cigarette, and the sound of the fan and the car door unlocking came simultaneously. Arthur opened the door and climbed inside. The car was like a narrow beetle shell. Inevitably, his shoulder touched the other's shoulder—a long-unfamiliar intimacy that made him anxious. 雙腳踏上冰面，亞瑟艱難地伸出左手，朝著漁民揮了揮，右手緊握著一根手杖。今天沒有狂風，卻依舊很難站穩。將全身的重量都交給手杖的瞬間，他產生了自己已年華老去的錯覺。現在他在黑暗中踽踽獨行，彷彿游弋於虛空。旅店遠離海岸，一輛車正在不遠處等待著。在聯繫旅店老闆時，他告訴亞瑟這輛車由另一位入住者駕駛。這位住客生長在這裡，至少二十多年，幾乎算是半個原住民。之後的幾年，神秘住客曾離開過——走到遠方去，但腳步又最終回到這裡，如同無法脫離引力的一顆衛星。寒風中亞瑟慢慢走著，雙手緊握行李箱。他的靴底在寥寥幾步中感受到某個不似普通石子的存在，於是彎下腰去仔細查看，光照因為他影子的移動而發生變化。彷彿目睹一隻眼睛睜開，他的腳邊躺著一塊海玻璃。他覺得奇怪，卻將它撿起放進口袋，像在異國他鄉找到了自己的護身符。他又走了幾步，咳嗽著。這地方冷到他無法相信有其他人存在，但那輛車就在這裡，柴油燃燒的味道充盈鼻腔。他目睹引擎冒出的熱氣在空氣中凝成一片水霧，旋即如一片羽毛般下落。寂靜中發動機轟響如雷。履帶車，以及車裡那個人正同時吸煙，玻璃上結著一層糖殼般的冰。他走過去，對方沒有注意到他的存在，於是他便敲敲車窗。那個人從平躺姿勢中坐起，將煙頭按滅。排風扇和車門鎖的聲音同時響起。亞瑟打開門鑽進去。車像一隻狹窄甲蟲殼。他不可避免地與那個人肩膀貼著肩膀，久違的親密使他恐懼不安。
"Put on your seatbelt." 「繫上安全帶。」
Immersed in the darkness of the polar night, their breathing grew clearer. He turned slightly and fastened his seatbelt. His frozen body gradually warmed; warm air rushed toward his forehead, blowing his fringe aside. 置身於極夜的黑暗，呼吸聲變得更加清晰。他稍稍側過身，將安全帶扣好。凍僵的身體逐漸回溫，暖風沖向他的前額，吹起劉海。
"Sit tight." 「坐穩。」
A layer of ice had formed on the other's thick mask from his exhaled breath. His voice was light and soft, his language like some ancient murmur. In this bone-chilling place, Arthur heard the tropics in his voice. 對方戴著的厚口罩上，呼出的空氣結了一層冰。嗓音輕快柔軟。他的語言如同某種古老的低訴。身處寒冷刺骨的地方，他在他的聲音裡聽見了熱帶。
"Arthur Kirkland. The owner told me. My name is Francis Bonnefoy. Perhaps, if you like, I could be your guide." 「亞瑟·柯克蘭。老闆跟我說了。我叫弗朗西斯·波諾弗瓦。也許你願意的話，我會成為你的嚮導。」
Arthur placed his suitcase on his lap and hugged it tightly, reducing his anxiety in this way. He stared at the darkness illuminated by the headlights. Wherever they passed, the tracks roared, compacting the snow, the ice layer rustling. This tiny sound stirred some echo in his heart—like the sound of insects gnawing on grass blades. The first time he heard it as a child, he thought the house had termites. He continued to hear it as an adult, until he realised it was a sound coming from within his own body. Some insect was lodged inside him, feasting on his organs. His flesh and cells cried out as they died. He did not know what kind of insect it was, but it certainly would not turn into a butterfly. The car always moved slowly through the polar region, threading between towering, narrow ice shelves and deep blue caverns, then suddenly, in a moment of dizziness, burst into a vast white desert. He calculated the time in the darkness, turning the sea glass between his fingers. The silence was as deep as the ocean; sometimes he wondered if he was still in the cabin, so he reached out to check the time, but his gloves covered his watch. 亞瑟將行李箱放在腿上，抱緊了它，以這種方式削減不安。他目視著被車燈照亮的黑暗。他們經過的地方，履帶咆哮著把雪壓實，冰層沙沙作聲。這種細微的音調在他心中激起了某種迴響，宛如蟲啃噬草葉般的聲音。他童年第一次聽到這聲音時以為家裡鬧了白蟻。成年後也常聽見，直到他意識到這是他身體裡傳出的聲音。某種蟲子盤踞在他的身體裡，對著他的臟器大快朵頤。他的血肉和細胞叫著死去。他不知道這是什麼蟲子，但它一定變不成蝴蝶。車在極地中總是走得很慢，穿行於高大狹窄的冰架與深藍色的巨大洞窟間，又會在某個恍惚一瞬，突然躍進廣闊的白色荒漠。他在黑暗中計算著時間，拿起海玻璃，在手指間轉著玩。四周寂靜如深海，有時他懷疑自己還在船艙裡，便伸出手看看時間，手錶被手套蓋住了。
"Seven o'clock in the morning." 「早上七點鐘了。」
Francis read his thoughts and said softly, then fell silent again. The suffocating stillness made Arthur hope for the first time that he would speak again, but the other remained silent. And so the silence pierced him again, bringing a phantom pain—sharp, cutting, intense, worse than the cold. 弗朗西斯看出了他的心思，輕輕說了一句，接著又陷入沉默。令人窒息的沉寂使亞瑟第一次期盼他再說一說話，但對方噤聲，不言不語。於是，寂靜刺回到了他身上，帶來一種幻覺中的痛楚，尖銳、鋒利、強烈，勝過寒冷。
"How much longer until we arrive?" 「我們還有多久能到？」
"Three minutes." 「三分鐘。」
"How do you find your direction?" 「你怎麼辨別方向？」
"I don't know. Maybe by instinct. Sometimes I feel like I'm an animal, just trapped inside this skin. Maybe even an insect. But overall, I'm an animal." 「不知道。也許靠的是直覺。有時候，我覺得我是一種動物，只是被裝在了這副皮囊裡。也許還可能是昆蟲。但總體上，我是一隻動物。」
The sea glass spinning between his fingers fell to the floor. Arthur did not bend down to pick it up. He frowned at the other's strange expression, thinking hard, turning his head to find his eyes. The other was wearing goggles, frosted over. Throughout his life, Arthur had possessed a certain ability: through a person's eyes and first conversation, he could roughly understand their situation—how they walked, whom they had loved and left, what their deepest pain was, whether they liked cats or dogs. But with this person before him, he could discover nothing. 正在他手指間轉動的海玻璃掉在地板上，亞瑟沒有彎腰去撿。對於對方的奇怪表達，他皺起眉頭努力思考，偏過頭去找他的眼睛。對方戴著護目鏡，上面結著霜。在亞瑟過往的人生裡，他總有一種能力，透過眼睛和初次交談，他便能知曉對方的大概情況。他們走路時的樣子，和誰歡愛又離別，最深的痛楚是什麼，喜歡貓還是狗……但是對於面前這個人，他卻無可探知。
"Then what kind of animal are you?" 「那你是什麼動物？」
"A caiman?" 「凱門鱷？」
"But those are jungle animals. We're in Antarctica." 「那是雨林中的動物，我們可是身在南極。」
"Exactly. So I'm depressed every day, missing my river, my piranhas, and the towering trees." 「是啊。所以我每天都很憂鬱，想念著我的河流，食人魚和巨大參天的樹林。」
After Arthur let out a soft laugh, he laughed too. They reached their destination after some inconsequential conversation. Francis took his suitcase and jumped lightly out of the car. In this extreme cold, houses were built like huge metal capsules to resist the wind. The heating was on full blast; they shed their clothes one by one. The walls were so thick they felt stifling, the windows as narrow as fingernails. He imagined the outside was space, and they were the only surviving interstellar travellers, just waking from hibernation, slowly adjusting to their bodies. Francis stood with his back to him, removing his hat, goggles, and mask one by one. His golden hair was smooth and soft, gleaming like the sun, almost unreal. 在他發出輕笑後亞瑟也跟著笑了，他們在一些無關緊要的對話後到達了目的地。弗朗西斯接過他手中的行李箱，輕巧地跳下車。在極端寒冷的地方，房子為了抵抗風力建得像巨大的金屬膠囊。暖氣開得很足，他們一件一件地脫下身上的衣服。牆壁厚得使人煩悶，窗戶狹小如指甲。他幻想著外面是太空，而他們是剛從休眠中醒來，正在緩慢適應著身體的僅存星際旅行者。弗朗西斯背對著他，依次摘下帽子、護目鏡、口罩。他的金髮光滑柔順，發出的光澤宛若太陽，幾乎不真實。
"Forgive my earlier coldness. I'm not someone who introduces himself well in cramped, cold spaces." 「請原諒我剛剛的冷淡。我不是一個在擁擠寒冷的空間裡介紹自己的人。」
Francis turned around and smiled at him. His face was soft, lean, elegant. His cheeks were hollow, his thin lips pressed together. He reminded Arthur of a sculpture accidentally discovered in ancient ruins—wordless, conveying history and stories through its unchanging expression and every subtle detail of its skin. He noticed his rare violet eyes, deeply set in their sockets. Those eyes trembled, revealing a hint of ecstatic joy, like a spider slowly crawling toward a moth. 弗朗西斯轉過身來，對他露出一個微笑。他一張臉柔和、瘦削、優雅。兩頰深陷下去，薄唇抿著。他讓他想起古遺跡中偶然發現的雕塑，無言無語，僅僅靠千年不變的表情和每個微妙的皮膚細節傳達著歷史和故事。他注意到他有一雙不多見的紫羅蘭色眼睛，深嵌在眼眶裡。這雙眼睛在顫抖，同時流露出一絲狂喜的笑意，就像蜘蛛緩緩爬向飛蛾。

The ginger-yellow doormat at the entrance to the room came from the hands of an Ika⑦ woman. When Francis first saw her, they were both experiencing the polar night together. She knelt by the roadside in prayer, her forehead touching the ground like a bird with a broken neck. Few people passed by her; the place she had chosen had never been a market or a crossroads. Years ago, the last of the Ika people had been driven from their homeland. Dragging their few possessions—children, rare herbs, snakeskins, and caiman teeth—they began their journey south. Along the way, countless belongings were abandoned—teeth, snakeskins, the pride of being children of nature. They kept walking until their feet carried them from the tropics into the Antarctic Circle, where they settled in a place where even nature could not accept them. At that moment, he stood quietly watching the woman. Her kneeling posture was twisted and stiff. He guessed it was because of what she wore—a long, dark green wool coat. He gazed for a long time at that layer of plant colour set against the desolate whiteness. It was as if she had wrapped herself in the rainforest, her homeland on her shoulders. No matter how many times she faced the polar night, the ever-present stars and moonlight still puzzled and frightened her. Every time the polar night descended, it felt as if tomorrow would never come. In the darkness, her yellowed eyes were full of pain and longing. She knew nothing of night or cold. Behind her stood a portable wooden box stuffed with handmade linen tapestries. "Viracocha." He happened to understand some South American indigenous languages—the Ika sun god, the creator of the universe. In the darkness that was devouring everything, the sky split open with a mouth full of gleaming sharp teeth—the Milky Way. On this dim, lightless day, the sun god was being sold for three hundred pesos each. 房間門口鋪著的薑黃色地墊來自一個伊卡人⑦手中。弗朗西斯看到她時他們正一起經歷一場極夜，而她跪在路邊禱告，額頭貼著地面，宛如一隻折頸的鳥。她的身邊鮮有人經過，她所選的地方從未成為過集市或樞紐。幾年前，最後一批伊卡人被逐出家園地。他們拖著僅有的遺產——孩子、稀有草藥、蛇皮和凱門鱷的牙齒，向南開啟流浪之旅。一路上無數行李被丟下——牙齒、蛇皮，身為自然子民的自尊。他們不停走著，直到雙足從熱帶踏入南極圈，之後在這個連自然也無法接納他們的地方安頓。在那一刻，他靜靜看著那個婦女，她跪拜的姿勢扭曲僵硬。他猜想是由於她所穿的衣服——一件長長的、深綠色的羊毛大衣。他長久地凝視那層置於荒涼白色間的植物顏色，彷彿她用雨林裹緊了身體，肩上扛著自己的家園。無論多少次面對極夜，長久存在的星斗和月色仍舊讓她疑惑而恐懼。每次極夜降臨，明日都彷彿不會前來。黑暗中，她發黃的眼睛寫滿痛苦和渴望。對於夜晚和嚴寒，她一無所知。在她的身後擺著一個便攜木箱，裡面塞滿了手工亞麻繡毯。「維拉科查。」他恰巧懂一些南美土著人的語言，伊卡人的太陽神，宇宙的創造者和締造者。身處正在吞噬一切的黑夜，天空裂開一道長滿閃亮尖牙的口子，是銀河。暗淡無光的這一天，太陽神在以每位300比索的價格被出售。
Francis felt unfamiliar with the experience of leaving his homeland. The starting point of his memory was the white wilderness; beyond that, only blurred, chaotic fragments remained. Those fragments hung on his nerves like wind chimes, shaking endlessly, making him dizzy. Thus, he sometimes could not tell where he was—still in his mother's womb? Or wrapped in a blanket, thrown into a wooden cabin? Even now, there were pirates in the Drake Passage.⑧ They would torture their own kind by any means, mercilessly bullying the weak, then erase all traces of their predation, as if nothing had ever happened. No one would ever admit any wrongdoing. "Primitive Beauty." Humans were sometimes more terrifying than predatory whales. He knew clearly that he had experienced all this slaughter, but he could remember none of the details. He had also left Antarctica, travelling to the other side of the earth, like a salmon swimming upstream. Back to his homeland. France—beyond its name, it gave him nothing. Upon arriving, he found that he knew nothing about them and could not understand their language. They spoke a language like a bass horn—gloomy, staccato, not like singing. One night, he listened to an argument. A man and a woman were fighting fiercely in the room next door. Then, for some reason, they began to make love. The sound of shattering glass was drowned out by moans. A wandering street musician passed by the window, playing an accordion and singing. The music sounded like a train departing into the distance. Unable to sleep, he took his camera and went out, standing beneath the Eiffel Tower for a while, trying to find something to photograph. The air was thick and foul, with a colourful oil slick floating on the puddles. Interference of light. A man in a suit with slicked-back hair walked by; two girls in yellow skirts sat on a bus stop bench, chatting; a heavily tattooed young man leaned against a phone booth, smoking. And he was like an animal staggering across an oasis into the city. He breathed heavily, suddenly thinking of the Ika woman kneeling in the polar night, her thin spine nearly swallowed by the Milky Way. But, just like at this moment, he could do nothing. 對於離開故土的感受，弗朗西斯感到陌生。記憶的起點在白色荒原，再遠一點就只餘模糊嘈雜的片段。那些片段懸掛在他的神經上，如風鈴般搖晃不止，令他腦袋眩暈。因此，他有時分不清自己的位置——是還在母親的子宮裡？還是被毯子裹緊，丟進了木頭船艙？德雷克海峽⑧直到現在都有海盜，他們會不擇手段地折磨同類，毫無憐憫地欺辱弱小，再將捕食痕跡盡數抹去，一切彷彿從未發生過。沒人會承認自己的任何錯誤。「《原始之美》。」人有時比食肉鯨更可怕。他清楚地知道自己曾經歷過這一切的屠殺，卻無法記起其中的任何細節。他也曾離開過南極，穿越至地球的另一端，如同一隻鮭魚溯游而上。回到自己的故土。法蘭西——除了名字，它什麼也沒給予他。到達後，他發現自己對他們一無所知，也無法聽懂他們的語言。他們說一種類似於低音號的語言，沉鬱頓挫，不似歌聲。某個夜晚，他聆聽了一場爭執。一男一女在他的隔壁房間大打出手。接著，不知道出於什麼原因，他們開始歡愛，玻璃碎裂聲被呻吟掩蓋。有流浪藝人拉著手風琴唱著歌從窗邊經過，樂音像是一列遠去的火車。實在難以入睡，他手持攝影機出了門，在艾菲爾鐵塔底下站了一會兒，試圖給自己找到點什麼可拍的東西。空氣渾濁，散發臭氣，積水上浮著一層五彩油膜。光的干涉反應。一個西裝革履，頭髮油亮的男人在行走，兩個黃裙少女坐在公車站上交談，一個滿臉刺青的青年靠在電話亭上抽菸。而他彷彿一隻步履蹣跚穿越綠洲，闖入城市的動物。他大口呼吸著，無端想起在極夜中跪拜的伊卡女人，她的單薄背脊快被銀河吞噬了。但是，與此時此刻一樣，他什麼也做不了。

When Arthur suddenly burst in without a word, Francis was in the darkroom developing photographs. With slender fingers, he picked up a sheet of photographic paper and dropped it into the developer, gently shaking it. Blood-red smoke rose. The darkroom was like a living, pulsing entity. The already-developed photos were clipped to a thin line with wooden clothespins, hanging in the air to dry. Blown by an unseen wind, the pale surface of the paper rippled; developer dripped down. "Revelation"—these photographs were weeping. Another corner was even messier: film pools, liquid trays, and abandoned negatives. The films were stacked into a pyramid, protected beneath a thick layer of insulation cloth, like memories yet to be born, waiting to be awakened from the shadows. This was a womb of creation; the outside world no longer existed, only the process of "becoming." 亞瑟不發一言地突然闖進門時，弗朗西斯正在暗房裡洗照片。用纖長的手指夾起相紙，將它投入顯影液輕輕搖晃。血紅色的煙霧升騰而起，暗房像一個有生命的、跳動著的實體。已經洗出的照片被木夾固定在一根細繩上，懸在空中等待被晾乾。被無形的風吹動，相紙的蒼白表面泛起漣漪，顯影液滴落。「開示」，這些照片正在流淚。另一個角落更加混亂，膠卷池、液體托盤和被遺棄的底片。膠卷壘成一座金字塔，被一層厚保溫布保護在下。就像襁褓中尚未誕生的記憶，等待著從陰影中被喚醒。這是一個創造的子宮，外部世界不復存在，只剩下「成為」的過程。

He wiped his hands on his linen apron; a faint fragrance lingered in the air. He walked toward Arthur, the dark red light flowing down his body. He was crossing an isolated creative space, as if breaking through the car's windshield with his own hands, jumping into the water after him. He stood before Arthur, his golden hair brushing Arthur's forearm. A row of photos separated them. His elbow felt Francis's touch; he unconsciously drew a little closer. That touch was like a question, like a plea. But in the end, Francis said nothing. He took Arthur's wrist, a few of his fingers pressing exactly on his radial artery. He was capturing his life, feeling his emotions through those bright, defiant green eyes. For some reason, Arthur took out the camera, wanting to capture this rare moment, trapped by the blizzard. Through the lens, their gazes met, intertwined. Sometimes he was drawn to a single part of his body—his eyes, his hair, his hands. But Francis's ears were unbearable to him. A bullet had once pierced his ear, and now its shape looked like a crescent moon. 忙於他們的小廚房，弗朗西斯抽出空隙嬉笑著與亞瑟打趣。他微笑時會瞇起眼睛，金髮如湖底的水草般晃動。不知何故，亞瑟拿出相機，想在暴風雪的囚禁中記錄下這個罕見的時刻。透過鏡頭，他們目光相融，糾纏在一起。有時他會單單被他身體的某個部分吸引，眼睛、頭髮、手。但是弗朗西斯的耳朵令他無法忍受，一顆子彈曾經刺穿了他的耳廓，現在耳朵的形狀看起來像新月。
"Sly little seal." 「狡猾的小海豹。」
Francis let out a low laugh, turned to face the camera, and stood as if for a passport photo. Then he took the camera and photographed Arthur. After dinner, they leaned over the developing tray together, waiting for the figures to suddenly appear on the photographic paper. The photograph Francis took of Arthur was in good condition, but the one Arthur took of Francis—his features had disappeared from overexposure. They looked at each other and burst out laughing. 弗朗西斯發出一聲低笑，轉過身來正對著鏡頭，就像拍證件照那樣。然後，他接過相機為亞瑟拍照。晚飯後，他們一起湊在顯影池前，等待人影在相紙上的突然出現。弗朗西斯拍的這張照片條件良好，而亞瑟為弗朗西斯拍的那張——五官因過度曝光消失不見。他們對視，接著大笑。在旅舍中的很多時間都不需要言語，只有相機的聲音、衣服的柔軟沙沙聲，還有他們心臟微弱的跳動。漫長的極夜中，他們發現自己在沙發上，足夠近，呼吸在寂靜的空氣中混合。亞瑟幾乎心懷著恐懼靠近。脫離猶豫不決，弗朗西斯會拉住他的手，將他拽到自己身邊。他的金髮垂落在他的肩膀上，但他卻從來不讓他碰。他們的嘴唇在一個柔軟的，揮之不去的吻中相遇。
In his arms, Arthur always felt the heaviness in his chest begin to lift. Like the first butterfly to feel spring and break free from its cocoon, he began to believe that life still had something to give him. The cold, desolate landscape outside seemed far away. He had always been afraid to let anyone get this close. But Francis's quiet strength and easy laughter broke through the defences he had carefully built. Now, his warm body pressed against his. So he closed his eyes and let go of his last doubt. His hand gripped Francis's tightly. The fear that had held him back for so long slowly gave way to something deeper—trust, and perhaps even love. Months passed, but the aurora did not appear. 在他懷中，亞瑟總能感覺到胸部的沉重感開始上升。彷彿第一隻感受到春天，開始破繭的蝴蝶，他開始相信生活還有東西可以給他。外面的寒冷，荒涼的風景似乎很遠。他一直害怕讓別人靠得這麼近。但是弗朗西斯沉靜的力量和輕鬆的笑聲突破了他精心構築的防線。此刻，他溫暖的身體緊貼著他的身體。於是他閉上眼睛，放下最後一絲疑慮。他的手緊緊握住弗朗西斯的手，長久以來讓他退縮的恐懼慢慢讓位於更深層次的東西——信任，甚至可能是愛。幾個月過去了，極光沒有出現。
◇
On the last night before the polar night gave way to the endless polar day, they sat side by side on the steps in front of the inn, leaning close together, surrounded by the quietly swirling cold air of Antarctica. All around was silent, except for the imperceptible movement of the ice beneath their feet. The sky was clear; the Milky Way was magnificent. The huge, gleaming fangs—just as on that day, the day Francis had given him back his life. He turned to look at him, their fingers interlaced. He closed his eyes and pressed his forehead close. This was the only sign of life on this land, the proof of his existence. Before meeting Francis, he had hardly ever felt his own life. But now, his arteries pulsed deep within his body, sending hot blood everywhere; his whole body was warm. Filled with thoughts and memories that could not be expressed, he had become a container, thoroughly loaded with stories. 極地黑夜讓位於無盡白晝之前的最後一個夜晚，他們並排坐在旅舍門前的台階上，緊緊靠在一起，周圍是南極默默旋轉的清冽空氣。四周靜謐無聲，只有冰層在他們腳下微不可察地移動。天空晴朗，銀河聲勢浩大。巨大的閃亮獠牙——一如那天，弗朗西斯將生命重新賦予他的那天。他轉頭看向他，十指交扣，閉上眼睛將額頭貼近，這是此時這片土地上唯一的生命跡象，也是他存在的證明。在遇到弗朗西斯前，他幾乎從未感受過自己的生命。但此刻，他的動脈在身體深處跳動，將滾燙的血液送去各處，渾身都熱熱的。身體充滿了無法表達的思緒和記憶，他徹底成為了一件裝著記憶的容器。
"What will you do if the aurora doesn't come tonight?" 「如果今晚還沒有極光，你要怎麼辦？」
"Wait. I'll keep waiting. Eventually it will come." 「再等下去，總會等到的。」
Francis's voice was nearly a whisper, almost a prayer. His hoarse voice broke the fragile spell of the night like an omen. He smiled, his gaze fixed on the horizon. 弗朗西斯的聲音近乎耳語，又似祈求。他的沙啞嗓音打破黑夜脆弱的魔咒，像一個先行的預兆。他微笑著，目光凝視著地平線。
"Are you still fixated on Vinson?" 「你還對文森峰感到執著嗎？」
"That wasn't Vinson. I know that now. But it nearly killed me. Now I see clearly." 「那不是文森峰。我現在知道了。但僅僅那樣就快要了我的命了。我現在認清楚了。」
The thought of returning to the world beyond Antarctica was puzzling. Now he wanted to stay here forever, with Francis. His fingers remained intertwined with Francis's, like the wind twisting the snow. For a long time, they sat quietly, holding each other for warmth, pressing hot kisses on each other's eyes and ears. Behind them, the iron wall of the inn reflected a faint light. It was here, on this frozen continent at the bottom of the world, that they had found each other. His life was here. The beginning or end of life was never a place, but a memory of a person. Now they existed in each other's memories, impossible for anything to erase. Time passed. Then, as if by some unspoken agreement, they both stood up, brushed the snow from their windproof suits, and walked step by step to the nearby hill. The snow crunched under their boots, like walking on old wooden boards. Sometimes he would suddenly stop on purpose, pulling Francis down onto the snow with him, sprawling out and laughing as they rolled. Snow clung to his face; his eyelashes turned white. When they reached the top of the hill, they stopped and turned to face each other. Francis grinned; his violet eyes never left him, moving with him, wildly uncontrollable. For a short while, Francis said nothing, taking Arthur's hand and pulling him close, beginning a strange, primitive dance. They were too thickly dressed, moving slowly, like two penguins. Two people swayed under the stars, almost merging into one. There was no music, no other sound—only their breathing and the rustle of their coats. Sometimes Francis would suddenly raise his hands, stretching his fingers far into the Milky Way, as if his body were evaporating. Once, he pointed to the sky again. As if summoned by him, a faint, green light appeared at his fingertip. 回到這個南極之外的世界的想法令人費解。現在他想永遠留在這裡，和弗朗西斯在一起。他的手指與弗朗西斯的手指一直交織在一起，就像寒風捲著雪。良久，他們靜靜地坐著，身體緊貼在一起取暖，在彼此的眼眶和耳廓上落下灼熱的親吻。他們身後，旅舍的鐵牆反射著微弱的光。正是在這裡，在世界底部的這片冰封大陸上，他們找到了彼此。他的生命在這裡。生命的起點或終點從來不是一個地方，而是他與某個人相關的記憶。現在他們存在於彼此的记忆，無法被任何東西抹去。時間過去了很久，接著，彷彿達成了某種不言而喻的協議，他們都站了起來，拂去防風服上的積雪，一步一步走向不遠處的小山丘。雪在靴子下發出嘎吱嘎吱的響聲，就像踩在老舊的木板上。有時，他會故意突然停下，拉著弗朗西斯倒在雪地上大字排開，嬉笑翻滾。他的臉上沾了雪，睫毛變成白色。到達山頂後，他們停了下來，轉過身面對面。弗朗西斯咧嘴一笑，紫羅蘭色的雙眼一直落在他身上，隨他移動，野性得不受控制。這一小段的時間裡，弗朗西斯一言不發，執起亞瑟的手將他拉近，開始一種奇怪的、原始的舞步。他們穿得太厚，動作緩慢，像兩隻企鵝。兩個人在星空下搖擺，幾乎融為一體。沒有音樂，沒有其他任何聲音，只有彼此的呼吸和外衣摩擦發出的沙沙聲。有時，弗朗西斯會突然舉起雙手，將指尖長長地延伸進銀河裡去，彷彿他的身體正在蒸發。某次，他依舊向著天空一指，如同受到他的召喚，一抹微弱的、綠色的光出現在他的指尖。
"Arthur!" 「亞瑟！」
"Francis!" 「弗朗西斯！」
They called each other's names at almost the same time, their heads together, watching the barely perceptible ripples of light in the night sky. For only a few seconds, irregular pale green and purple tore a gash in the sky. An otherworldly light poured down, so vivid, so unnatural, twisting constantly, like the great arteries of some creature. 他們幾乎同時叫出對方的名字，腦袋挨在一起，注視著在夜空中幾乎難以察覺的光的漣漪。只有幾秒鐘，不規則的淺綠色與紫色將天空撕開一道口子。一種超凡脫俗的光芒傾瀉而下，它是如此鮮豔，如此不自然，不斷扭曲著，彷彿某種生物的巨大動脈。
"You're lucky. Purple is rare." 「你很幸運，紫色並不多見。」
The sky was almost too bright to look at. The light raced into the distance. He heard Francis's voice, saw those emotion-filled eyes turn toward his face. The colour of his eyes—now the colour of the aurora, churning in the night sky, like butterflies flying into the depths. It was a little unsettling. The aurora was like a hand, making Arthur's skin prickle with unease, his heart pound. The colours of the aurora, the colours of his eyes, twisted and coiled like serpents, transforming into shapes both beautiful and terrifying. Violet. A vast, ancient feeling struck him. This sight existed only in dreams, or in legends. 天空幾乎亮得無法直視。光線叫囂著向遠方奔去。他聽見弗朗西斯的聲音，看著他那雙飽含情緒的眼睛轉過來對著他的臉。他眼睛的顏色——此時正如極光的顏色，在夜空中翻騰，就像蝴蝶飛向深處。這有些令人不安，極光像一隻手，讓亞瑟的皮膚因不安而刺痛，心臟怦怦直跳。極光的顏色，他眼睛的顏色，像蛇一樣旋轉盤繞，相互轉換，形成既美麗又恐怖的形狀。紫羅蘭色。一種浩瀚而古老的感覺擊中了他。這種景象僅存在於夢中，或是傳說中。
"Francis... what is this?" 「弗朗西斯……這是什麼？」
Arthur's voice was hoarse, barely audible. He wanted to take his hand, as all the blood in his body was cooling after a boil. But Francis did not respond. Instead, he walked past Arthur. The aurora above continued to boil, growing brighter and more chaotic. A giant mouth descended to swallow them. The world was disintegrating. But in only a few minutes, the aurora vanished. The last night of the polar night passed, and the sun rose. The whole ice surface shone; their bodies were pierced by a thousand gleaming blades. At the very end of the earth, two people were undergoing their final passion. But Francis turned his back to him. The sun hung before him. He opened his arms, letting the sunlight colour his golden hair. The sun slept like an infant in the crook of his arm. Then, slowly, without warning or words, he reached up and removed the wind hood he had always worn. His golden hair fell like rain. In that moment, he turned his head and smiled at Arthur without speaking. He looked directly into those green eyes, refilled with life. He threw himself toward Arthur, then reached both hands to his own head, pulling something off. On his bare scalp, several long, jagged scars coiled together. Huge, uncovered, deep purple suture marks, like fissures in sea glass. 亞瑟的聲音沙啞，幾乎聽不見。他想拉著他的手，全身的血液正在經歷沸騰後的冷卻。但弗朗西斯沒有回應。相反，他從亞瑟身邊走過。頭頂上的極光繼續沸騰，顏色越來越鮮豔，越來越混亂，一隻巨口降下吞噬他們。世界正在解體。但是，只有幾分鐘，極光消失了，極夜的最後一晚過去，太陽升起，整個冰面都泛著光，他們的身體被千把雪亮的利刃刺穿。此時，地球的最末端，兩個人正在最後受難。但弗朗西斯背對著他，太陽懸吊在他的面前。他張開雙臂，任由陽光為他的金髮著色。太陽像臥在他臂彎中的嬰兒般沉睡。慢慢地，沒有任何預兆和言語，他伸手摘下了他一直戴著的防風帽。金髮像雨水般流瀉。這個瞬間，他回過頭，對著亞瑟無言地微笑。他直視著他那雙綠色的，重新斟滿生命力的眼睛。在直視中將自己拋向他，旋即將雙手伸向自己的頭頂，扯了什麼東西下來。在他光裸的頭皮上，幾道長長的、鋸齒狀的疤痕盤曲在一起。巨大的，無法蓋住的深紫色縫合痕跡，就像海玻璃的裂隙。
Arthur cried out, trembling uncontrollably. As he gazed at this man he loved so deeply, who had hidden this terrible secret from him, his breath caught in his throat. He wanted to say something, anything, but the words would not come. Francis's expression was calm, almost serene. It seemed he said something too, or perhaps nothing at all; his voice drifted away in the wind. Then he fell, collapsing onto the ice. 亞瑟驚呼起來，顫抖不已。當他凝視著這個他深愛的男人，這個對他隱瞞了這個可怕秘密的男人時，他的呼吸卡在了喉嚨裡。他想說些什麼，什麼都想說，但話到嘴邊卻說不出來。弗朗西斯的表情平靜得近乎安詳。他好像也說了什麼，又好像什麼都沒說，聲音隨風飄蕩。接著，他倒下了，倒在冰面上。
◇
During the long polar day, Arthur stood tall, his movements slow and mechanical. He knew what he had to do. He wore a pair of gloves. The polar region had now entered its warm season, but it was still very cold. One room still held Francis's belongings: his sky-blue sweater, his cameras, and the hat and wig that had long hidden his secret. Now, he carefully held the small metal urn containing Francis's ashes, slowly and methodically burying it in the snow, his fingers already numb from the cold. When the task was done, he stood there for a long time, staring at the disturbed snow. When he finally returned to the house, he sat down on a bench. Sometimes he would forget to eat, then sit for hours in the darkroom. Time was as impossible to measure during the polar day as during the polar night. Sometimes, on nights when the blizzard raged, he would hear endless knocking on the door. Then he would open it, and he would see Francis smiling and waving at him. He wanted to throw himself into his arms. But the inn's rules, printed in red by the door, reminded him. 漫長的極晝裡，亞瑟高高站著，動作緩慢而機械。他知道自己該做什麼。他戴著一副手套，極地現在已經進入暖季，但還是很冷。某一個房間仍舊保留著弗朗西斯的遺物：他天藍色的毛衣、他的相機。還有那頂長久以來一直隱藏著他的秘密的帽子和假髮。此刻，他小心翼翼地捧起裝有弗朗西斯骨灰的小金屬甕，慢慢地、有條不紊地把它埋在雪裡，手指已經凍得麻木了。任務完成後，他在那裡站了很久，凝視著那片被攪亂的雪地。當他終於回到屋裡時，在長椅上坐了下來。有時候，他會忘了吃東西，然後在暗房裡坐好幾個小時。極晝與極夜一樣，時間無法計算。有時候，暴風雪肆虐的夜晚，他會聽見門板上無止境的敲擊聲，接著打開門，他會看見弗朗西斯笑著向他招手，他希望自己投入他的懷抱。但門邊用紅字寫就的旅館守則提醒著他。
"3. Ignore any knocking on the door. Do not open it. It is only the blizzard." 「3.不要理會門板上的敲擊聲，更不要開門，那只是暴風雪。」
They had spent countless nights here together. Only nights. New dreams were strangled before they could take shape. He was alone again, standing at the edge of the endless daylight. Even the thickest clothes could not keep out the biting cold. Francis was now part of the land they had both loved, resting beneath the pure snow, six feet under. Now, he sat in the darkroom, where he had spent countless hours with Francis, developing photos of animals and landscapes that now felt so silent they haunted him. The red light above him flickered gently, casting shadows on the familiar walls. Absentmindedly, he fiddled with a roll of film—a captured moment they had never developed, a moment that never came. The light outside never changed. It was eternal, indifferent to his grief. He had always thought there would be more time, more nights with Francis under the stars, under the aurora. Now those dreams were frozen, like the images in the film, like the man who had inspired them. He could hear the faint hum of the generator, the only sound left in this desolate place. He did not know how much longer he could stay here—alone in this vast, white wilderness. Once, he had come here only to avoid being alone. His eyes scanned the photographs: seals, penguin families, the blue whale who lost her calf. Then his own photograph. He looked good, smiling, thinking about the future. A voice guided him in his ear. On a whim, he reached out and took down the photograph, looking at the back. 他們曾在這裡共度了無數個夜晚，只有夜晚。新的夢想還未成型便被扼殺。他又成了孤身一人，站在一望無際的日光邊緣，即使是最厚的衣服也被寒冷刺得透不過氣來。弗朗西斯現在是他們共同熱愛的土地的一部分，安息在純淨的雪地下，六呎之下。此刻，他坐在暗室裡，他曾和弗朗西斯一起在這裡度過了無數個小時，沖洗那些現在感覺靜得讓人魂牽夢縈的動物和風景照片。他頭頂的紅燈輕輕閃爍，在熟悉的牆壁上投下陰影。心不在焉地擺弄著一卷膠卷，那是他們從未沖洗過的，捕捉到的瞬間，卻從未實現。外面的光線從未改變。它是永恆存在的，對他的悲傷無動於衷。他一直以為會有更多的時間，會有更多的夜晚與弗朗西斯一起在星空下度過，在極光下。現在，這些夢想凝固了，就像膠片中的影像，就像激發他們夢想的那個人一樣。他能聽到發電機微弱的嗡嗡聲，這是這個荒涼的地方僅存的聲音。他不知道自己還能在這裡待多久——在這廣袤、潔白的荒原上獨自一人。曾經他來到這裡，只是為了不獨自一人。他的目光掃掠過那些照片：海豹、企鵝家庭、失去孩子的藍鯨。接著是他的照片，他笑得很好看，那時他想著未來。耳畔一個聲音引導著他，鬼使神差地，他伸出手摘下那張照片，看向背面。
"I love you more than you love yourself." 「我比你自己更加愛你。」
His vision began to blur. Perhaps the real Francis would have valued his life more than his own. Through those violet eyes, the self Francis saw in him was so beautiful. Perhaps he could go on living, because Francis loved him more than he loved himself. In the distance, he saw the wind sweeping over the ice, like his sudden love rushing toward him.
