EN / 繁

The Steadfast Prince and the Happy Tin Soldier 堅定的王子與快樂的錫兵

◇
This was a child's holiday. 這是孩子的節日。
Christmas crackers were mostly handmade, stuffed with paper crowns, hard-won chocolates, and joke slips about William IV. He was clever: he would first stand the cracker upright and give it a shake to locate the chocolate, which he could only have a few times a year. Tipping his head back, he let the chocolate melt on his tongue, savouring the rare pleasure of sweetness. After the simple Christmas dinner, the boy and his parents laughed as they opened presents beneath the plastic Christmas tree, which was draped with old rag strips. The tree had been shut away in the cramped space under the stairs for years; its leaves had turned sticky and grey. The boy received one toy a year, and he was content with that. As he was tossing the vegetable salad, his father told him there would be no gift this year. The toy factory was about to start layoffs; the father had given away the doll he usually pilfered to the factory manager's little girl. This year, all the good dolls were in short supply, so he could only take a pretty one with a glaring defect. His mother, wearing a white dress with a frayed ribbon lace—her most expensive garment—stood silently to the side. The night before, she had rummaged through the bins in Hampstead and wrapped the gift in relatively intact wrapping paper, writing her husband's name and the child's name on it. She had witnessed everything. She was not skilled at being an accomplice to the killing of joy. She didn't know what to do; she repeated awkwardly, "We have no choice." The boy ran upstairs in a huff and threw himself onto the bed. The quilt gave off a disgusting musty smell. He stood up, shook the quilt out, and flapped it about. He was afraid of slugs getting into his bed—he had been afraid of them ever since he first saw one in the summer when he was three. Fortunately, there was no shiny slime on the floor. It was cold. He walked to the window; it was shut. He pushed it open. His eyes and ears were born to seek joy. He looked in the direction they yearned for. From Hampstead came the sound of laughter. 聖誕爆竹大多是手製的，中間塞著紙帽、來之不易的巧克力和關於威廉四世的笑話紙條。他很機靈，會先把爆竹豎起來，搖一搖，以找到那塊一年吃不了幾回的巧克力。他仰著頭，把巧克力含進嘴裡，讓舌尖恣意享受甜味帶來的難得快意。簡單的聖誕晚餐結束，孩子和他的父母歡笑著，在掛滿舊衣服布條的聖誕樹下拆禮物。聖誕樹的葉子是塑膠的，在樓梯下的狹小隔間裡關了很多年，葉子發黏發灰。孩子一年可以獲得一個玩具，他對此很滿足。他正拌著蔬菜沙拉，父親卻告訴他今年無法獲得禮物了。玩具廠將開始裁員，他把每年順回來的娃娃送給了廠長家的小女孩。今年好的娃娃全都供不應求，他只能順了個好看、但缺陷異常顯著的。他的母親穿著一件白色裙子，有一處的絲帶花邊開線了，這是他最貴的一件裙子。她沉默地站在一旁。昨晚，她去漢普斯特德的垃圾桶裡翻找了一番，用相對完整的包裝紙包好了那個禮物，在上面寫上自己丈夫和孩子的名字。她目睹了這一切，她對於當一個扼殺快樂的共犯並不熟練。她不知道怎麼做，尷尬地重複著：「我們別無選擇。」孩子賭氣跑上樓，撲在床上。被子發出一股惡心的潮味，他站起身，把被子撐起來抖來抖去。他害怕蛞蝓進到他的被子裡，他自三歲夏天第一次見到這種東西後就一直怕著它。所幸，地上並沒有亮晶晶的黏液。天氣很冷，他走到窗邊，窗戶是關好的。他又把窗戶推開。他的眼睛和耳朵天生用來尋找快樂。他順著它們所嚮往的方向看去，漢普斯特德傳來陣陣笑聲。
This was a child's holiday. 這是孩子的節日。
Her parents had begun decorating the house almost as soon as summer ended. A week ago, they ordered the servants to clean the house thoroughly and clear the passage behind the back door, to welcome the fresh Christmas tree they had ordered from Norway. They did not have to do the trimming or decorating themselves. On Christmas Eve morning, they went to the department store to buy new clothes; she got five new dresses. Tonight, she wore the one trimmed with white tulle and red velvet lace for dinner. She didn't like smoked salmon, so her parents had prepared pork cutlet as an appetiser. There were three plates of roast turkey—crispy, salty chicken wrapped in a blanket of bacon, topped with fragrant spices. The vegetables inside the turkey's belly had been shipped from near the Mediterranean, where the sun shone even in winter. After dinner, the adults drank wine under the Christmas tree and sang, while she alone opened her thirty-seven presents this year. There were three more than last year, which pleased her greatly. She had too many dolls, rocking horses, and music boxes—they lacked novelty. As she opened each one, she tossed it aside. A maidservant coming to change the tea tripped over a doll, spilling tea and staining the carpet, which drew a loud scolding from her father. Seeing this, she chimed in with a few words of her own. 她的父母幾乎在夏天結束時就開始佈置屋子。一週前，他們吩咐傭人將家裡打掃乾淨，清出後門前的通道，以迎接他們從挪威訂購來的新鮮聖誕樹。他們不用承擔修剪和裝飾的工作。平安夜上午，他們去百貨商店買了新衣服，她有了五條新裙子。今天晚上，她穿著那件鑲著白紗和紅色天鵝絨花邊的那件吃了晚餐。她不愛吃煙燻鮭魚，於是父母為她準備了炸豬排作為前菜。烤火雞有三盤，鹹脆的雞肉被包裹在培根的毯子中，最頂上澆灌著散發著濃香的辛香料。火雞肚子中的蔬菜從地中海附近運來，那裡冬天也陽光明媚。飯後，大人們在聖誕樹下喝著紅酒唱起歌，她則獨自一人拆她今年收到的三十七件禮物。禮物比去年多了三件，這讓她心滿意足。這些洋娃娃、玩具木馬、八音盒，她有了太多，缺乏新意。她每拆一件，就把它們扔在遠處。換茶水的女僕走過來，被一個娃娃絆倒。茶水灑出來，把地毯弄髒了，引來她父親的大聲呵斥。她見勢幫腔了幾句。
I was her last present. I was wrapped in a crude sheet of green iridescent paper, tied with a white ribbon. She pinched the ribbon between her thumb and forefinger, gave it a hard tug, and the frail ribbon snapped at once. Her eyes blinked, stung to tears. The coloured lights on the Christmas tree made it hard for her to see my body, so she snatched me up and carried me into the hallway for a closer look. 我是她的最後一件禮物。我被包在一張簡陋的綠色偏光紙裡，上面繫著白色絲帶。她用拇指和食指捏住那根絲帶，用力一拉，脆弱的絲帶旋即斷裂。她的眼睛眨了眨，被刺激得流淚。聖誕樹上的彩燈使我看不清我的身體，於是她抓起我來到走廊仔細端詳。
What was I? I was a tin soldier with only one leg. Beyond that, I was nothing. 我是什麼？我是一個由錫製成的，只有一條腿的士兵。除此之外，我什麼也不是。
"Fran... Francis." 「弗朗…弗朗西斯。」
My name. I was a defective product—a broken body, inorganic violet eyes, and the illusory capacity for thought. My leg had been sawn off by a drowsy worker. 我的名字。我是一個有著殘缺軀體，無機的紫眼睛以及那仿若錯覺的思考能力的殘次品。我的腿被瞌睡的工人鋸掉了。
The girl who held me flushed a strange red. She carried me upstairs and placed me among the other dolls. Most of their faces had been damaged, their clothes torn, their silk-thread hair tangled. She placed me among them. Then she grew sleepy. The fire blazed, as if it were spring. Her bed was warm and soft. She fell into a dream. 拿著我的女孩表情泛起了一種奇異的通紅。她把我拿上樓，擺在一眾娃娃旁邊。它們的臉幾乎都被毀壞過，衣服被撕爛、一些絲線做的頭髮變得亂七八糟。她把他擺在它們之間。接著她開始困了，爐火熊熊燃燒，仿若春天。她的床鋪溫暖鬆軟，她進入夢鄉。
This was a child. Young yet playing the grown-up, a small body containing tremendous destructive energy. And yet a child—evil yet innocent, harsh yet tender, foolish yet adorable, making one feel neither inclined nor entitled to blame. For this was a child; the world's future was theirs. Willing or not, the future would belong to them. 這是孩子。年紀輕輕便作大人樣，小小的身體蘊含著巨大的破壞性的能量。然而這是孩子，邪惡而天真，刻薄而柔軟，愚蠢而可愛，使人既無心也無權責怪。因為這是孩子，世界的未來是他們的。無論願不願意，未來終將屬於他們。
◇
While the girl slept soundly, I too rested with my eyes closed. After a while, a rustling sound came from beside me, like a mouse gnawing at a tin can. But there could be no mice in this house; they hired pest control every two months. The noise stopped for a moment, then a chilly voice crept into my right ear. 在女孩酣睡的時間裡，我亦閉目養神。過了一會兒，我身邊傳來一陣窸窸窣窣的聲響，彷彿老鼠在啃咬著鐵皮罐子。但這幢房子裡是不可能會有老鼠的，他們每兩個月就請人來滅鼠。那陣聲音停了一陣，緊接著一個冷冰冰的聲音鑽進我的右耳。
"Hey, newcomer. This is my spot." 「喂，新來的，這是我的位置。」
I opened my eyes. Among all the girl's toys, a lavishly dressed doll stood up and stretched. He wore several rings of different sizes on his fingers, which clinked against his body as he moved. A red velvet cape was draped casually over his shoulders, fastened with a gold button, covering a dark blue trimmed shirt and white silk formal wear beneath. His little leather shoes clicked loudly on the cherrywood desktop. His hair was curled with copper wire and gilded, each strand like a stiff thorn. His name was "Arthur the Prince." At this moment, he was looking down at my missing leg with his green eyes—one a neatly cut square gemstone, the colour of deep pool water; the other like glass, as pale as a newly sprouted leaf. 我睜開眼睛。在女孩的所有玩具中，一個衣著華麗的娃娃站起身來，伸了個懶腰。他的手上戴著大小不一的幾枚戒指，隨著他的動作與他的身體碰在一起，發出叮叮咚咚的響聲。肩上隨意地搭著一件紅絲絨披風，用金絲扣子固定住，罩著底下的深藍色緄邊襯衫與白色絲綢禮服。小皮鞋踩在櫻桃木桌面上，聲音響亮。他的頭髮用銅絲捲成，又鍍了金，每一根都像一根堅硬的刺。他的名字是「Arthur the Prince」，亞瑟王子，此時他正用他的綠眼睛居高臨下地審視著我的斷腿。一隻是切割得四四方方的寶石質地，潭水似的深綠色。另一隻像是玻璃做的，淺得像剛發芽的嫩葉。
"Leave me alone." 「別來煩我。」
I glanced at him and lay still against the alarm clock, unmoving. Toys showed no age in appearance, but he was as dazzling as a newly polished copper kettle. Toys had no age, but they had minds. I guessed he was no more than ten. 我瞟了他一眼，靠著鬧鐘一動不動。玩具在外表上看不出年齡，但他像擦亮的銅壺般嶄新雪亮得晃眼。玩具沒有年齡，但有著心智，我估摸著他不會超過十歲。
"What did you say?" 「你說什麼？」
Our little Prince Arthur clapped his hands with a tinkling sound, apparently not expecting to be spoken to like this. 我們的小王子亞瑟叮叮噹噹地拍了兩下手，似乎沒有料到有人會這麼對他。
"Please mind yourself... Your Royal Highness." 「請您管好您自己…王子殿下。」
"I am commanding you to move aside, soldier... Fran... Fran... cis." 「我正在命令你過去一點，士兵…弗朗…弗朗…西斯。」
"Oh? It seems His Royal Highness, occupied with state affairs, has never had the chance to read. Even saying my name ties your tongue in knots." 「哦？看來王子殿下忙於國務，未曾有機會閱讀，唸我的名字都使您口舌打結。」
"What did you say?" 「你說什麼？」
I yawned, turned my back to Arthur, and ignored him. 我打了個哈欠，把身子轉向一邊，背對著亞瑟，不再理會他。
"It seems someone has finally disobeyed you, Your Royal Highness." 「看來終於有人不聽從你的命令了，王子殿下。」
The other toys spoke up. 其他玩具出聲道。
"Leave me alone!" 「別來煩我！」
Arthur let out a muffled grunt through his nose, folded his arms, and lay down with his back to me. His back pressed against mine, but he squirmed as if he didn't notice. 亞瑟從鼻子裡擠出一陣悶悶的哼聲，抱臂背對著我躺下。他的背貼住我的背了，卻像沒感覺到一樣動來動去。
"Arrogant one-legged chicken..." 「傲慢的獨腳雞……」
"Infantile baby prince." 「心智不成熟的嬰兒王子。」
"Are you two going to quarrel like this forever?" 「你們以後要一直這麼吵架嗎？」
"No. I will not say another word to this childish little prince." 「不會的，我不會跟這幼稚的小王子說一句話。」
The pressure on my back disappeared. After some annoying clinking, my neck was grabbed from behind. But the grip was gentle, and the pressure wasn't right. 壓在我背上的力量消失了。一陣惱人的叮叮噹噹後，我的脖子被人從後面纏住。但是力道不大，壓的位置也不對。
"Do you want to duel with me, soldier?" 「你要跟我決鬥嗎，士兵？」
"Isn't that a bit harsh for a disabled man? Be careful, or I might tear your fancy suit." 「這是不是對於殘疾人而言太嚴苛了，小心你這身禮服要被我劃破了。」
I took his arm with one hand and struck the back of his knee with the other. Arthur lost his balance and fell. His hair scratched the table, leaving a mark on the surface. 我用一隻手握著他的胳膊，另一隻手在他的膝窩猛擊。亞瑟失去平衡，摔了一跤。他的頭髮戳在桌子上，桌面上多出一道劃痕。
"You've never dueled anyone before, have you, Your Royal Highness?" 「王子殿下根本沒跟人決鬥過吧？」
I released him, used my sword as a cane, and limped toward the window. I sat down on the sill, watching the snow-covered street. 我放開他，用佩劍做拐杖，向窗邊走去。在窗框上坐下，看著落滿積雪的街道。
"I lost. I apologise." 「我輸了，我向你道歉。」
Arthur walked over and sat down not far from me. 亞瑟走過來，在距離我不遠的地方坐下。
"I accept. His Royal Highness need not open his golden mouth. That scrap metal of yours, combined with your voice, is noisy enough to melt my tin head." 「我接受。王子殿下免開金口。你那身破銅爛鐵加上你的嗓音，吵得我錫腦袋都要化了。」
"What did you say?" 「你說什麼？」
Arthur jumped to his feet, but lost his balance again and tipped toward the window. At the call of those mismatched eyes, I instinctively reached out and caught his hand. 亞瑟猛地站起身來，他卻在我面前再次失去平衡，向窗外跌去。在那雙深淺不一的眼睛的呼喚下，我下意識拉住他的手。
"If you were cast iron, you'd definitely break from that fall." 「你如果是生鐵，掉下去一定會斷。」
Arthur closed his eyes. I knew he had chosen to trust me. Then his body began to fall. I sighed, lamenting my ill fate. I wrapped my arm around him, shielding him in my embrace. A few seconds later, we landed on a soft pile of snow. Neither of us was flattened. 亞瑟閉上眼。我知道他選擇了相信我。緊接著，他的身體便開始下墜，我嘆了口氣，暗嘆自己命運多舛。用胳膊環住了他的身體，將他護在懷中。幾秒鐘後，我們摔在了鬆軟的雪堆上，誰也沒有被摔扁。
"Forgive me, after all, I am missing a leg." 「原諒我吧，畢竟我缺了條腿啊。」
◇
He always followed behind me. I couldn't say what he resembled. Perhaps a kitten's tail, or something else. That green gaze of his would vanish the moment I turned my head, always making me think of spring. Arthur and I walked on the snow. It seemed we had never walked side by side. I had thought it was his arrogance and disdain that kept the distance between us—he always stayed behind me. But it seemed there was more to it than that. 他總是跟在我身後，說不清他像什麼。可能是一根小貓的尾巴或是其他什麼東西。他那綠色目光在我回頭瞬間，便悄然流逝，總讓我想到了春天。我和亞瑟走在雪上，我們好像從未並排走過。我原以為是他的高傲和不屑造成我們間那段距離，他總跟在我身後。但似乎又不僅是如此。
"Why do you always walk behind me? You should be faster than me." 「你為什麼總是在我身後走，你應該比我走得快才對。」
"I don't want to... I don't know the way!" 「我不想……我不認路！」
His words made me chuckle. I didn't find him annoying. On the contrary, his childish clumsiness and innocence stirred some echo of justice in my heart. I began to understand why everyone treated him as a prince. No one could see his smile and not like him. He had never smiled at me, yet he made one imagine his smile through his sorrowful expression. What did his glass and gemstone eyes look like when they moved? What was he like when he rusted? What was he like when he first discovered himself? Would he be surprised or afraid? And at this moment, as I gazed at him, he was scooping up snow and pressing it against the paint he had scraped off when he fell. Behind him was an extinguished cigar. 他的話讓我忍俊不禁。我並不覺得他討人厭。相反，他那孩童般的笨拙和天真在我心裡激起了某種正義的回響。我開始理解為什麼大家將他當作王子，沒人能看到他的笑容而不喜歡他。他從未對我笑過，卻誘人透過他愀然的神色想像他的笑容。他那雙玻璃的、寶石的眼珠轉起來是什麼樣子？他生鏽時是什麼樣子？他第一次發現自己時是什麼樣子？他會驚喜還是恐懼？而此刻，我凝視他的這個瞬間，他正把雪捧起來，覆在自己掉下來時蹭掉的漆上。他身後是熄滅的雪茄。
"Where are we now?" 「我們現在在哪？」
He turned to me, his frail shoulders trembling. 他轉向我，荏弱的肩膀輕輕顫抖。
"I don't know." 「我不知道。」
He muttered, drawing closer to me, though I couldn't make out what he was saying. The moment he leaned in, the ground beneath us trembled slightly, and a clamour arose. The laughter of children was high and thin, as easy to recognise as finding a thorn on a rose stem. The sound made me frown. I put my arm around Arthur's waist and ducked into the shadow cast by a gift box. 他小聲嘀咕著靠近我，不知道在說什麼。他靠過來的瞬間，我們腳下地面輕輕顫抖，一陣嘈雜聲傳來。孩子們的笑聲又尖又細，辨認它們容易得像在玫瑰花莖上找一根刺。那聲音使我皺起眉頭，我摟住亞瑟的腰，躲進一個禮物盒撒下的陰影裡。
"Don't make a sound." 「別出聲。」
His ear was just beneath my lips. I could see the faint blush on its curve, trembling with refusal. 他的耳朵此刻就在我的嘴唇下，我會凝望那耳廓上不顯眼的紅暈，顫抖中寫滿拒絕。
"They're coming. Play dead." 「他們過來了，快裝死。」
He looked up at me, wearing a cute expression of displeasure, then lowered his head and leaned against my chest. His stiff hair pricked me lightly, a little itchy. I held him and lay down on the ground, eyes wide, praying that no one would find us. 他抬頭看了我一眼，露出一個可愛的不悅表情。又低下頭，靠在我的胸口。他硬邦邦的頭髮輕輕扎著我，有點癢。我抱著他倒在地上，睜大眼睛，祈禱沒有人發現我們。
"My mum gave me a pony this year, but it's chestnut. I told her I wanted a black one!" 「我媽今年給我送了一匹小馬，但那匹小馬是栗色的，我都說了想要黑色的！」
This was the voice of a boy who sounded about ten. 這是一個聽上去十幾歲的男孩聲音。
"Our family said we'd go to the South of France for the holidays, but the snow was too heavy. We couldn't make it." 「我們家說了要去南法過節，但是雪太大了，沒去成。」
This was a younger boy's voice, laced with both laughter and regret. 這是一個年輕一點的，同時帶著笑意與遺憾的男孩聲音。
"I... my mother gave me a doll. I really like it." 「我……我媽媽送了我一個洋娃娃，我很喜歡。」
This voice was small and low, a little timid—a girl's voice. 這個聲音又小又低。這是一個有點怯生生的，女孩的聲音。
"Francis, put me down—" 「弗朗西斯，把我放……」
"Shh. Don't make a sound. They're coming." 「噓，別出聲，他們過來了。」
Arthur fell silent at these words and looked up at me. A shadow fell into his flickering irises. Someone was approaching. I held him tighter, praying we wouldn't be discovered. The gift boxes in front of us were kicked away. I had no choice but to lie down on the ground with him in my arms, pretending to be lifeless objects. 亞瑟聞言噤聲，抬著頭看我。一片陰影落入他的跳動著的虹膜，有人過來了。我把他抱得更緊了一些，祈禱著不要被發現。我們前面的幾個禮物盒被踢走了，我別無選擇，抱著他躺在地上，假裝自己是沒有生命的死物。
"The ground is cold." 「地好冰。」
"Bear with it, little prince." 「忍著點，小王子。」
"You—" 「你……」
Before he could finish, his words fell to the ground. The box in front of us flew up and struck my temple—a hard blow. A tinkling sound echoed in my head. The cardboard box skidded across the floor a few times and disappeared into the darkness. 他的話還沒說完就掉在地上，擋在我們前面的箱子飛起來，撞到了我的太陽穴，很重的一下。我的腦子裡傳來叮叮噹噹的聲音。紙箱在地上擦了幾圈，落入黑暗之中。
"Look! Here's a tin soldier lying here. And a little prince. Still pretty new." 「瞧！這兒躺著一個錫兵。還有一個小王子。還很新呢。」
The oldest boy lifted us with a finger, dangling us before his flat, brown eyes. He exhaled a breath that smelled of various stews. 最大的孩子用手指輕輕一提，讓我們雙腳懸空，在他那雙扁扁的褐色眼睛跟前晃來晃去。他呼出一股各種燉菜混合成的氣味。
"I'll take this prince! You can have the cripple." 「這個王子我要了！這個殘廢的東西給你們。」
I moved slightly, slipping my fingers between Arthur's. His hands were small and cold; it felt like holding a bird's bones. The boy grabbed my shoulder, trying to pull us apart. I held Arthur's hand tightly. 我稍微動了一下，將手指擠進亞瑟的十指指縫間。他的手很小、又很涼，感覺握住了鳥的骨頭。那孩子抓住我的肩膀，想把我們分開。我緊緊地握住亞瑟的手。
"Hey! They won't come apart." 「嘿！分不開。」
The boy looked around, as if searching for a handy stone. 男孩環顧一圈，似乎想找一塊趁手的石頭。
"Don't bother. These two lumps of iron must have melted and fused together before being thrown out. You know how hot the fireplace gets this time of year." 「別白費勁了。這兩個鐵疙瘩肯定是化了之後又熔在一起了才被人丟掉了。你知道的，這個季節壁爐總是燒得很燙。」
"Let's send them on a voyage, then!" 「那我們讓他去航行一番吧！」
"Don't you think that's a bit too much?" 「不覺得有些過分嗎？」
The timid girl's voice was the last memory I had of land. The two boys folded a boat from a sheet of newspaper, laid us sideways inside it, and flicked it into the gutter. The boat nearly capsized. We floated downstream along the drain. The children ran along the bank, clapping their hands. The paper boat bobbed up and down. Arthur stirred in my arms and stood up. 那個怯生生的女孩的聲音成了我在陸上的最後記憶。兩個男孩用一張報紙摺了一條船，把我們橫放在裡面，接著用手指彈進水溝裡。船差點翻了，我們沿著水溝順流而下。這兩個孩子在岸上跟著跑，拍著手。紙船一上一下地簸動著，亞瑟在我懷中動了動，站了起來。
"Go to hell, you brats!" 「去你們的，臭小子！」
He walked to the bow, his limbs clinking as they knocked against his body. At some point, he had drawn my sword—a blade nearly as tall as he was—and was brandishing it like a flag at the two stupid boys. His expression was triumphant, but more than that, it was boastful. His eyes blazed—my fluttering green sun. The paper boat flowed into a long, wide sewer. Everything around us was pitch black. 他走到船頭，四肢與身體碰著，一陣叮叮噹噹的響聲。他不知什麼時候抽出我的佩劍，那柄幾乎有他一半高的佩劍正握在他的手中，像一面旗幟般，向著那兩個蠢鈍的男孩揮舞不停。他的表情像是得意，更多的是炫耀。兩隻眼睛燃燒起來，那是我振翅著的綠色太陽。這艘紙船流進一條很長很寬的下水道裡去了。四周一片漆黑。
"Here." 「還你。」
"Thank you." 「謝謝。」
He turned the blade around, offering me the hilt, then sat down beside me. The water trembled in the darkness, fighting against foul-smelling floating rubbish. Arthur gently tugged at my sleeve, as if that were enough to reassure him. There was no sound but the flow of water. I closed my eyes, imagining something that might break or invade the stillness we had won in this moment. 他將刀轉了個方向，刀柄對著我，接著在我身邊坐下。黑暗中水流在顫動，它們正在對抗臭氣熏天的漂浮垃圾。亞瑟用手輕輕抓住我的袖子，彷彿這樣便可以安心。除了水流聲什麼也聽不到，我閉上眼，想像著有什麼東西能打破或者入侵我們在此刻獲得的寂靜。
"I'm sorry for what I said before. And I'm sorry they said what they did. You're not a cripple, Francis. You... you're brave. You saved me." 「我很抱歉，之前那麼說你。也很抱歉他們那麼說你。你不是殘廢，弗朗西斯。你……很勇敢，你救了我。」
"I just didn't want to die so soon." 「我只是不想那麼快死掉。」
The waterway narrowed. The air grew thick with the scent of moss. I lay back, and the paper boat fitted me exactly. One inch more, and my head or my missing leg would have been forced over the edge. Above me, tens of thousands of fungi and insects glowed with fluorescence, like a starry sky. 水流變得狹窄了，四周的空氣被苔蘚的氣息浸潤。我向後仰面躺下，紙船正好裝下我。再多一寸，我的腦袋或是那條斷腿，就會被迫伸出去。我目光所及的頂上，成千上萬的真菌和昆蟲發著螢光，宛若星空。
"Are those stars?" 「那是星星嗎？」
"How could they be stars? They're mushrooms." 「怎麼可能是星星，那是蘑菇。」
"Francis, have you ever thought about where our lives come from?" 「弗朗西斯，你有沒有想過我們的生命從何而來。」
"I don't know. I've been a toy for as long as I can remember. It was as if the moment that worker put in my eyes, I suddenly came to life. Later, they accidentally sawed off my leg. But to be honest, I don't feel much of anything. It's a very strange feeling—like someone scratching your thigh with a piece of paper, but my leg was gone. And it won't ever come back. Who cares about a cheap tin soldier? For all I know, there could be thousands of others just like me out there. Thinking about it that way, it's almost better to be missing a leg. At least this way I'll be remembered." 「我不知道，我有記憶開始就一直是一個玩具。好像那個工人給我放上眼睛的那一刻，我就突然活了過來，後來他們不小心鋸掉了我的腿。但說實話，我感覺不到什麼，那種感覺特別奇怪，就像有人拿紙劃了一下你的大腿，但我的腿沒有了。而且以後都不會再有了。誰會在意一個廉價的錫兵呢？說不定外面還有成千上萬個長得跟我一樣的錫兵。這麼一想，沒了腿還好一些。至少我能被人記住。」
His cat-like gaze turned toward me. The bioluminescence lit the right side of his face. I reached out and pressed my hand against his illuminated cheek. He did not turn away. In the darkness, it was as if he wanted me to see him. 他那貓一般的凝視轉向我，生物發出的光照亮了他的右側臉頰。我伸出手，貼在他被照亮的臉上，他沒有把臉轉開。黑暗中，彷彿他想要讓我看到他。
"I don't remember anything either. Just my name. I have nothing. It seems we both have nothing, and we both know nothing." 「我也，一點都不記得。只有名字，我什麼也沒有。我們好像都什麼也沒有，也什麼都不知道。」
He pressed his hand to mine, rubbing gently. I could feel the steady coolness of his touch. 他又把手貼在我的手上，輕輕摩擦著。我能感覺到那雙手穩定傳來的低溫。
"But there's one thing different about you. Even though you're made of tin, your hand is warm." 「但是，你還有一點不同。你雖然是錫做的，但你的手是暖的。」
He closed his eyes, and I closed mine. A low laugh came from the darkness, followed by a final sentence. 他說完就閉上眼睛，我也跟著閉上眼睛。黑暗中傳來一聲低笑，接著跟了一句。
"Thank you for saving me." 「謝謝你救了我。」

The current grew faster. At the end, sunlight shone. A rumbling came, light as a pulse. Arthur had dozed for a while and was now waking. His eyelids fluttered like wings; his brow relaxed cheerfully, revealing a defenceless appearance. After a moment, an undercurrent struck the bottom of the boat, causing it to sway. Arthur clutched my sleeve, his eyes struggling to open. I covered his eyes with my hand, trying to block the blinding light before us. He sat up, grasped the edge of the boat, and tilted his head back. We left a long, dark trail on the water behind us. 水流越來越急，盡頭處陽光照耀。傳來一陣隆隆聲，輕如脈搏。亞瑟瞇著眼睡了一會兒，此刻正在醒來。他的睫毛如翅膀般翕動，眉頭快活地舒展開，顯現出沒有防備的樣貌。過了一會兒，一股暗流拍打船底，帶起一陣晃動。亞瑟抓著我的袖子，有點睜不開眼。我把手覆在他的眼睛上，企圖擋住那陣橫在我們眼前的強光。他坐起身，雙手扶著船沿，向後偏頭。我們划過的水面上留下一條長長的墨跡。
"Is that the sea ahead?" 「前面是海嗎？」
He looked ahead, rising before me like a sail. The closer we came to the exit, the wider the sewer grew, beginning to slope. I felt sure we had dropped several metres with the current. The light of microorganisms and fungi overhead faded; the moss grew drier. The sun was near. In a matter of seconds, we rushed out of the sewer into an open, boundless expanse of water, like insects falling into a trembling blue web. I tried to grab Arthur's hand, but the current swept us apart. The existence of taste amazed me: the water that filled my nose and throat was bitter, but not salty. That taste wandered over my tongue, cutting back and forth like a knife. My eyes stung; my ears went temporarily deaf from the pressure. But I floated. The water pressed around me, buoying me like a feather. I was hollow, and I was missing a leg. I swam back toward the place we had fallen from, searching for any trace of gold or green. Diving was harder for me. I tied stones to my ankle and plunged into the dense, net-like green algae forest. No suffocation or chaos—only blurred light and phantoms. The bottom became a corridor of endlessly reflecting mirrors, making it hard to see any detail of what I was pursuing. Amid countless buzzing sounds, the popping of bubbles, and the whispering rustle of sand grains, I heard a clear clinking. I turned and swam toward it. Arthur's legs were trapped in sand, which was swallowing him. He looked at me with pleading eyes, as if too exhausted to struggle any longer. The sand was consuming him, as it had consumed any gear, anchor, or copper plate that had ever fallen into it. I swam to him, brushed the pale green algae from his golden hair. We could not speak. One by one, our senses were extinguished; language dried up. I understood: he would not die. We were hard to kill. But we would be buried in the sand, in darkness, like cicada nymphs—only we had awakened early, only to find the ground above us paved over with concrete. We would rust, and eventually break apart. But that would take years. I untied the rope from my ankle, slipped my hands under his arms, and swam with one leg toward the surface. But this smooth progress did not last long. Something was approaching us. I strained my eyes open. The cold silver light of scales refracted into my eyes. This was a scene from my dream—a deep, dark mouth opened from a nightmare and swallowed us both whole. In that nightmare, I lost my eyes, and everyone stumbled through eternal blindness. 他看向前方，像一片帆般在我面前升起。越靠近出口，陰溝越寬，開始漸漸傾斜。我覺得我們一定隨著水流下落了好幾米。頭頂上微生物和真菌的光逐漸熄滅，苔蘚愈加乾燥，太陽在不遠處。可能幾秒的功夫，我們從陰溝衝入一片開闊無垠的水面，彷彿蟲子掉進顫抖著的藍色蛛網。我試圖抓住亞瑟的手，可是水流把我們沖散了。味覺的存在使我驚奇，灌入我鼻腔和喉嚨裡的水是苦的、但並不是鹹的。那陣味道在我的舌頭上遊走，像一把刀似的劃來劃去。我的眼睛被刺得生疼，耳朵因水壓暫時失聰。但我浮了起來。水擠在我的周圍，托起我如同托起一片羽毛。我是空心的，還缺了條腿。我向我們掉下來的地方游去，尋找著任何金色或綠色的影子。下潛對我而言更加困難，我在自己的腳踝上綁了石頭，鑽進細密如網的綠藻森林。沒有窒息和混亂、只有看不清的光線與幻影。水底成了不斷反射的鏡子走廊，使我難以看清我所追尋的每一個細節。在無數的嗡嗡聲、氣泡破裂聲，以及宛如低語的沙礫摩擦聲中，我聽到一陣清脆的敲擊。我調轉方向，朝著那陣敲擊聲游去。亞瑟的腿陷在沙子裡，沙子正在把他吞掉。他用求助似的目光看著我，似乎已經累得無法再掙扎。沙子正在吞掉他，就像吞掉曾經陷入其中的任何齒輪、船錨與銅盤。我游到他身邊，撥開纏繞在他金髮上的淺綠水藻。我們無法進行對話，感官一個接一個熄滅，語言旋即枯竭。我明白，他不會死，我們很難被殺死。但我們會被埋在沙子裡，暗無天日。如同幼蟬——只不過我們提前醒來，發現埋著我們的地面澆築上了水泥。直到我們生鏽，最後破碎。只是那需要花上好幾年。我解下腳踝上的繩子，將雙手從他腋下穿過，用單腿向著水面奮力游去。只是這種順利並未持續多久，有什麼東西朝我們靠了過來。我努力睜著眼，鱗片的銀色冷光折射進我的眼睛。這是我夢中的一個場景，這張幽深黑暗的口從一個噩夢中張開，將我們兩個囫圇吞去。在那個噩夢裡，我失去了我的眼睛，所有人全都在永恆的盲目中躑躅獨行。
We were swallowed whole by a giant fish. Then the fish surfaced, expelling all the water from its body, like a sponge being wrung out. I crouched down, groping for Arthur's body in the narrow darkness. A wound appeared on my finger—cut either by Arthur's hair or my sword. I called his name, praying for an answer. 我們被一條大魚吞進了肚子裡。接著，這條大魚浮出水面，將身體裡的所有水分排出去，像一塊海綿被擰乾。我蹲下身，在一片狹窄的黑暗中摸索著亞瑟的身體。我的手指出現了一道傷口，不知是被亞瑟的頭髮還是我的佩劍割傷的。我叫著他的名字，祈禱著一個回應。
"Wake up. We don't need sleep." 「醒醒，我們不需要睡覺。」
I turned my head. Someone punched me hard on the back. I smiled, finding his eyes in the thick, impenetrable darkness. Those eyes in the darkness were like two will-o'-the-wisps, like giant glowing moths. He took my hand gently. Beneath his cold ceramic skin came a pulse-like throbbing—his iron skeleton contracting in the cold. 我扭過頭，有人在我的背上重重打了一拳。我露出一個微笑，在濃得化不開的漆黑中找到了他的眼睛。那雙眼睛在黑暗中像兩團磷火、又似閃著光的巨大飛蛾。他輕輕拉住我的手，冰冷的陶瓷肌膚下傳來宛如脈搏的跳動，那是他身體裡的鐵骨架在遇冷收縮。
"Are we... finally going to die?" 「我們……終於要死了嗎？」
I lay back in the stinking fish stomach, my hands behind my head. Arthur lay down beside me, looking straight at me, panting like a little dog to avoid the smell inside the fish's belly. 我躺在散發著腐臭的魚胃裡，雙手枕在腦後。亞瑟也跟著我躺下來，直視著我，為了避開魚肚子裡的氣味而像小狗似的喘氣。
"But do toys even die? ...Come to think of it, to be stuck with me at the final moment—what a shame for you, my prince." 「不過玩具會死嗎？……話又說回來，最後時刻還跟我在一起，真是委屈你了，我的王子殿下。」
"I'm not—" 「我不是……」
He suddenly took my hand again, rolled up my sleeve, and guided my finger to touch something. Something set into his back—a regular pattern, like a carved mark. 他又突然拉過我的手，捲起袖子，我的手指被他握著去觸碰什麼東西。那個東西鑲在他的背上，像是刀刻的一個規則的圖案。
"What is this?" 「這是什麼？」
"A skull tattoo. I'm not a prince, Francis. I'm a fraud. This outfit belonged to another toy. Even my eyes were stolen. I used to be a pirate toy. I had one eye." 「骷髏刺青。我不是個王子，弗朗西斯，我是個騙子。我這身衣服是另外一個玩具的，連我的眼睛都是偷來的。我原來是個海盜玩具，一個瞎了一隻眼的。」
"So what? We're toys. What does it matter who we are or who we belong to?" 「那有什麼。我們是玩具，我們是誰，我們屬於誰，有什麼相關？」
"But aren't you curious? We don't know who we are, yet we have life. We don't know where we're going, yet we have a future. And we've always been like this." 「可是，你不好奇嗎？我們不知道自己是谁卻有生命，我們不知道去哪卻有未來。而且我們一直是這個樣子。」
I sat up, put my hand on his shoulder, and accidentally brushed his hair. I was now certain that the wound on my hand had come from him. 我坐起來，把手放在他的肩膀上，無意間擦過他的頭髮。我更加確信手上的傷口便來自於他。
"Then where did you originally want to go?" 「那你本來想去哪兒？」

We rose higher and higher, then heard the sound of surfacing. A few seconds later, a jolt told us we had been thrown somewhere. 我張了張嘴，想告訴他我們落入的不是海，卻沒出聲。我再說些什麼，這條魚卻突然開始東奔西撞，做出許多可怕的動作。我們不斷上升，聽到出水的聲音。幾秒後，傳來一陣晃動，我們應該是被扔在了某個地方。
"We won't be stuck in the fish's belly." 「我們不會在魚肚子裡的。」
I drew my sword, confirmed we were on solid ground, and thrust it forward. The fish did not die; it kept struggling, and the wound became jagged. Arthur took my hand and helped me push. After a moment, a lightning-like beam of light stabbed in, sliding from my blade into the slimy fish belly. I forced the gap open with my body, let Arthur squeeze out before me, and followed him out. We were in a large, deep barrel. Besides this fish, there were several others beneath our feet. 我拔出佩劍，確認我們在地面上之後向面前刺去。魚沒有死，魚還在掙扎，刀痕變得歪歪扭扭的。亞瑟握住我的手，幫著我用力。過了一會兒，一道閃電似的光芒刺了進來，從我的刀劍滑進黏糊糊的魚肚子裡。我用身體勉強支開這道裂縫，讓亞瑟從我身前擠出去，我也跟著出去。我們在一個深深的大桶裡，腳下除了這條魚，還有幾條魚。
"Hey!" 「嘿！」
We were still catching our breath when we heard a voice. We immediately lay flat on the fish's belly. A hand suddenly grabbed us and stuffed us into a pocket. Tossing like a carriage, we were carried off to who knows where. 我們還在喘著氣，聽到聲音就馬上臥倒在魚肚子上。一隻手突然抓起我們，又把我們放進口袋。我們又在馬車一般的晃動中，不知被帶向何處了。
◇
My eyes hold some visual memories: Christmas crackers stuffed with chocolate, the plastic Christmas tree draped with old rags, vegetable salad, a flat, brown, pitiful hand like a bird's claw, and a pair of drowsy eyes. We had been cleaned and set by the fireplace to warm. I recognised the man who had cut off my leg. He had found us while fishing and brought us to his son, who had received no Christmas gift this year. 我的眼睛裡存有一些視覺記憶：塞著巧克力的聖誕爆竹，掛滿舊布條的塑膠聖誕樹，蔬菜沙拉，一隻如鳥爪般扁平、棕色、可憐的手，以及一雙昏昏欲睡的眼。我們被洗乾淨了，放在壁爐上烤火。我認出那個切掉我腿的男人，他在釣魚時發現了我們，把我們帶給他今年沒有收到聖誕禮物的兒子。
"I recognise him. But that seems to be a memory from my other eye." 「我認得他。不過那好像是我另一隻眼睛的記憶。」
Arthur quietly moved closer to me, his chin touching my shoulder. The deep green gemstone of his left eye trembled slightly. A square of candle flame flickered before us. The boy returned. I recognised him as the eldest, the one who had put us in the paper boat. But he didn't recognise us—after all, we had travelled through the gutter and rolled around inside a fish's stomach. In his understanding, defective products like a tin soldier with no leg probably numbered in the thousands at the factory. Arthur didn't recognise him, and I didn't tell him. I could sense that this boy was struggling to maintain the identity he had built on lies—the identity he had claimed to the other two children, that he had a chestnut pony but wanted a black one. He came over, pinched us by the waist with his fingers, and carried us upstairs. His room was very small and shabby. The quilt smelled musty; the window was patched with newspaper. He placed us away from the cracked glass, then changed into his pyjamas, climbed into bed, and prayed before a faded portrait of Jesus. No one had taught him the words of prayer. 亞瑟悄悄往我身邊靠了靠，下巴貼著我的肩膀。他左眼的深綠色寶石微微顫晃著，一片方形燭火在我們前方亮了起來。男孩回來了，我認出他是那個把我們放進紙船裡的孩子，最大的那個孩子。只不過他沒有認出我們——畢竟我們在陰溝裡走過，又在魚肚子裡打滾了一番。在他的認知中，殘次品，比如沒有腿的錫兵，估計玩具廠裡有成千上萬個。亞瑟沒有認出他，我也沒有告訴他。我能感覺到這個男孩正在苦苦維持自己架構於謊言的身份，那個他對其他兩個孩子說的身份，他有一匹栗色小馬，但他想要黑色的。他湊過來，用手指分別掐住我們的腰，帶上樓去。他的房間很小很破，被子有一股潮乎乎的味道，窗戶用報紙貼過了。他把我們放在遠離玻璃裂縫的地方，接著換了睡衣，爬上床，對著牆上褪色的耶穌畫像禱告。沒有人教過他禱文。
"Hello, God. I want a pony. I really want a pony. A chestnut one would be fine too." 「您好，上帝。我想要一匹小馬，我真的想要一匹小馬。栗色的也可以。」
His pyjama sleeves were much longer than his arms; when he pressed his palms together, the sleeves slid down to his armpits. After his prayer, he wrapped himself tightly in the quilt and fell asleep. 他的睡衣袖子比他的手臂長很多，當他闔起掌來，就順著瘦削的手臂滑到他的腋下。禱告完畢，他裹緊被子進入夢鄉。
"I have to help him." 「我得幫幫他。」
"Don't get involved in this mess." 「不要蹚這趟渾水。」
I frowned, baffled by Arthur's thought. 我皺起眉頭，對亞瑟的想法表示不解。
"Why do you want to help him?" 「你為什麼要幫他。」
"He feels familiar." 「他讓我很熟悉。」
"That's because you just saw him. He's the one who put us in the paper boat. Even if you help him, he won't thank you." 「那是因為你剛剛見過他。就是他把我們放進紙船裡的。即使你幫他，他對你也不會表示什麼感謝。」
"But we're toys. Isn't our purpose to make children happy?" 「可我們是玩具，我們的存在就是要讓孩子開心的不是嗎？」
I sighed, planted my sword on the ground, and leaned on its hilt to support my weight. 我嘆了口氣，佩劍支在地上，我用雙手扶著劍柄，支撐著自己的體重。
"Alright. How will you help him?" 「那好，你要怎麼幫他？」
"Lend me your sword." 「把你的佩劍借我用用。」
I turned the sword around and handed him the hilt. He took it and silently slipped the tip into his own eye socket—the one set with the deep green gemstone. 我將佩劍轉了個方向，劍柄交給他。他拿過那把劍，把尖端悄無聲息地沒入自己嵌著深綠色寶石的眼眶。
"What are you doing!" 「你要幹什麼！」
"Francis, you were right. But I think I might want to return it to its owner. I've seen the owner of that eye. His head had been twisted off. But he was still alive. He was screaming silently. And the moment the eye was gouged out, I could no longer hear the screaming." 「弗朗西斯，你是對的。但我可能想把它還給什麼人。我見過眼睛的主人，他的頭被擰了下來。但他還活著，他在無聲地尖叫，接著在眼睛被挖出來的那一刻，我就聽不到那陣尖叫了。」
I sighed, tore a black ribbon from my trousers, and tied it over his empty eye socket. He handed the sword back to me and placed the gemstone in a conspicuous spot. Then he leaned against me and closed his eyes. 我嘆了口氣，撕下褲子上一塊黑色的絲帶，綁在他空蕩的眼眶上。他將佩劍交給我，又把寶石放在顯眼的位置。接著，他靠著我閉上眼。
"Now I'm a pirate again." 「現在我又變成海盜了。」
I closed my eyes too. I don't know how long passed. Bathed in sunlight, Arthur gazed at the frozen river in the distance, shielding his eyes from the harsh light. It was colder today than the previous days. The fire burned all day. After a while, the boy got up. He walked to the window, where we stood properly. 我也閉上眼。不知過了多久。在陽光籠罩下，亞瑟眺望著遠處結冰的河面，用手擋住跳進他眼睛的刺眼陽光。今天比前幾天更加冷了，火爐一整天都在燃燒。過了一會兒，男孩起來了。他走到窗前，我們好好站著。
"What's this?" 「這是什麼？」
He picked up the green eye, looked at Arthur, and tried to press the gemstone back into his eye socket with two fingers, but could not make it stay. On his final attempt, the gemstone slipped from his fingers and rolled into some dark crack. It disappeared. The boy flew into a rage. He carried us downstairs. I was placed on the mantelpiece. I watched him take Arthur and throw him into the fire. In the end, we did not get to speak. 他拿起那枚綠色眼睛，看向亞瑟。接著，他用雙指捏起他，試圖把那枚寶石重新按進他的眼眶，但怎麼也沒有辦法。在他最後一次用力時，寶石從他指頭下面溜走，不知滾進哪道黑暗的縫隙。消失不見了。男孩惱羞成怒。將我們拿下樓。我被放在壁爐上，看著他拿起亞瑟，丟進火爐裡去了。最後，我們沒有說上話。
"Your Royal Highness." 「王子殿下。」
In my sight, the flames leapt high. I made a final decision—I jumped in. He was before me, already melting. I braced my hands on his shoulders, arms outstretched. His remaining eye did not waver in the slightest. I held him tightly, pressing closer and closer. My fingers locked against his spine. His cheek was just beneath my eyes. Then, I could see nothing more. 在我的視線裡，爐火竄得猛高。我最後做了一個決定——我跳了進去。而他在我的面前，正在熔化。我把手撐在他的肩上，雙臂伸直，他僅有的那隻眼睛沒有一絲一毫的晃動。我用力地抱住他，與他越貼越緊。指尖鎖住他的脊椎。他的臉頰就在我的眼睛底下。後來，我什麼都看不見了。
◇
What am I? I am a heart, melted from tin and iron. If this heart is too pure and kind, it will be taken advantage of. Beyond that, I am nothing. 我是什麼？我是一顆由錫和鐵熔成的，一顆心。這顆心如果太過純粹善良，就會被人利用。除此之外，我什麼也不是。
① Christmas cracker: A British Christmas tradition. A cardboard tube wrapped in paper, containing a paper crown, a small gift, and a joke slip. When pulled by two people, it makes a cracking sound. ① 聖誕爆竹（Christmas cracker）：英國聖誕傳統裝飾物，內含紙帽、小禮物與笑話紙條。
② William IV (21 August 1765 – 20 June 1837), King of the United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland and King of Hanover from 1830 to 1837. The third son of George III. Born at Buckingham Palace, he joined the Royal Navy at 13 and served in the American War of Independence, later rising to Rear Admiral. Known for his straightforward manner, he was called the "Sailor King". ② 威廉四世（William IV，1765年8月21日—1837年6月20日），大不列顛及愛爾蘭聯合王國和漢諾威國王（1830年－1837年在位），喬治三世第三子。生於白金漢宮，13歲加入皇家海軍，參與北美獨立戰爭，後晉升海軍少將，因豪爽作風獲稱「水手國王」。
③ Hampstead: A district in North London. Relatively affluent. ③ 漢普斯特德（Hampstead）：倫敦北部地區。較為富裕。
④ The Flower Duet (Lakmé). Set in colonial British India, the opera tells the story of Lakmé, daughter of a Brahmin priest, and a British officer—a love destined to fail. Lakmé does not die because of "failed love"; rather, her existence cannot be accommodated by the social structures of either side. Her purity, beauty, and tenderness can only be appreciated and consumed, but never protected. In the end, Lakmé chooses suicide, becoming a being who cannot integrate into any order. The Flower Duet appears in Act I, sung by Lakmé and her servant Mallika as they row along a river. It is the most peaceful, conflict-free moment in the entire opera—an illusion of a world not yet invaded by colonial violence. The repeated phrase "Descendons ensemble" does not point toward free progress, but rather a downstream drift, an irreversible sinking. ④《花之二重唱》。《拉克美》以英屬印度殖民時期為背景，講述婆羅門祭司之女拉克美與英國軍官之間一段注定無法存續的愛情。拉克美並非因「愛情失敗」而死亡，而是因為她的存在無法被任何一方的社會結構所容納——她的純潔、美與溫柔，只能被欣賞、被消費，卻無法被保護。最終，拉克美選擇自盡，成為一個無法融入任何秩序的存在。《花之二重唱》出現在歌劇第一幕，由拉克美與侍女瑪莉卡在河上划船時演唱。這是整部歌劇中最寧靜、最無衝突的一刻——一個尚未被殖民暴力侵入的世界幻象。歌詞中反覆出現的「讓我們一起划下去（Descendons ensemble）」，並非指向自由的前行，而是順流而下、不可逆轉的下沉。
