EN / 繁

The Tree Frog 雨蛙

◇
His rainy season. 他的陰雨。
Today, changing his woollen school blazer for a silk-blend one was one of the signs that spring had truly arrived. The raindrops were as fine as the dark green horizon far away, thick with wild garlic, slanting across the misted glass. His knee-high socks were pulled up to just below his knees, the garters fastened. The knee that had been struck by the teacher's cane yesterday had turned a deep purple, like earthworms coiled beneath the skin. Arthur jumped out of bed; a gust of wind slipping through his clothes made him shiver. The closer he got to the window, the colder the room became. He stood before the window, pressing his hand against the cool glass. He loved to trace the patterns left by rainwater on the glass: this one looked like a hat, that one like a peacock's footprint. And that one... the lonely silhouette of someone. He pressed his eye closer, trying to catch another glimpse of that fleeting mark. A tree frog leaped onto his right eye through the glass, a sticky, wet slap. These emerald-green creatures were always fragile yet full of life—another sign of spring's arrival. It crawled across the glass for a while, then finally stopped near where his hand lay. It was tiny, with a vivid orange spot on its arched back, like the eye of some reptile, making it look different from other tree frogs. Tree frogs usually nest in tall trees, laying their eggs near the rainy season so the tadpoles are spared the scorching sun. Arthur straightened up and looked into the distance; the nearest tall plane tree stood twenty metres away. And this one had come all this way. He arched his back again, brought his face close to it, and tapped gently on the glass. The tree frog tilted its little head. This was the silent moment of his dreams. He lived alone in the storage room at the end of the dormitory corridor; he could not remember how long it had been. Perhaps it had not always been this way. Or perhaps it had. But he had chosen to forget. In any case, there were no laughs or taunts this morning—only the crisp patter of rain on damp earth and the fragrance of grass blades. 他的陰雨。
In the faint, buzzing prelude of dawn, he closed his eyes, stretched his arms wide to the left and right, and let his body, steeped in the dust of the old school building, dissolve into the air. The joints and blood in his body made sounds; he felt a part of himself slowly transform into something else—a note of music, a flame, a continuous drizzle. These things built his self, gradually separating from his wandering thoughts and becoming part of the world. His body travelled to various places, flying over the high walls of the boarding school, scattering everywhere. And then, then... suddenly, the connecting thread snapped. A thumping sound came from the next room; someone was banging on his wall, laughing. He turned his head toward that grey wall, plastered with dark yellow, mouldy wallpaper. Red and blue seeped from his brain—the breath of anger and melancholy. The saw-like morning bell rang. 在黎明嗡嗡作響的微弱前奏中，他閉上眼、向左右遠遠地伸開手臂，將處在舊校舍灰塵中的身體融進空氣。身體各處的關節和血液發出聲響，他覺得某一部分的自我漸漸變為其他東西——一個音符，一團火，一陣連綿的陰雨。這些東西構築成了他的自我，隨著他漫無邊際的思緒，漸漸從腦中抽離，變為世界的一部分。他的身體到了各種地方，飛過寄宿學校的高高圍牆，散落在各處。然後，然後……突然間，那根連接的弦繃斷了。隔壁房間傳來咚咚的響聲，有人嬉笑著使勁敲他的牆壁。他扭過頭，看向那堵灰色的，貼著暗黃色發霉壁紙的牆。大腦裡滲出紅色與藍色——憤怒與憂鬱的氣息。如鋸子般的起床鈴響起了。
◇
He walked through the corridor, trying to keep his chest held high. In this moment, he was a walking mirror. As he walked, he thought about what "special ingredients" might be in his breakfast. Sometimes a wriggling green caterpillar, sometimes a dozen mosquitoes or two earthworms. These things did not happen every day, but they happened often, and they were hidden deeper and deeper each time. Once, he accidentally swallowed something and still had no idea what it was. He walked through the crowd—as if listening to the sound of fish swimming through the glass of an aquarium, he could not make out what anyone was saying. He reached his place, lifted the lid of his dish, and found the usual stewed peas and cream soup before him. Everything seemed impossibly normal. He picked up his spoon and searched carefully through the soup. A hard, slippery object sank to the bottom of the bowl, and he scooped it up with his spoon. In an instant, his anxious dark green eyes twitched in pain, almost merging with the tiny thing convulsing in the scalding soup. Like two shards of broken glass. A tree frog, belly up, its mouth open. He could see its thin intestines and other organs, like a pink cloud. It was not dead yet; it was still struggling. On its back, a striking orange spot. 他穿過走廊，盡力挺著胸。這個瞬間，他是一面行走著的鏡子。他走著，邊思考今天的早飯中會有什麼「特殊調料」。有時，是一條正在蠕動著的青蟲，有時會是十幾隻蚊子或是兩條蚯蚓。這些事並不會每天發生，但會常常發生，且隱藏得越來越深。有次，他不小心吞了個什麼東西進去，直到現在也毫無頭緒。他穿過人群——彷彿隔著水族箱的玻璃聽魚游動激起的水流聲，他聽不清他們任何一個人說話。走到自己的位置，掀開餐蓋，自己面前擺著常規的燉豌豆與奶油濃湯，一切看起來都正常得不可思議。他拿起勺子，在那碗湯中細細尋找。一個硬硬的，滑溜溜的東西沉入湯盆底部，被他用勺子撈起。頃刻間，他那雙充滿憂慮的深綠色眼睛痛苦地跳動起來，與這個小小的，正在滾燙的湯水裡抽搐的東西幾乎合為一體。如同兩塊破碎的玻璃。一隻雨蛙張開嘴，肚皮向上。他看見它細細的腸子與其他臟器，像一團粉紅色的雲。它還沒死，還在掙扎，背上一個醒目的橘色斑點。
"Agh..." 「咳……」
After an irresistible dry heave, Arthur raised his head, trying to find a laughing face. But he failed. Everyone had their heads down. There were no whispers, no secret glee, no post-prank chatter. Everything was silent, as if he were the only discordant error in this harmonious scene. Thus, he could not locate the source of the malice. So he was the first to stand up, dump his breakfast, and prepare to put his tray in the return rack. 一陣不可抗拒的乾嘔之後，亞瑟抬起頭，試圖尋找某一張正在嬉笑的臉。但他失敗了，所有人都低著頭，私語和竊喜從未出現，沒有惡作劇成功後的交談，一切寂靜無聲，彷彿他即是這片祥和裡唯一錯亂的、不和諧的謬誤。這樣，他便無法定位惡意的來處了。於是，他第一個站起身，將早餐倒掉，準備將餐盤放進回收處。
"Black sheep." 「黑羊崽。」
A tray was pressed down on top of his. The other person did not even call his name, because he did not know it. Arthur took in the exaggerated smile before him, trying to recall where and why he had crossed paths with this person. But he failed again. Perhaps out of disgust for him, perhaps out of amusement, perhaps because everyone else was doing it. In any case, more and more trays piled up in his hands, nearly blocking his view. He walked through the narrow corridor like an eagle with a broken wing. To keep everything balanced, he moved like a circus performer walking on a high wire—constantly alert, always careful. At any such moment, he might fall from a single distraction, a sway of the heavy load, or the laughter of the audience, shattering into pieces. Sometimes he could not understand his own intrusive thoughts, like reading a telegram code without any clues. He did not understand himself, and no one understood him. 一個餐盤壓了上來，他甚至沒有喊他的名字，因為他並不知道他的名字。亞瑟將面前誇張的笑容收進眼睛裡，努力回憶自己是在什麼地點，為什麼與這人產生了交集。但他依舊失敗了。也許出於對他的厭惡，也許是因為有趣，也許是因為其他人也這麼做。總之，他手中的餐盤越來越多，幾乎將視線全都擋住了。他在狹窄的走廊上穿過，如同一隻翅膀骨折的鷹。為了讓手中的東西保持平衡，他像一個在高空中行走的馬戲演員，時刻清醒，小心翼翼。任何一個這樣的瞬間，他都可能因為走神，手中重物的搖擺或是觀眾的笑聲摔下去，粉身碎骨。某些時候，他讀不懂自己內心的侵入性想法，就像毫無提示地閱讀一則電報上的密碼。他不了解自己，也沒有人了解他。
I can hold on. 我能堅持。
Good. At least that's safer. 好，那總歸安全一些。
Suddenly, someone bumped into him. The iron trays scattered across the floor like a shower of silver bullets shot from a gun. The cream soup, sauces, and bone fragments with pink flesh still attached splattered onto his calves. Some of the residue was still scalding hot, and through his knee-high socks, the injured area began to redden. He lowered his head, gazing for a long time at the slowly swelling burn on his skin. Somewhere deep inside his body, a sound of something shattering echoed. 突然，他被什麼人撞了一下，手中的鐵製餐盤全部散落在地，如一把銀光閃閃的子彈、脫槍而去。擦不乾淨的奶油湯、醬汁與粘連著粉嫩血絲的骨頭碎片，在他的小腿上紛紛濺落。有些殘渣仍是滾燙的，隔著小腿襪，受傷的部分開始發紅。他低下頭，長久地凝視那塊正在漸漸鼓起的燙傷皮膚，身體深處傳來什麼東西碎裂的聲音。
"I'm sorry, are you hurt?" 「對不起，你受傷了嗎？」
A young, languid, gentle voice made him pause. He looked up. In the overcast rectangular hall, he could only make out a face slightly older than his own in the dim light. His gaze stumbled recklessly, bumping into those cornflower-blue eyes, like glass meeting glass. A cheerful smile. The other person crouched down. His wet, lusterless golden curls now hung around Arthur's neck. A droplet of water slid from the tip of his hair onto the corner of Arthur's mouth, like a heartbeat. The man was not wearing a uniform; he was soaked through, his face the only clean part. When Arthur did not answer, he took the initiative to help pick up the trays. Moved by some unknown emotion, Arthur reached out and grasped the slender fingers that were about to touch the tray. 一個年輕的、懶散又輕柔的聲音使他微微愣神。抬起頭，在陰雲籠罩下的長方形禮堂裡，他只能透過微弱的光線看見一張比他年長一些的臉孔。他的眼神莽撞地跌落了下來，磕磕碰碰地碰上那雙矢車菊般的眼睛，如同玻璃相觸。一個快活的笑容。對方蹲下身，那被雨水打濕，失去光澤的金色捲髮，此時正垂在亞瑟的脖頸周圍。從髮梢滑下的水珠滴落在他的唇角，如一次心跳。那個人沒有穿校服，渾身都濕透了，只有臉尚且乾淨。見他沒有回答，對方便自作主張地幫他撿起餐盤。被某種不知名的情緒觸動，亞瑟伸出手去，握住他將要觸碰到餐盤的纖細手指。
"Don't help me. I don't need it." 「你不要幫我，我不需要。」
In response to his quiet protest, the other smiled and ignored it. He gathered all the greasy trays into his arms, stood up, and walked toward the return area. He walked quickly but steadily. Arthur followed behind him. The students were still eating; their gazes were like thin ropes, winding around their necks. He could not remember any of their faces—they were like a permanent, blurry fog in his memory. Their faces turned into cold dust and ash, fading with the wind. But in the end, not a single person looked up, as if they were all blind to this, or simply did not care. 對於他的低聲抗議，對方在笑容中置若罔聞。將佈滿油污的餐盤全部抱進雙臂，站起身，往回收處走去。他走得很快，卻穩穩噹噹，亞瑟跟在他的身後。同學們仍在進餐，他的目光正如一條細細的繩索，朝他們的脖子纏去，繞去。他記不住他們任何人的面孔，彷彿記憶中永久存在的一團模糊的霧，他們的臉化為冰冷的塵土與灰燼，隨風消逝。但最終，沒有一個人沒有抬起頭，彷彿對這一切熟視無睹，或是毫不在意。
He withdrew his gaze, followed the stranger in small steps into the dimly lit washroom, and stood by the sink. The only clear things now were the angel sculpture above the sink and the stranger's gentle face. His fingernail pressed against the angel's sharp wing; pain came suddenly. The other man crouched down again. He looked up at Arthur, searching for an answer in his eyes. Deep in his soul, Arthur felt a strange, ineffable guidance. He had seen this guidance in his dreams before—a gentle, colourful, transparent, formless radiant human shape. Indifferent words fell from its soft voice, like talking about the unrelated affairs of any stranger. But now, he entrusted himself to this guidance and nodded gently. The stranger laughed twice, washed his hands under the running water, then crouched down again, unfastening the garter from Arthur's calf. His fingertips were wet and hot, like sunlight brushing across his skin, like a warm ash floating down. Arthur trembled almost imperceptibly and closed his eyes. He hugged himself with hands full of unease, anger, and terrifying euphoria. This was it—his emotions had imploded. 收回目光，亞瑟小步跟在陌生人身後，走入燈光昏暗的盥洗室，在洗手池邊站定。現在對他來說，唯一清晰的東西，便是水池上的天使雕塑，以及陌生人的柔和臉孔。他的指甲正按在天使尖利的翅膀上，疼痛驟然而至。對方再次蹲下身子。在他面前抬起頭，用眼神尋找一個答案。在靈魂深處，亞瑟受到某種不可言說的奇怪指引。他曾經在夢中見過這種指引，一個柔和的、五彩繽紛的、透明但沒有形體的人形光輝。漠然的單詞從祂輕柔的語音中說出來，彷彿談論著任何一個陌生人的，不相干的事。但此時，他將自己交付於這種指引，輕輕點了頭。陌生人笑了兩聲，在水流底下把手洗淨，旋即再次蹲下身，解下他小腿襪上的襪帶。他的指腹濕潤而滾燙，如輕擦過他的肌膚的陽光，又似一片悠悠飄落、仍有餘溫的爐灰。亞瑟微不可察地顫抖，閉上雙目。他用充滿不安、憤怒、以及讓人恐懼的欣快的雙手抱緊自己。是這樣的，他的情緒發生了內爆。
"The rain is cold, isn't it? Not a good day to enrol. I've just arrived in Kent.① Third year. Francis Bonnefoy. Sorry you have to see me in such a sorry state so soon." 「雨水很涼，不是嗎？這不是一個入學的好天氣。我剛來肯特郡①。三年級，弗朗西斯·波諾弗瓦。剛來就讓你看見我狼狽的樣子，真是抱歉。」
Like a persistent wolf, the stranger followed the path of the emotions flowing through Arthur's body. To reassure him, he talked about the weather. In a secular sense, there was no better way to bring two people closer. Sometimes, fate was moved in such ways. 陌生人像一隻鍥而不捨的狼，追循著情緒在他身體裡流過的路線。為了讓他安心，他與他談論天氣。世俗意義上，沒有比這更好的手段能拉進兩人之間的距離。有時，緣分便是這麼被搬運的。
"Arthur Kirkland... Thank you, but I hope we can keep our distance. If you really get to know me, you'll regret helping me." 「亞瑟·柯克蘭……謝謝你，不過我希望我們以後可以保持距離，如果你真的了解我，一定會後悔幫了我。」
After a brief silence, Francis shook his head, his smile returning, and continued to wash the fresh burn with cold water. The redness slowly faded from his calf, then travelled through his blood vessels to his face. It was so hot here. Fortunately, he was wearing his spring uniform today. They had not had time to say much before the bell for first period rang. Geography—one of his favourite subjects; his understanding of the world came from it. Greenland, Africa, and that mysterious, seemingly unknowable Mariana Trench. How deep was the sea? In that darkness, he could walk through the water, pluck the faint glow of a plankton—his only connection to the real world. He swam toward them... The geography classroom was at the far end of the washroom. Arthur heard countless footsteps racing down the corridor in that direction. He cupped some cold water in his hands and splashed it on his face. 一小會兒的沉默後，弗朗西斯搖了搖頭，恢復笑容，繼續用冷水為他清洗剛剛的燙傷。那塊紅腫慢慢在他的小腿上消失，又跟著血管飛向他的臉。這裡好熱，好在他今天穿了春季校服。他們還沒來得及說太多話，第一節課的鈴聲響起了。地理課，這是他最喜歡的課之一，他對世界的了解便來自於此。格陵蘭、非洲、還有那個神秘的、彷彿永不可知的馬里亞納海溝。海到底有多深？身處那樣的黑暗，他可以在水中行走，摘下一朵浮游生物的微光，這便是他與現實世界的唯一連接，他向它們游去……地理教室遠在盥洗室的另一頭，亞瑟聽見無數的腳步踏過走廊，朝那個方向狂奔去。他用手掬了一點冷水，朝自己的臉潑去。
"There." 「好了。」
The sensation of cold water on his injured calf vanished. Francis refastened the garter for Arthur, then stood up. The rain gradually stopped. The newborn sun broke through the clouds, flooding the washroom with golden light. Arthur noticed a drop of water in the shadow cast by his eyelashes—easy to miss if you didn't look carefully. The sun's tear. 受了傷的小腿處，冷水的觸感消失了，弗朗西斯為亞瑟重新扣上襪帶，隨後站起身。陰雨漸止，新生的太陽穿透烏雲，盥洗室內金光萬丈。亞瑟注意到他睫毛投下的陰影中有一滴水，不仔細看便會錯過。太陽的眼淚。
◇
Inevitably, Arthur was drawn to Francis, yet he could not ask anyone about his past. His secrets lay in a casket sunk to the bottom of the water, impossible for anyone to retrieve or discover. Nonetheless, he loved everything about him. He did not often run into Francis, but when they met, Francis was always alone. Most of the time, Francis wore his own shirt and shorts, loosely tucked into his belt, not his school uniform. This ease, sometimes, was love. When Francis walked toward him, he would smile and wave. He would come to him, talk about trivial things. His eyes were unforgettable; the scar from the burn still itched on his calf. Sometimes, he would come up to him, wrap his arms around him, and affectionately cup his face, as if they had known each other for a long time. He talked incessantly, laughed loudly, told him things beyond Kent. He used this to ease Arthur's loneliness. 不可避免地，亞瑟被弗朗西斯吸引，卻無法向任何人探聽他的過去。他的秘密藏在沉入水底的匣子裡，難以被任何人打撈和探知。儘管如此，他還是熱愛他的一切。他不常常碰到弗朗西斯，但他們相遇時，他總是孤身一人。很多時候，弗朗西斯穿著自己的襯衫與短褲，鬆鬆垮垮地把下擺塞進腰帶，沒有穿校服。這種輕鬆感，有時便會是愛。當弗朗西斯朝他走來時，會向他笑著招手。向他走來，與他談論一些無關緊要的事情。他的眼睛讓他難以忘記，燙傷的疤痕仍在小腿處發癢。有時，他走到他面前，用雙臂環繞住他，親暱地捧住他的臉，彷彿他們已經相識很久。他不停地說話，大笑，對他講一些肯特郡之外的事。他用這種方式削減他的孤單。
"Francis, where do you come from?" 「弗朗西斯，你從哪個地方來？」
"A very faraway place, a place with snowy mountains and soft clouds. You've never seen it, you should go see it." 「很遠的地方，一個有雪山和鬆軟的雲的地方。你一定沒見過，應該去見見。」
These conversations happened countless times, and each time his answer was different. But Arthur believed him without question. He was the first person to reach out to him, the one who leaned over to touch his wound. In a way, he had saved him. His art grades had always been poor. Otherwise, he would have painted Francis sooner. Tracing his contours in oils: the temporal bone, the occipital bone, the defined cheekbones. And then a painting that captured his soul. That painting would be a mass of black, or perhaps nothing at all. Francis, Arthur—I cannot possess you, but I do not want you to get lost. 這樣的對話，進行過無數次，每一次他的回答都各不相同。但對於他的話，他深信不疑。那是第一個朝他伸出手的人，那個傾過身體，觸碰他傷口的人。在某種程度上，他拯救了他。他的美術課成績一直不好。否則，他將更早地將弗朗西斯畫下來。用油彩循著他的輪廓。顳骨、枕骨、輪廓分明的顴骨。再畫一幅描摹他心靈的畫。這幅畫將是一團黑色，或是什麼也畫不出來。弗朗西斯，亞瑟，我無法擁有你，但我不想讓你迷失。
When they met again, Kent was still drizzly. By the tall window at the far end, two male statues leaned together, resting. Traces of corrosion from the rain wound down their surfaces like two black tear stains. In the damp air, the unreturned books piled together gave off a musty smell. A few wooden shelves made faint creaking sounds, like ancient breathing. Arthur had been locked in here for the entire night. Usually, the library was only locked during exams; by the next morning—close to dawn—the proctor would unlock the door for him. He knew these times well, because he had experienced them more than once. Perhaps a few years ago, or a dozen years ago, another equally fragile, quiet, withering child had sat here, enduring the abuse and insults of the night, the unprovoked violence of the whole universe.
