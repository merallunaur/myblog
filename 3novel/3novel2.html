Clouds were multiplying. The enormous lead-grey cloud that had been over the hill was already hidden. The sun was lost in the thick layers of cloud, impossible to tell the time by its light. Winslow's① ochre lungs, before the coming thunderstorm, were desperately expanding and contracting. With eyes closed, you could hear the breathing of the plains stretching on. The air before a storm grows strangely still; the muffled thunder of hooves sounded in the distance and fell again as if nearby. Arthur stood at the door. Two ants crawled up his bare arm, walking together around his cephalic vein. He lowered his head and waved them away. He did not like insect repellent; the smell of phenol would carry him back to his war-torn homeland. One of the ants bit him. He remembered the breeze Francis had raised when he fanned away the insects, trying to recall that faded face. He had not yet come home today. 雲正在繁殖。最開始山丘上那朵巨大的鉛灰雲朵已經被遮得看不見。太陽迷失在厚厚的雲層中，無法用光線辨明時間。溫斯洛①顏色土黃的肺葉，在即將到來的雷暴前拼命張合，閉眼即能聽見荒原綿延的呼吸。暴風雨前的空氣會變得異常安靜，如悶雷般的馬蹄聲在遠處響起，又在彷彿近在咫尺的地方落下。亞瑟站在門前，兩隻螞蟻爬上他裸露的手臂，在頭靜脈周圍一直同行著。他低下頭揮揮手。他不喜歡驅蟲膏，苯酚的氣味會把他帶回戰火紛飛的故土。一隻螞蟻咬了他一口，他回想起弗朗西斯為他扇風驅蟲時帶起的清風，盡量去追憶那張褪色的臉。他今天還沒回家。
In the distance, the railway workers covered their mouths and noses with bandanas; a string of thin, wind-dried beef jerky swayed on their cowhide belts. Arthur heard the sound of boots crossing the crossroads, the jingling of keys, the sound of men returning to their nests like a school of fish, closing their doors behind them. Usually, he could also hear English spoken in all kinds of accents—before they died of pneumonia, beatings, heavy metal poisoning, and organ damage. 遠處用鐵道工們用方巾圍住口鼻，牛皮腰帶上還晃蕩著一串細長風乾牛肉。亞瑟聽見靴子踏過十字路發出的聲響，聽見男人們鑰匙的碰撞聲，聽見他們如一群魚一般回到自己的巢窠，關上門。往常，他也能聽見各種各樣口音的英語——在他們死於肺炎，死於毆打、死於重金屬導致的中毒和器官損傷之前。
The first raindrop fell on his nose, sliding down his skin to his chin. The sky had shed a tear on his face. The cry of a neighbour's newborn baby rang out at the same time as a clap of thunder. A newborn child is an angel; at five, a little monster; at ten, a criminal hiding its tail. Arthur frowned, covering his ears to block out the noise. But a pair of hands pulled his hands down, and someone approached him. All kinds of sounds swirled in his ears, so that by the time he noticed, the person was already very close. 第一滴雨落向他的鼻尖，順著皮膚滑向下頜。天空在他臉上落下了一滴淚。鄰居家新生嬰兒的哭聲與一聲驚雷同時響起。剛出生的孩子是天使，五歲就變成小混蛋，十歲是藏起尾巴的罪犯。亞瑟皺了皺眉頭，摀住耳朵試圖隔絕噪音。但一雙手將他的手從耳旁拿下，一個人向他靠近。各種聲音在他耳中盤旋，於是等到他注意到時，那個人已經離他很近了。
"Bad weather, isn't it?" 「壞天氣，不是嗎？」
The pungent smell of saffron mixed with tobacco. Arthur looked up but did not answer. He gripped the doorframe with both hands, tensing his thin body as if this were his only way to resist the world. He recognised the man; his breath smelled of leather and mouldy turkey. The stench made him frown, but the man did not seem to mind. 刺鼻的藏紅花氣味與菸草混在一起，亞瑟抬起頭，沒應聲。他用雙手緊抓著門框。他繃緊自己單薄的身軀，彷彿這樣才與世界有了一絲對抗的力量。他認得那人，他的呼吸中有一股皮革和發霉火雞的臭味。這臭味使他皺起眉頭，而那個人卻並不在意。
"Francis... oh, not me, I mean your Francis. He ran off with his partner." 「弗朗西斯……哦，不是我，是你的那個弗朗西斯。他跟他的搭檔私奔了。」
The stranger exhaled smoke, saying the words as if they were a greeting. Arthur did not move. With joyful concern, the man gazed at the young man with the pale forehead, at the bright, moon-like yet utterly hollow green eyes beneath his knitted brows. Finding no trace of the shock or shame he had expected, the man pursed his lips and turned to leave. But a pair of hands suddenly grabbed his sleeve. 亞瑟面前，陌生男子吐著煙，如問候般說出了這句話。亞瑟沒有動。懷著快樂的關懷，男人凝視著面前這個有著蒼白額頭的男青年，凝視他擰緊的眉毛下那雙明亮如月，卻又無比空洞的綠眼睛。他沒有在他臉上找到他想看到的錯愕與羞愧，於是撇了撇嘴準備扭頭離開。一雙手卻突然抓住他的袖子。
"Is he dead? He's dead, isn't he?" 「他死了嗎？他死了，對不對？」
"He ran off." 「他私奔了。」
"He's dead." 「他死了。」
"Can't you understand? He ran off." 「你聽不懂嗎？他私奔了。」
"Is he dead? Is he dead?" 「他死了嗎？他死了嗎？」
The man was shocked by the anger brimming in his words, realising he could not swallow these questions like tequila. In the end, he tensed his arm, pushed Arthur back into his house, and closed the door. 男人被那語言中斟滿的憤怒震驚，意識到無法像喝下龍舌蘭一樣吞下這些詰問。最後，他繃緊手臂將亞瑟推回他的房子，關上門。
"Thunderstorm's coming. Good luck. Stop thinking about him!" 「雷雨要來了，祝你好運，別再想他了！」
Rolling thunder sounded again from the distant clouds. Arthur leaned against the door, sweat flowing between his cold shirt and his skin. The rain fell sparsely on the roof, its sound growing muffled, like both confession and curse. Summer had arrived in Arizona. He remembered the legend of Tlaloc.② Was it mercy or anger that shaped the face of the desert? 遠方雲層中又響起連綿的雷聲，亞瑟靠在門後，汗水從冷冰冰的襯衫間流過。雨聲稀稀拉拉地落在房頂上，聲音變得朦朧。既像傾訴、又似詛咒。亞利桑那的夏季到來。他想起特拉洛克②的傳說。塑造荒漠臉孔的，是仁慈還是憤怒？
At last, he groped his way to his feet and stood again by the door, his face turned toward the direction the man had ridden off. His and Francis's house was far from the others; you had to cross a whole stretch of bare grassland. The sound of hooves scraping over exposed roots was like a rattlesnake slithering into the distance. A bolt of lightning split the darkness before his eyes, startling him. He went back inside and closed the window as the thunder passed. It was then that he heard the horse scream. 最後，他摸索著站起來，依舊站在門邊，臉龐對著那個男人騎馬離去的方向。他和弗朗西斯的房子離其他人很遠，得跨過一整片光禿禿的草場。馬蹄刮過裸露的草根，聲音正像一條響尾蛇向遠方游去。一道閃電劈開他眼前的黑暗，嚇了他一條。他回到屋子裡，在雷聲過去時關上窗。那時，他聽到了馬的尖叫。

The bus from Winslow to Flagstaff③ started running at six in the morning, every two hours. The thunderstorm had passed hours ago, leaving only the echo of clouds in the air. Arthur opened the window again; wind and sand rushed eagerly into his eyes, forcing him to close it once more. The city woke each day with a powerful sexuality; sunlight flowed and burst between the clouds like molten iron. The little water vapour left on the earth, mingled with the moans of the neighbour woman from afar, churned in Arthur's ears. In the grey-green light, Arthur explored the wooden floor with his cane. Around dawn, he had already gone out to feed the cows. Now, sunlight bounced into the house like glass marbles. The floor wax, freshly applied before Francis left, gleamed like a woman's lips. He took a shower, then went to the wardrobe; his finger was cut by the grapevine pattern on the doorknob. Yesterday's shirt was still unironed; he had stood at the door waiting for Francis all through the thunderstorm. To light the stove, he had struck fifteen matches. Now, he still could not gauge the distance between his hand and the iron; the heat left a triangular burn on his skin. 從溫斯洛到弗拉格斯塔夫③的巴士早上六點運營開始，每兩小時一班。雷暴在幾個小時前遠去，空氣中徒留雲層的回音。亞瑟重新打開窗，風沙迫不及待地撲向他的眼睛，他不得不重新把窗戶關好。這座城市每天都與強大的性慾一起醒來，陽光如滾燙的鐵水般在雲層之間流淌迸射。大地上所剩無幾的水汽，混入遠處傳來的鄰居女人的呻吟，在亞瑟的耳中攪動著。灰綠色的光線裡，亞瑟用手杖探索著木地板。約莫天還沒亮的時候，他就出門餵了牛。現在這個時刻，陽光像玻璃球一樣跳進屋裡，弗朗西斯走之前新塗的地板蠟，如女人的嘴唇一樣閃閃發光。他洗了個澡，撲到衣櫃前，手指被門把的葡萄藤花紋劃出一道口子。昨天的襯衫還沒有熨，雷暴來臨前他一直站在門口等弗朗西斯。為了點燃爐子，他劃了十五根火柴。現在，他又掌握不好手背靠近熨斗的分寸，那溫度在他的皮膚上留下一個三角形烙印。
"Not like that." 「不是這樣。」
He refused to show vulnerability, lifting the iron with both hands and pressing it down on the shirt spread over the board. A scorched smell rose from the fabric over his left chest. He ironed the shirt, shaved, and put on cologne. Arthur fastened each button, smoothing them one by one, then fastened his garters. Now he was as neat as a new Bible. He stood by the window like a statue, his head turned toward the town, motionless. He often waited like this by the window until the wilderness sunset shone directly into his still eyes. Then a faint point of light would appear in the darkness before him, like a firefly flying into a jar. Then the point of light would be blocked again, and Francis would press down from above, resting his chin on top of Arthur's head, holding him tightly. He could feel Francis's hands, dusted with mint-scented talcum powder, cupping his face, forcing him to look up. But he never voluntarily turned his face toward his—half of his twenty-some years had been spent in darkness, but before that, he had met Francis, a boy with bright violet eyes. That boy had now grown into a man. The colour of those eyes was so thick in his memory, unshakeable. Even after he lost his sight, they still held that intense, loving gaze. That made the darkness less hard to bear. There was nothing more to say about his eyes. One morning he woke up and realised he was blind, as if he had woken in the night. He did not weep for his eyes; instead, he blamed himself for not throwing himself wholly into the embrace of the night before—he had not realised that it was the last time he would see Francis smiling in the moonlight, the last time he would see those violet eyes, blooming with the purest love. From then on, he never aimed his eyes at Francis again, with a determination that was almost cruel. He was afraid of his own dead eyes. In that purple garden where butterflies forever danced, he did not wish to bear withered fruit. 他拒絕流露出脆弱，用雙手舉起熨斗，壓向燙板上鋪開的襯衫，左胸口的布料旋即傳來焦味。把襯衫燙好，刮鬍子，噴香水。亞瑟摸著扣子，一顆一顆整理平整，最後扣上襪帶。現在他像一本新聖經般整齊了。他站在窗前的姿態像座雕塑，頭向著城鎮的方向，一動不動。他常這樣在窗前等待，直到荒野上的落日直射著他靜止的眼睛，他眼前的黑暗會出現一個微弱的光點，宛如飛入罐子的一隻螢火蟲。然後，那光點會再次被遮住，弗朗西斯會從他的上方壓下來，下巴擱在他的頭頂上，緊緊抱住他。他能感受到對方抹著薄荷味滑石粉的雙手放在他的臉上，逼迫他抬頭看他。但他從未主動轉向他的臉——他二十多年的時光有一半都在黑暗中度過，好在在那之前他遇到了弗朗西斯，一個有著明亮的紫色眼睛的男孩。現在已經長成一個男人。那雙眼睛在他的記憶裡的顏色濃得化不開。在他失去視覺後依舊保持著濃烈的、充滿愛意的注視。這樣他的黑暗便沒有那麼難熬。關於他的眼睛沒什麼可說的。某個醒來的早上，他發現自己如同在夜裡醒來一般。他沒有為自己的眼睛流淚，反而怪罪自己沒有奮不顧身地投入前一個晚上的擁抱——他沒有意識到那是他最後一次看見月光下微笑著的弗朗西斯，最後一次看到那雙如紫羅蘭般盛放著最純真愛情的眼睛。從此，他再也沒將他的眼睛對準過弗朗西斯，決絕得幾乎殘忍。他害怕自己已經死掉的眼睛。那片永遠蝴蝶翩躚的紫色花園中，他不願結出乾癟的果實。
Now a spider crawled over his fingers; he shook his hand and sent it back into the darkness. The light grew a little brighter. Today was a new day. As the church bells rang together with the neighbour woman's orgasmic scream, he set out. 現在蜘蛛爬上他的手指，他抖了抖手將它送回黑暗。光線變得亮了點，今天又是新的一天。當鐘聲與鄰居女人高潮時的尖叫一起響起時，他上路了。

The ride to Flagstaff was bumpy. Arthur pressed his cane against his stomach, trying to fight off the nausea. The last time he had travelled this road was with Francis, on their way to report to the circus for the first time. Unlike him, Francis had an incomparable pair of eyes. During the war, he was the finest, most novel flying toy. But the nation never lacked new toys. Later, they spent a week hiding in a fish-stinking ship's hold, crossing the ocean. On those nights of tossing and turning with the waves, when he reached for Francis's face, feeling the angle of his raised head, he always found him gazing at the sky. Now, the highest sky Francis could touch was the circus tent. His act was extremely dangerous, yet he loved it like an addiction. When he performed, he wore that denim jacket with the jellyfish-like streamers on the sleeves. The huge embroidered eye on its back—when he threw himself into the air—would glare angrily at the blue sky. 去弗拉格斯塔夫一路顛簸。亞瑟用手杖抵住自己的腹部，試圖對抗那一陣噁心。上次走這條路還是與弗朗西斯去第一次去馬戲團報到。與他不同，弗朗西斯有一雙無與倫比的好眼睛。戰爭進行時，他是一件最好、最新奇的飛行玩具。但國家從來不缺新的玩具。後來，他們一個星期都躲在散發著魚腥味的船艙裡，漂洋過海。那些與海浪與顛簸相伴的夜晚，當他將雙手伸向弗朗西斯的面頰，感受他昂起的角度時，總發現他在望著天空。現在，弗朗西斯能觸到最高的天空是馬戲團的頂棚。他的表演極其危險，卻上癮一般愛著這項運動。他表演時穿著那件袖子有水母般飄帶的牛仔外套，背後那隻巨大的眼睛刺繡——在他將自己拋向天空時，總在憤怒地瞪著藍天。
His love for the sky was terrifying. In their distant childhood, when their homeland still softly embraced them, the aircraft experiments on television had made him scream with excitement. Arthur had once seen him building something like it in the backyard—like a fragile, broken bird. His stumbling flights almost never succeeded; when he descended, he would swoop down from above to hug Arthur. That habit did not change for many years. When he hugged Arthur, what Arthur felt first was always the reassuring darkness above his head. He had never imagined that one day the sky would take him away. 他對天空的熱愛令人恐懼。在他們遙遠的孩提時代，故鄉還柔軟地環抱著他們時，電視中放著的飛行器實驗就讓他興奮地大叫。亞瑟曾經見過他在後院造類似的東西——如一隻脆弱的，殘缺的鳥。他跌跌撞撞的飛行幾乎從未成功過，他降下來時總從上方俯衝下來抱他。這個習慣在之後的很多年都沒有改變。當他擁抱亞瑟時，亞瑟最先感受到的總是頭頂令人安心的黑暗。他從沒想過天空有一天會帶走他。
The other passengers gasped. The bus was now passing the Meteor Crater.④ Arthur heard the click of shutters; a flash of light passed before his blind eyes, pocketing the wonder outside the window. 乘客發出驚呼，現在巴士正經過亞利桑那隕石坑④。亞瑟聽到快門聲響起，閃光燈閃過他的眼睛，將窗外的奇觀收入口袋。
"Arthur, that's a meteor crater. It looks like a hundred football fields. There's some water in the middle now, like a giant eye." 「亞瑟，那是一個隕石坑。看起來有一百個足球場那麼大。現在中間積了點水，像一隻巨大的眼睛。」
For a moment, he felt Francis's voice rise in his mind, like a gramophone that had been stuck on a speck of dust and suddenly started playing again. If Francis were still here, he would brace his hands on either side of Arthur's body, his breath landing on his shoulder like a bird. He had never felt lonely in the darkness; Francis's wisteria-like language had wrapped him in a cocoon, giving him a cradle to sleep in during the shaking, wild winds. Even though he had looked to the sky thousands of times, praying for Francis, he still felt it was Francis who had let him land. Francis's aggressive laughter, his trembling chest, his long hair falling over his cheeks as he kissed, the lingering mint oil and powdery iris scent on his skin—he could count the details about him like stars, but none of that mattered now, because he had vanished without a trace. 有一瞬間，他感到弗朗西斯的聲音從他的腦中響起。像被一粒灰塵卡住又突然開始播放的留聲機。如果弗朗西斯還在，他會將雙手撐在他的身體兩側，呼吸如一隻鳥一般跳上他的肩膀。他從未在黑暗中感到寂寞，弗朗西斯如藤花般的語言將他纏繞成繭，讓他在晃動不止的狂風中有了安睡的搖籃。即使是他曾經數千次望向天空，為弗朗西斯祈禱，他仍舊覺得是弗朗西斯讓自己降落。弗朗西斯來勢洶洶的笑聲，震動不止的胸膛，親吻時垂落在臉頰上的長髮，皮膚上殘存的薄荷油與脂粉般的鳶尾花香氣，他……他可以像數星星一樣數出無數關於他的細節，但這些現在都沒有意義了，因為他現在不知所蹤。

The circus was preparing for its first open-air performance, several times larger than before. Because of this, Francis often came home late, and his salary had not increased much. Looking back, Arthur could not even weep. The loss of sight meant his memories were made of elusive smells and sounds; only when summoned again did they have meaning. Arthur stepped into the tent. A kind animal tamer helped him to the manager. The dozens of scars on the tamer's arms opened and closed like mimosa leaves as Arthur leaned against him. 馬戲團正籌備著首場露天表演。規模是原來的數倍。弗朗西斯因此經常晚歸，薪水卻沒漲多少。回想過去，亞瑟甚至無法哭泣。缺失的視覺讓他的回憶總是由抓不住的氣味與聲音組成。只有被再次喚起，回憶才有意義。亞瑟踏入帳篷，一個好心的馴獸師把他扶到經理面前。馴獸師手臂上的數十個傷疤，在亞瑟靠著他時如含羞草般張合著。
"What animals do you handle?" 「你負責什麼動物？」
"Vultures." 「禿鷲。」
"Oh... I didn't... expect that." 「哦……我沒…預料到。」
"Yes, everyone thinks lions and tigers are the dangerous ones. Who would expect an ugly creature that only stares at corpses?" 「是，大家都以為獅子老虎才會傷人。誰能想到這種醜陋的、只盯著屍體的動物呢？」
The tamer laughed dryly; his breath was soaked in alcohol. Arthur followed him forward. The wooden floor vibrated rhythmically; in the distance came the sound of chains dragging and the low breathing of some large animal. The air here was wetter, warmer than the plains, the smells concentrated into a finite space. Hay, animal dung, old wood, and the unmistakable smell of blood, all layered together. The circus is only the most direct version. Deformity, danger, near-death—things that could not originally be borne—are organised into rhythm, lights, and applause, becoming consumable content. The audience stands at a safe distance, participating through watching. This structure is not interrupted by accidents; on the contrary, a hurried ending only adds to its mystery. Spectacle. 奇觀。
People are used as tools by nations and wars, transformed into commodities by spectators, defined as burdens in relationships. When everything is transformed into something usable, relationships themselves are rewritten. In the end, only one form remains: to be eaten, or to eat. To be watched, or to watch. 人被國家與戰爭當作工具使用，被觀者轉化為商品，在關係中被定義為負擔。當一切都被轉化為可使用的對象時，關係本身也會被改寫。最後只剩下一種形式：被吃，或者去吃。被看，或者去看。
